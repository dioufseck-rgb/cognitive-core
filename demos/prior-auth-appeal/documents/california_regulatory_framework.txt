PART I — INDEPENDENT MEDICAL REVIEW (IMR)
CHSC § 1374.30 et seq. | Effective: 1994, amended 2013, 2022

§ 1374.30 — RIGHT TO INDEPENDENT MEDICAL REVIEW
(a) Any enrollee whose request for a health care service has been denied,
modified, or delayed by a plan or its contracting provider on the basis of
medical necessity may submit an application to the Department for an
independent medical review of the decision.

(b) The right to IMR is independent of and in addition to any other
grievance process or appeal right. An enrollee need not exhaust the plan's
internal appeal process before requesting IMR, except as provided in
subdivision (c).

(c) EXCEPTION — URGENT/EXPEDITED CASES: For cases involving an imminent and
serious threat to the health of the patient, the enrollee may request
expedited IMR directly without first completing any internal appeal.

§ 1374.31 — PLAN OBLIGATIONS ON DENIAL
(a) When a plan issues a denial based on medical necessity, the plan SHALL:
    (1) Provide written notice of the denial within the timeframes specified
        in § 1367.01
    (2) Include in the denial notice: the specific criteria applied, the
        specific clinical reason for the denial, and notice of IMR rights
    (3) Provide the enrollee's treating provider with the same information
        within the same timeframe

(b) A denial notice that fails to specify the clinical criteria applied or
    the specific clinical reason for the denial is DEFECTIVE and shall be
    treated as if no denial was issued for purposes of appeal timelines.

§ 1374.32 — STANDARD OF REVIEW FOR IMR
(a) In conducting an IMR, the independent medical reviewer SHALL determine
    whether the health care service is medically necessary using the following
    standard:

    "Medically necessary" means health care services that a physician or other
    provider, exercising prudent clinical judgment, would provide to a patient
    for the purpose of preventing, evaluating, diagnosing, or treating an
    illness, injury, disease, or its symptoms, and that are:
    (1) In accordance with generally accepted standards of medical practice;
    (2) Clinically appropriate, in terms of type, frequency, extent, site,
        and duration, and considered effective for the patient's illness,
        injury, or disease;
    (3) Not primarily for the convenience of the patient, treating physician,
        or other provider; and
    (4) Not more costly than an alternative service or sequence of services
        at least as likely to produce equivalent therapeutic or diagnostic
        results.

(b) CRITICAL: The IMR standard does NOT require that the service be
    consistent with the plan's internal coverage criteria. If the service
    meets the standard in subdivision (a), the reviewer SHALL recommend
    approval regardless of whether the plan's internal criteria are more
    restrictive.

(c) The plan's internal medical necessity criteria may be used as a reference
    but SHALL NOT be the sole or determinative basis for an IMR decision.

PART II — MYELOPATHY AND URGENT CONDITIONS
DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014 | Issued: September 2022

Re: Prior Authorization Requirements for Time-Sensitive Neurological Conditions

The Department issues this guidance following review of denial patterns in
neurological cases across plan products.

II.1 — MYELOPATHY CLASSIFICATION
The Department has determined that cervical myelopathy with objective evidence
of cord compression and myelomalacia on MRI constitutes a condition with
potential for irreversible harm if untreated. Plans SHALL treat such cases as
urgent unless compelling clinical evidence supports otherwise.

II.2 — CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT EXCEPTIONS
For conditions where delay of treatment risks irreversible harm, plans SHALL
NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment as a
prerequisite to surgical authorization when:

(a) Objective imaging evidence demonstrates progressive pathology (cord signal
    change, myelomalacia, or progressive herniation on serial imaging), AND

(b) Treating physician provides documentation that conservative treatment has
    been attempted and has failed to arrest progression, AND

(c) Surgical intervention is indicated under generally accepted clinical
    guidelines for the condition.

The presence of myelomalacia on MRI is specifically identified as a finding
that may support waiver of standard conservative treatment requirements
even where a full course has not been completed, when the treating physician
documents urgency.

II.3 — PLAN CRITERIA VS. CLINICAL STANDARD
Plans shall not apply internal criteria that are more restrictive than
generally accepted standards of medical practice for conditions identified
in this guidance. Where a conflict exists between plan criteria and generally
accepted standards, the clinical standard governs.

PART III — SPECIFIC PROVISIONS: PHYSICAL THERAPY REQUIREMENT
CIC § 10169.5 | Effective: January 1, 2023

§ 10169.5 — PHYSICAL THERAPY AS PREREQUISITE
(a) A health insurer or disability insurer shall not condition approval of
    a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where:

    (1) The member's treating physician has documented in writing that
        physical therapy is contraindicated or is unlikely to benefit
        the member given the nature and severity of the condition; OR

    (2) The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the
        treating physician documents that the member reached a functional
        plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms; OR

    (3) The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological
        compromise that physical therapy cannot address.

(b) For purposes of this section, "functional plateau" means documented
    failure to achieve meaningful functional improvement after a course
    of therapy, as determined by the treating physical therapist or
    physician.

(c) A plan's internal requirement for a minimum number of weeks of physical
    therapy shall not be applied mechanically without reference to whether
    the member has achieved a functional plateau or whether the condition
    involves neurological urgency under subdivision (a)(3).

PART IV — TIMELINE REQUIREMENTS
CHSC § 1367.01 | CCR Title 28 § 1300.67.2.1

IV.1 — STANDARD PRIOR AUTHORIZATION
Plan must issue decision within:
- 5 business days of receipt of all information (standard)
- 72 hours for urgent/expedited requests

IV.2 — APPEAL DECISIONS
First-level internal appeal: decision within 30 days
Expedited appeal (urgent): decision within 72 hours
IMR determination: DMHC issues within 30 days (3 days for expedited)

IV.3 — DEEMED APPROVALS
If a plan fails to issue a decision within required timeframes, the
request is DEEMED APPROVED for the period of the delay.

PART V — INDEPENDENT MEDICAL REVIEW OUTCOMES (DMHC DATA)
Summary of IMR outcomes for spinal surgery cases, California 2023

Total cervical spine IMR cases reviewed:          847
Overturned (plan denial reversed):                612  (72.3%)
Upheld (plan denial confirmed):                   235  (27.7%)

Most common overturn reason:
  Conservative treatment already completed:       38%
  Myelopathy exception not applied:               29%
  Plan criteria more restrictive than clinical    
    standard:                                     21%
  Procedural defect in denial notice:             12%

Note: 72.3% overturn rate for cervical spine cases significantly exceeds
the overall IMR overturn rate of 52% across all specialties, suggesting
systematic over-denial in this category.
