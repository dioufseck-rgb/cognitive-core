Dear Mr. Reyes,

This letter serves as the official determination for your appeal concerning the prior authorization denial (Case ID: PA-2024-G001) for an anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7. HealthFirst Health Plan has completed a thorough review of your appeal, the initial prior authorization request, the denial rationale, and all submitted medical documentation, in accordance with our internal clinical coverage criteria, state regulations, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

---

**Summary of Initial Denial (Dated 2024-11-01):**

The initial request for CPT 22551 was denied by Dr. Alan Foster, HealthFirst Utilization Management, with **Reason Code MED-003**. The stated reason was: "Member does not meet conservative treatment requirements under CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2. Physical therapy: member has completed only 3 weeks of physical therapy. Standard pathway requires 6 weeks minimum. Functional plateau not documented. All other criteria remain unmet."

**Summary of Appeal Basis:**

Your appeal, submitted by Dr. Sarah Okonkwo, contended that:
1.  The denial miscounted physical therapy (PT) duration; you completed 3 weeks at Valley PT and 6 weeks at Capital PT, totaling 9 weeks.
2.  Records from both PT providers were submitted with the original authorization request.
3.  Capital PT's discharge summary explicitly documents a functional plateau.
4.  All other criteria (pharmacotherapy, injection) were also completed.

---

**Appeal Review Findings:**

Our comprehensive review, following the established evaluation plan, identified several critical points:

1.  **Procedural Adequacy of Initial Denial:**
    The initial denial notice cited HealthFirst's clinical criteria (CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2) and provided a specific clinical reason for denial. As such, the denial was procedurally adequate in its form as per **CHSC § 1374.31(b)**.

2.  **Factual Accuracy of Denial Rationale:**
    The initial denial claimed that you completed "only 3 weeks of physical therapy." Our review of the submitted clinical record, specifically the documentation from Valley Physical Therapy (2024-06-01 to 2024-06-21, 3 weeks) and Capital Physical Therapy (2024-07-08 to 2024-08-16, 6 weeks), confirms that you completed a total of **9 weeks of physical therapy**. Both sets of records were reportedly submitted with the original prior authorization request.
    **Conclusion:** The initial denial was based on a significant factual error regarding the duration of conservative physical therapy completed.

3.  **Determination of Primary Diagnosis & Clinical Presentation:**
    *   **Presenting Diagnosis:** Cervical radiculopathy C6-C7, right-sided, persistent.
    *   **Imaging (MRI 2024-08-20):** C6-C7 large disc herniation with moderate right C7 nerve root compression. No cord contact, no myelomalacia, right foraminal stenosis confirmed.
    *   **Neurological Exam (2024-08-25):** Right C7 dermatomal numbness, diminished right tricep reflex, grip strength 4/5 right. No bilateral findings, no Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) signs.
    **Conclusion:** Based on the absence of myelomalacia, UMN signs, bilateral symptoms, or gait disturbance, and the explicit diagnosis, your primary diagnosis is **Cervical Radiculopathy C6-C7**. This means the HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 "Myelopathy Exception" (Section 2.2(A)) does not apply as myelopathy is not the primary diagnosis.

4.  **Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024) for Radiculopathy:**
    As your primary diagnosis is radiculopathy, we applied the standard pathway criteria under CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2:
    *   **Physical Therapy (Section 2.2(a)):** Requires a "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy." You completed 9 weeks of PT across two providers, exceeding this requirement. Furthermore, the Capital PT discharge summary explicitly documents "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected from continued PT," indicating a functional plateau.
    *   **Pharmacotherapy (Section 2.2(b)):** Requires a trial of at least two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each. You completed Gabapentin for 4 months and Naproxen for 3 months. This criterion is met.
    *   **Interventional Treatment (Section 2.2(c)):** Requires at least one interventional treatment. You received a Right C7 Epidural on 2024-09-15. This criterion is met.
    *   **Diagnosis (Section 2.1) and Imaging (Section 2.3):** Met, as confirmed by MRI findings consistent with your symptoms.
    **Conclusion:** When the factual error is corrected, you **meet all applicable conservative treatment requirements** as outlined in HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 for cervical radiculopathy.

5.  **Review for State Law Overrides:**
    *   **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 - Physical Therapy as Prerequisite:** This state law prohibits conditioning surgical approval on completion of PT if "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms." Your Capital PT discharge summary explicitly documents a functional plateau.
    **Conclusion:** Even if the plan's 6-week PT requirement were still in question, **CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)** overrides such a requirement due to the documented functional plateau.
    *   **DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014 - Myelopathy & Urgent Conditions:** This APL primarily addresses myelopathy, especially with myelomalacia or progressive pathology, to waive conservative treatment. Given your primary diagnosis of radiculopathy and absence of myelomalacia, this specific APL's more stringent waiver provisions are not directly applicable to your case.

6.  **Review for Clinical Standard Override (CHSC § 1374.32(b)):**
    **CHSC § 1374.32(b)** mandates approval if a service meets "generally accepted standards of medical practice" regardless of more restrictive plan criteria.
    *   **AANS/CNS (2023), NASS (2020), AO Spine (2021) Guidelines:** For cervical radiculopathy, surgery (such as ACDF) is considered a "reasonable indication" (Tier 2) when there are persistent symptoms and objective findings after an adequate course of conservative treatment (typically 6-12 weeks), including physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, and interventional pain management.
    *   **Conclusion:** Your clinical presentation (persistent right C6-C7 radiculopathy with objective findings and documented failure of 9 weeks of PT, 4 months of pharmacotherapy, and an epidural injection, with a functional plateau) meets these generally accepted standards for surgical intervention.

---

**Final Determination:**

Based on the comprehensive review of your appeal, the clinical records, HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria, applicable California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, the initial denial for CPT 22551 was found to be factually incorrect and inconsistent with our own criteria and state regulations.

*   The initial denial was based on a factual error regarding the duration of physical therapy completed. You completed 9 weeks of PT, not 3.
*   Upon correction of this factual error, you **meet all conservative treatment requirements** outlined in HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 for cervical radiculopathy, including adequate duration of physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, and interventional treatment.
*   Furthermore, a functional plateau in physical therapy was explicitly documented, which, under **CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)**, independently prohibits conditioning approval on further physical therapy.
*   The requested ACDF (CPT 22551) for your clinical presentation is consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice for persistent radiculopathy failing conservative care, as per **CHSC § 1374.32(b)** and major spine society guidelines.

Therefore, the initial denial of prior authorization for CPT 22551 is **OVERTURNED**.
