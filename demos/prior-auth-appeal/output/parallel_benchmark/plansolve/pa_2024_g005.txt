Dear Mr. Obi, Dr. Diallo, and HealthFirst Utilization Management,

This letter serves as the formal determination regarding the appeal for prior authorization of CPT code 22551 (ACDF C6-C7) for member Gerald Obi (M-G005), initially denied by HealthFirst Utilization Management.

**Background:**
HealthFirst's initial denial, dated 2024-11-08, cited two grounds for denial:
1.  Failure to meet conservative treatment requirements, specifically a shortfall in physical therapy (5 weeks completed vs. 6 required).
2.  Poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes (HbA1c 8.2%) presenting perioperative risks and wound healing concerns.

The appeal, submitted on behalf of Mr. Obi, disputed these grounds, asserting that 7 weeks of PT were completed, diabetes control is not a criterion under HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, all cervical spine criteria are met, and perioperative diabetes management is the surgical team's responsibility.

**Evaluation Findings:**

Following a comprehensive review of the submitted clinical record, HealthFirst's Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), relevant California state law, and generally accepted clinical standards, the following findings are made:

**1. Procedural Adequacy of the Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(a)-(b))**
The initial denial notice dated 2024-11-08 was found to be procedurally adequate. It explicitly stated the specific clinical reasons for the denial (PT shortfall and diabetes control) and is assumed to have included clear notification of Mr. Obi's Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights, consistent with California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(a).

**2. Legitimacy of Denial Ground 2: Poorly Controlled Type 2 Diabetes**
HealthFirst's Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 (Sections 2 and 3) were thoroughly reviewed. No provisions, requirements, or exclusions pertaining to diabetes control, specific HbA1c levels, or general perioperative risk factors are listed as a basis for denying authorization for CPT 22551. While optimal perioperative diabetes management is a crucial clinical consideration for surgical safety and wound healing, it is the responsibility of the surgical team and is not a criterion for prior authorization under HealthFirst's stated policy. Therefore, this denial ground is not supported by HealthFirst's own medical necessity criteria, nor by generally accepted standards for prior authorization review.

**3. Review of Denial Ground 1: Conservative Treatment - Physical Therapy Requirement**

*   **3.1. Diagnosis Classification: Myelopathy vs. Radiculopathy (Domain Knowledge & Clinical Standard Section 1.1)**
    Based on the submitted clinical documentation, including MRI findings which explicitly state "No cord contact. No myelomalacia," and neurological exam findings consistent with a left C7 dermatomal distribution (numbness, diminished tricep reflex, weakness), Mr. Obi's primary diagnosis is confirmed as cervical radiculopathy C6-C7 with functional impairment. There is no objective evidence to classify this as primary cervical myelopathy (Domain Knowledge; Clinical Standard Section 1.1).

*   **3.2. Application of Plan's Myelopathy Exception (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(A))**
    Given the primary diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy, HealthFirst's Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A), which modifies conservative treatment requirements for primary myelopathy, is not applicable to Mr. Obi's case. The standard 6-week physical therapy requirement specified in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) is the appropriate criterion for evaluation under the plan's policy.

*   **3.3. Override by State Law (CIC § 10169.5 and DMHC APL 22-014)**
    *   **Factual Discrepancy:** The initial denial stated that Mr. Obi completed 5 weeks of physical therapy, falling short of the 6-week requirement. However, the comprehensive clinical record clearly documents that Mr. Obi completed 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy (21 sessions) continuously from 2024-07-01 through 2024-08-16. This duration *exceeds* HealthFirst's stated minimum requirement of 6 weeks. Therefore, the factual basis for this denial ground is incorrect.
    *   **Functional Plateau & State Law Override:** Furthermore, the physical therapy records explicitly state a "functional plateau documented at discharge: 'no further functional improvement expected.'" California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) stipulates that a health insurer shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms." Mr. Obi's case precisely meets this state law criterion, which overrides any plan-specific requirement for additional physical therapy, regardless of duration.

*   **3.4. Override by Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021, and CHSC § 1374.32(b))**
    Mr. Obi has completed a comprehensive course of conservative treatment, including 7 weeks of physical therapy with a documented functional plateau, trials of two pharmacotherapy agents (Pregabalin for 5 months, Duloxetine for 3 months), and one interventional procedure (Left C7 Epidural with 45% temporary relief that returned to baseline within 4 weeks). This extensive conservative management, coupled with persistent symptoms and functional impairment, aligns with generally accepted standards of medical practice (e.g., AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020) which consider surgical intervention a reasonable option (Tier 2 indication) for radiculopathy that persists after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment and has reached a functional plateau. Therefore, the requested service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice. CHSC § 1374.32(b) mandates approval if the service meets this standard, regardless of more restrictive plan criteria.

**Overall Medical Necessity Determination:**
Based on the comprehensive review and application of the hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Health Plan Criteria), the requested ACDF C6-C7 (CPT 22551) for Mr. Gerald Obi is deemed medically necessary. The two grounds for the initial denial are found to be either illegitimate or factually incorrect and overridden by state law.

**Disposition:**
The initial denial for prior authorization for CPT code 22551 for Mr. Gerald Obi is hereby **OVERTURNED.**

**Reasons for Overturn:**
1.  **Illegitimate Denial Ground:** The denial based on "poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes" is not supported by any criteria or exclusions within HealthFirst's Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024.
2.  **Factual Error in Denial:** The denial's assertion of a physical therapy shortfall (5 weeks completed) is factually incorrect. The clinical record clearly demonstrates that Mr. Obi completed 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy, exceeding HealthFirst's 6-week requirement.
3.  **State Law Override:** Pursuant to California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2), the plan cannot condition approval on further physical therapy where the member has completed a course of PT and reached a documented functional plateau, which is the case for Mr. Obi. This state law explicitly overrides the plan's conservative treatment requirements.
4.  **Alignment with Clinical Standards:** Mr. Obi has completed an adequate and comprehensive course of conservative treatment, consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice for cervical radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and functional impairment, thus warranting surgical intervention.
