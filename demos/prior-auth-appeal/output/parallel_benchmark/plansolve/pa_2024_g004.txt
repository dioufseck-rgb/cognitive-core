**SUBJECT: APPEAL DETERMINATION FOR STEPHANIE ODUYA — PRIOR AUTHORIZATION DENIAL (ACDF C5-C6)**

Dear Ms. Oduya,

This letter serves as the formal determination of your appeal regarding the denial of prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6. We have thoroughly reviewed your appeal, including the basis provided by your treating physician, Dr. Patrick Walsh, and all available clinical documentation, in accordance with HealthFirst's internal policies, California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

**BACKGROUND OF DENIAL AND APPEAL:**
On October 15, 2024, HealthFirst denied the request for CPT code 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) due to "MED-003: imaging shows mild-moderate disc herniation with no cord compression and no neurological deficit on examination. Conservative treatment pathway not completed. Request does not meet medical necessity criteria for surgical intervention at this time." This denial followed an independent clinical review by Dr. Maria Rivera, a board-certified neurosurgeon.

Your appeal, submitted by Dr. Walsh, disputes Dr. Rivera's interpretation, emphasizes his 25 years of spine surgery experience and clinical judgment based on direct examination, and expresses concern about potential permanent disability if authorization is denied.

**EVALUATION PLAN AND FINDINGS:**
Our review followed a structured evaluation plan based on a hierarchy of authority: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice (CHSC §1374.31(b))**
The initial denial notice dated October 15, 2024, specified a reason code (MED-003), provided a specific clinical reason for the denial (lack of cord compression, neurological deficit, and incomplete conservative treatment), and cited medical necessity criteria. This notice meets the requirements of California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31(b) for procedural adequacy.
*Conclusion: The denial notice was procedurally adequate.*

**Step 2: Verification of Member's Clinical Condition and Imaging Against Denial Reasons (Factual Dispute Resolution)**
This step involved a critical review of the objective medical records to verify the member's clinical condition, particularly in light of Dr. Walsh's assertion of "significant cervical pathology" versus Dr. Rivera's findings of "no cord compression and no neurological deficit."

*   **Presenting Diagnosis:** The treating surgeon's documentation notes a "Cervical radiculopathy C5-C6, right-sided."
*   **Imaging (MRI dated 2024-09-10):** The MRI findings explicitly state: "C5-C6 mild to moderate disc herniation. Right C6 nerve root contact without measurable compression. **No cord contact. No cord compression. No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia.** Mild right foraminal narrowing."
*   **Neurological Examination (dated 2024-09-15):** The findings document: "Right shoulder pain and neck pain 6/10. RIGHT C6 dermatomal tingling — mild. Bicep reflex 2+ bilateral, symmetric. Grip strength 5/5 bilateral. **No weakness. No UMN signs.** Functional assessment: working full-time, no activity limitations."

Based on these objective findings, the medical records *confirm* Dr. Rivera's assessment: there is no objective evidence of spinal cord compression, no T2 signal change (myelomalacia) indicating cord injury, and no upper motor neuron (UMN) signs or objective weakness consistent with cervical myelopathy. The primary documented condition is cervical radiculopathy. While Dr. Walsh expressed a strong clinical opinion, the detailed objective findings in the medical record do not support a diagnosis of myelopathy or a severe, urgent neurological compromise that would warrant immediate surgical intervention.
*Conclusion: Myelopathy is NOT objectively confirmed. The medical records confirm Dr. Rivera's findings of no objective cord compression or neurological deficit consistent with myelopathy.*

**Step 3: Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024)**
Given that cervical myelopathy was not objectively confirmed in Step 2, the "EXCEPTION 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY" of the HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 criteria does not apply. Therefore, the standard conservative treatment requirements for radiculopathy or cervical disc herniation with neurological deficit apply.

*   **HealthFirst Criteria Section 2.2(a) (Physical Therapy):** Requires a minimum of 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy. The member completed 4 weeks of physical therapy. **(NOT MET)**
*   **HealthFirst Criteria Section 2.2(b) (Pharmacotherapy):** Requires a trial of at least two pharmacotherapy agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each. The member used Naproxen 500mg BID for 8 weeks (one agent). **(NOT MET)**
*   **HealthFirst Criteria Section 2.2(c) (Interventional Treatment):** Requires completion of one interventional treatment (e.g., epidural steroid injection). The member had no documented interventional treatment. **(NOT MET)**

The member has not met the standard conservative treatment requirements outlined in HealthFirst's plan criteria for a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy.
*Conclusion: HealthFirst plan criteria for standard conservative treatment have not been met.*

**Step 4: State Law Override - Conservative Treatment Requirements (CIC §10169.5, DMHC APL 22-014)**
We next assessed whether California state law mandates a waiver of the conservative treatment requirements despite non-completion.

*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) §10169.5(a)(3):** This provision waives physical therapy requirements if the "condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." As established in Step 2, there is no objective evidence of cord compression, UMN signs, or myelomalacia. The condition, as documented, does not represent structural neurological compromise beyond a typical radiculopathy that would preclude a trial of physical therapy.
*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014 (II.2):** This guidance waives conservative treatment where "objective imaging evidence demonstrates progressive pathology (cord signal change, myelomalacia)" AND the physician documents conservative failure AND surgery is indicated under generally accepted guidelines. The MRI explicitly states "No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia." Therefore, the primary condition for this waiver is not met. Furthermore, the member's functional assessment indicates "working full-time, no activity limitations," which does not align with an urgent, progressively deteriorating condition.

*Conclusion: California state law does not mandate a waiver of conservative treatment requirements for this specific clinical presentation.*

**Step 5: Clinical Standard Override (CHSC §1374.32(b), AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021, NASS 2020)**
Finally, we evaluated whether the requested ACDF meets generally accepted standards of medical practice, which override plan criteria if more restrictive (CHSC §1374.32(b)).

For cervical radiculopathy without myelopathy, generally accepted clinical guidelines (e.g., AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline) typically recommend a conservative treatment trial of 6 to 12 weeks before considering surgical intervention, unless there is evidence of progressive neurological deficit or severe intractable pain unresponsive to adequate conservative measures. The current medical record indicates:
*   Mild right C6 dermatomal tingling.
*   Mild improvement in pain with 4 weeks of PT.
*   Moderate pain relief with 8 weeks of Naproxen.
*   No weakness or functional limitations.

These findings do not meet the "TIER 1 — STRONG INDICATION" for surgery (e.g., moderate-severe myelopathy, myelomalacia, or progressive neurological deficit) as defined by AANS/CNS guidelines (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3.1). While "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" is a "TIER 2 — REASONABLE INDICATION," the member has not yet completed a conservative trial of this duration, nor exhausted other appropriate conservative modalities (e.g., a second pharmacotherapy agent, interventional treatments). Therefore, based on generally accepted standards of medical practice for cervical radiculopathy, surgical intervention is not indicated at this time.
*Conclusion: Generally accepted clinical standards do not support surgical intervention at this time, as the member has not completed an adequate trial of conservative therapy for radiculopathy.*

**DETERMINATION:**
Based on the comprehensive review of the medical records, HealthFirst's plan criteria, California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, the appeal for prior authorization of CPT code 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for Stephanie Oduya is **UPHELD**.

**REASONING FOR UPHOLD:**
The objective medical evidence (MRI and neurological exam) confirms the initial reviewer's assessment that the member does not have cervical myelopathy, spinal cord compression, or myelomalacia. The presenting condition is consistent with cervical radiculopathy. For this diagnosis, the member has not completed the standard conservative treatment requirements as outlined in HealthFirst's clinical criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), specifically regarding the duration of physical therapy, the number of pharmacotherapy agents, and the trial of interventional therapy. Furthermore, no provision of California state law (CIC §10169.5, DMHC APL 22-014) or generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine) indicates that a waiver of these conservative treatment requirements is appropriate for the member's current clinical presentation of mild radiculopathy without signs of urgency or severe neurological compromise.

We recognize and respect Dr. Walsh's clinical judgment and experience. However, the objective findings in the medical record do not support a deviation from the established treatment pathways at this time. Completion of a comprehensive course of conservative treatment, as supported by plan criteria and clinical guidelines for radiculopathy, is still warranted.
