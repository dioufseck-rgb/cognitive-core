**I. Introduction**

This letter constitutes the formal determination regarding the appeal submitted for Michael Green, Member ID M-445577, concerning the denial of prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6. This review was conducted by an expert health plan appeals reviewer in accordance with HealthFirst Health Plan's internal appeal procedures, California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

**II. Background**

On November 8, 2024, HealthFirst Health Plan issued an initial denial for the requested ACDF C5-C6, citing "reason_code": "MED-003", with "reason_text": "Request denied. Member does not meet criteria for requested procedure under plan policy. See attached criteria document." The denial was issued by Dr. Patricia Huang, MD, at the initial review level.

The appeal, submitted on behalf of Mr. Green, raised the following bases:
1.  No criteria document was attached to the denial letter despite being referenced.
2.  Notice of Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights was not included with the denial.
3.  The member and provider were unable to evaluate the denial without the specific criteria that were applied.

*   HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria, Document: CC-SPINE-2024 (Source Document 1).

**IV. Review Findings**

The appeal was evaluated using the provided evaluation plan, applying the Hierarchy of Authority: **State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.**

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy of Initial Denial**
*   **Finding:** The initial denial notice was procedurally defective. The denial letter explicitly referenced an attached criteria document, which was not provided, and both the member and provider confirmed its absence. This failure to specify the clinical criteria applied renders the denial defective under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31(b). Furthermore, the denial failed to include notice of IMR rights, which is also a procedural defect (CHSC §1374.31(a)(2)).

**Step 2: Member's Diagnosis and Medical Necessity of Procedure**
*   **Finding:** The member has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 with functional impairment. The MRI dated September 20, 2024, demonstrates a large disc herniation at C5-C6 with moderate left C6 nerve root compression, consistent with the member's presenting symptoms of left C6 dermatomal numbness, diminished bicep reflex, and bicep weakness (4/5 left). The clinical findings align with HealthFirst's Plan Criteria 2.1(a) for Cervical Radiculopathy and Plan Criteria 2.3 for imaging support. Under generally accepted clinical standards (AANS/CNS 2023), ACDF for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after conservative treatment is considered a TIER 2 "Reasonable Indication" for surgery.

**Step 3: Plan's Application of its Own Conservative Treatment Requirements & Myelopathy Exception**
*   **Finding:** The member's clinical record confirms the completion of standard conservative treatment requirements as outlined in HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.2 for cervical radiculopathy:
    *   **Physical Therapy:** 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy (Plan Criteria 2.2(a) requires minimum 6 weeks).
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Gabapentin 600mg TID for 4 months and Naproxen 500mg BID for 3 months (Plan Criteria 2.2(b) requires two agents for minimum 4 weeks each).
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** Left C6 epidural steroid injection (Plan Criteria 2.2(c) requires one interventional treatment).
*   The member's diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy, not cervical myelopathy. Therefore, the Myelopathy Exception (Plan Criteria 2.2(A)) does not apply. Based on the documentation, the member has met the standard conservative treatment requirements of the HealthFirst plan policy. If the original denial was based on a failure to meet these conservative treatment requirements, it would represent an incorrect application of the plan's own criteria.

**Step 4: State Law Override of Plan's Conservative Treatment Requirements**
*   **Finding:** Even if the plan were to contend that conservative treatment requirements were not fully met or sufficient, California Insurance Code (CIC) §10169.5 overrides the mechanical application of minimum physical therapy duration. The member completed 7 weeks of physical therapy, and the PT notes document a functional plateau at week 6, with persistent radicular symptoms. CIC §10169.5(a)(2) states that a health insurer shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where "the member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms." This condition has been met, thus overriding any further physical therapy requirements. DMHC APL 22-014 regarding urgent neurological conditions and myelopathy does not apply as the primary diagnosis is radiculopathy without evidence of myelopathy or myelomalacia.

**Step 5: Clinical Standard Override of Plan Criteria**
*   **Finding:** In accordance with CHSC §1374.32(b), medical necessity must be determined based on generally accepted standards of medical practice, even if a plan's internal criteria are more restrictive. For cervical radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after documented conservative treatment failure and functional plateau, ACDF is considered an appropriate and medically necessary intervention. AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) identify "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" as a TIER 2 — Reasonable Indication for surgery. The member has undergone 7 weeks of PT, pharmacotherapy, and an interventional procedure, with documentation of a plateau and inadequate relief. This aligns with clinical standards for surgical consideration.

**V. Disposition Decision Tree Application**

1.  **Procedural Defect (Step 1):** The initial denial notice was found to be procedurally defective as it failed to provide the referenced criteria document and notice of IMR rights (CHSC §1374.31(b)).
2.  **Medical Necessity Determination:** Upon a full review of the clinical record, HealthFirst's criteria, California state laws, and generally accepted clinical standards, the requested ACDF C5-C6 is determined to be medically necessary for Mr. Green's cervical radiculopathy. The member met the plan's conservative treatment requirements (Step 3), and state law (CIC §10169.5) overrides any further PT requirements due to a documented functional plateau (Step 4). The procedure aligns with generally accepted clinical standards for this condition (Step 5).

Therefore, as per the disposition decision tree, if the denial notice is found to be procedurally defective (Step 1) AND the service is determined to be medically necessary upon full review (applying State law, Clinical Standard, and Plan Criteria in hierarchy), the appropriate disposition is **OVERTURN**.

**VI. Determination**

The initial denial for prior authorization of CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for Michael Green (M-445577) is **OVERTURNED**.

The initial denial was procedurally defective, as it failed to provide the referenced clinical criteria and notice of IMR rights, as required by California Health & Safety Code §1374.31(b). Furthermore, a comprehensive medical necessity review indicates that the requested procedure is medically necessary. The member has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 with objective neurological findings and imaging support. The member has completed a documented course of conservative treatment, including physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, and an interventional procedure, with a documented functional plateau and persistent symptoms. In accordance with California Insurance Code §10169.5(a)(2), further physical therapy cannot be a condition for approval due to the documented functional plateau. The requested procedure is consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice for cervical radiculopathy with failed conservative management (AANS/CNS 2023).

**VII. Action Required**

HealthFirst Health Plan is directed to authorize the Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 (CPT 22551) for Michael Green (M-445577).
