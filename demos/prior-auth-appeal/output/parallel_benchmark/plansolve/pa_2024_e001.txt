**SUBJECT: APPEAL DETERMINATION FOR PRIOR AUTHORIZATION DENIAL — PA-2024-E001**

Dear Mr. Patel,

This letter serves as the formal determination of your appeal regarding the denial of prior authorization for the requested two-level anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 and C6-C7. We understand the importance of this decision to your care and appreciate your patience during this review process.

**BACKGROUND:**
On November 1, 2024, HealthFirst PPO Gold denied authorization for a two-level ACDF (CPT 22552) for cervical disc disease at C5-C6 and C6-C7. The denial, issued by Dr. Patricia Huang, MD, cited reason code MED-003, stating: "Two-level fusion not supported. C5-C6 level shows pathology consistent with surgical criteria. C6-C7 shows mild changes only. Two-level fusion not approved when one level meets criteria and one does not."

**APPEAL BASIS:**
Your appeal, submitted by Dr. Lisa Park, MD, asserted the following grounds for reconsideration:
1.  Both levels (C5-C6 and C6-C7) are symptomatic per the treating physician.
2.  Adjacent levels should be addressed simultaneously to prevent accelerated degeneration.
3.  The member meets full criteria for both levels.

**REVIEW PROCESS:**
As an expert health plan appeals reviewer, I have conducted a thorough and impartial review of your case, including the initial prior authorization request, the denial documentation, your submitted appeal, and the available clinical record. This review strictly followed the HealthFirst internal evaluation plan for PA-2024-E001, which prioritizes applicable State Law, followed by clinical standards of care, and then HealthFirst's own plan criteria, where sources conflict.

**FINDINGS AND DETERMINATION:**

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b))**
*   **Finding:** The denial notice dated November 1, 2024, specifies reason code MED-003 and provides a specific clinical reason for the denial: "Two-level fusion not supported. C5-C6 level shows pathology consistent with surgical criteria. C6-C7 shows mild changes only. Two-level fusion not approved when one level meets criteria and one does not." This explicitly identifies the specific level for which criteria were deemed not met (C6-C7) and the reason.
*   **Conclusion:** The denial notice is procedurally adequate, consistent with California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b). There is no procedural defect.

**Step 2: Assessment of Primary Clinical Diagnosis, Myelopathy Status, and Completeness of Clinical Documentation**
*   **Objective:** To determine the member's primary diagnosis (radiculopathy vs. myelopathy) and its severity, including the presence or absence of myelomalacia, and to ensure all necessary clinical documentation is available for a comprehensive review against plan criteria, state law, and clinical standards. This distinction is critical, as state law (DMHC APL 22-014, CIC 10169.5) and clinical guidelines offer different pathways for myelopathy.
*   **Finding:** Upon review of the provided clinical record, critical information necessary to make a definitive determination regarding the presence and severity of myelopathy, and its primary diagnosis status, is missing from your appeal file. Specifically, the following documentation is required:
    *   **Detailed neurological exam findings:** The record lacks specific findings such as Hoffman sign, hyperreflexia, bilateral upper extremity weakness, or gait disturbance, which are essential for objectively confirming and classifying cervical myelopathy. The noted "right tricep reflex slightly diminished" and "mild C7 symptoms possibly present but not clearly documented" are insufficient to confirm myelopathy as a primary diagnosis.
    *   **Comprehensive MRI report:** While an MRI finding is provided, a full radiologist's report detailing the extent of spinal cord compression at both C5-C6 and C6-C7, and crucially, explicitly stating the presence or absence of myelomalacia (T2 cord signal change) at either level, is not available. The current MRI findings indicate "no cord contact" at C6-C7, but do not provide further detail on the cord at C5-C6 or C6-C7.
    *   **Treating physician's detailed clinical notes:** The appeal file does not contain comprehensive notes from Dr. Park that provide a clear clinical rationale for requesting a two-level fusion, particularly addressing the C6-C7 level given the initial denial's reasoning of "mild changes only." Such notes are necessary to understand the clinical significance attributed to the C6-C7 level in the context of your overall condition.
*   **Conclusion:** A definitive determination on the medical necessity of the two-level ACDF cannot be made without a complete clinical picture, especially regarding the potential diagnosis and severity of myelopathy. Without this information, it is impossible to accurately assess the case against HealthFirst Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024), California State Law (DMHC APL 22-014, CHSC 1374.32(b), CIC 10169.5), and generally accepted clinical standards (AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021, NASS 2020).

**DISPOSITION:**
