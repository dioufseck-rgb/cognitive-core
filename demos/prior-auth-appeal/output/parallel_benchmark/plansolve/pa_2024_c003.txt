**SUBJECT: Appeal Determination for Prior Authorization Denial of ACDF (CPT 22551)**

Dear Helen Brooks and Dr. James Park,

This letter serves as the HealthFirst Health Plan appeal determination for the prior authorization denial of an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) C4-C5 for Helen Brooks. The initial denial, dated 2024-11-20, cited that the "Myelopathy exception does not apply" and that the "Standard pathway: PT completed (8 weeks), pharmacotherapy met, but no interventional procedure completed," further noting that MRI T2 signal change was "minimal and may represent artifact" and "below threshold for clinical significance."

Your appeal asserted that a second radiologist opinion confirmed early myelomalacia and that the treating physician documented myelopathy on clinical grounds independently of imaging, and that the T2 signal change was not artifact.

We have conducted a thorough review of the medical records submitted, your appeal basis, the HealthFirst clinical coverage criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), relevant state laws, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

**Review Findings:**

**A. Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice:**
The initial denial notice (dated 2024-11-20) specified clinical reasons for the denial related to imaging findings and the lack of an interventional procedure, referencing the "Myelopathy exception" and "Standard pathway." This aligns with the requirements of California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31(b) for providing a specific clinical reason and criteria, and is therefore deemed procedurally adequate.

**B. Diagnosis Confirmation & Classification:**
Our review of the clinical record confirms the presence of cervical myelopathy.
*   **Neurological Exam (2024-10-05):** Documented bilateral Hoffman signs, bilateral hyperreflexia in upper extremities, and subtle balance impairment on tandem gait. These are objective upper motor neuron (UMN) signs consistent with myelopathy.
*   **Imaging Review:** While the initial MRI report (Radiologist A) noted "Possible early T2 signal change — equivocal, may represent artifact," a second read by Radiologist B (treating hospital, 2024-10-15) explicitly confirmed "C4-C5 cord compression with early myelomalacia. T2 hyperintensity is reproducible on multiple sequences and represents early cord signal change, not artifact. Consistent with early myelopathy." This finding of myelomalacia, which indicates existing spinal cord injury, in conjunction with the objective UMN signs, firmly establishes a diagnosis of cervical myelopathy as the primary condition (referencing Plan Criteria 2.1(b), Domain Knowledge, and Clinical Standard 1.1).

**C. Application of Plan's Myelopathy Exception (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2A):**
The HealthFirst plan criteria (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2A) state that for confirmed cervical myelopathy, the physical therapy (PT) requirement is reduced to a 4-week minimum, and the interventional treatment requirement is waived if the treating physician documents a risk of neurological deterioration.
*   Helen Brooks completed 8 weeks of PT, exceeding the 4-week minimum required under the myelopathy exception.
*   Pharmacotherapy requirements were met (Gabapentin for 3 months, Cyclobenzaprine for 6 weeks).
*   The treating physician explicitly documented that interventional procedures were not performed "due to myelopathy concern — epidural steroid injections are relatively contraindicated in cord compression." This documentation clearly satisfies the condition for waiving the interventional treatment requirement under the plan's own myelopathy exception.
Therefore, the plan's initial denial, stating the "Myelopathy exception does not apply," was an incorrect application of its own coverage criteria. Helen Brooks meets the conditions for the Myelopathy Exception 2.2A.

**D. State Law Override – Conservative Treatment Requirements:**
California state law provides specific conditions under which conservative treatment requirements cannot be imposed.
*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) §10169.5(a)(3):** Prohibits conditioning approval on physical therapy completion where "The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." Helen Brooks' confirmed myelopathy with cord compression, myelomalacia (as confirmed by Radiologist B), and objective UMN signs (Hoffman, hyperreflexia, gait impairment) unequivocally constitutes "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise."
*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014 (II.2):** States that plans "SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment as a prerequisite to surgical authorization" when objective imaging evidence demonstrates "progressive pathology (cord signal change, myelomalacia)" and surgery is indicated under generally accepted guidelines. The confirmed myelomalacia is explicitly identified in APL 22-014 as a finding that may support waiving standard conservative treatment requirements.
Based on these state laws, the plan's requirement for an interventional procedure as a prerequisite for surgery is overridden.

**E. Clinical Standard Override – Myelopathy/Myelomalacia Management:**
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, as outlined by authoritative sources such as AANS/CNS Guidelines (2023) and AO Spine Guidelines (2021), emphasize different treatment approaches for myelopathy compared to radiculopathy.
*   **Myelomalacia Significance:** The presence of myelomalacia (T2 signal change) on MRI indicates existing spinal cord injury and is considered a strong surgical indication, often serving as a contraindication to extended conservative treatment trials (Clinical Standard 1.2).
*   **Conservative Treatment for Myelopathy:** For moderate to severe cervical myelopathy with objective cord compression (which Helen Brooks exhibits), major spine societies (AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021) strongly recommend surgical intervention (Tier 1 indication). These guidelines explicitly state there is "no high-quality evidence supporting a mandatory course of physical therapy prior to surgical intervention" for moderate to severe myelopathy and that "delaying surgery in such patients... may result in neurological deterioration" (Clinical Standard 2.1).
*   **CHSC §1374.32(b):** Mandates that if a service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice, it "SHALL be approved regardless of whether the plan's internal criteria are more restrictive."
The plan's denial, based on a conservative treatment pathway (specifically the interventional procedure) that is not supported by evidence for myelopathy with myelomalacia, is inconsistent with generally accepted clinical standards of care.

**Resolution of Conflicts & Determination:**

The hierarchy of authority for conflict resolution is State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria. In this case, there are significant conflicts between the plan's denial reasoning and both state law and generally accepted clinical standards, as well as an incorrect application of the plan's own criteria.

*   The plan's failure to correctly apply its own Myelopathy Exception 2.2A, where the member clearly qualified, would lead to approval.
*   California State Law (CIC §10169.5(a)(3) and DMHC APL 22-014 (II.2)) overrides the plan's conservative treatment requirements due to objective neurological compromise and myelomalacia, mandating approval.
*   Generally accepted clinical standards (AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021), consistent with CHSC §1374.32(b), mandate approval of surgical intervention for myelopathy with myelomalacia, deeming the plan's more restrictive conservative treatment requirements (specifically for interventional procedures) inconsistent with prudent clinical judgment.

Therefore, applying the hierarchy of authority and the disposition decision tree, the denial cannot be upheld.

**DETERMINATION: OVERTURN**

The denial for prior authorization of an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) C4-C5 for Helen Brooks is **OVERTURNED.**

**Reasoning for Determination:**
The decision to OVERTURN the denial is based on the following:
1.  **Misapplication of Plan's Own Criteria:** HealthFirst failed to correctly apply its Myelopathy Exception (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2A). Helen Brooks met all conditions for this exception, including documented myelopathy as primary diagnosis, completion of reduced PT (8 weeks), met pharmacotherapy, and a treating physician's documentation of risk contraindicating an interventional procedure.
2.  **State Law Override:** California state law (CIC §10169.5(a)(3) and DMHC APL 22-014 (II.2)) prohibits conditioning surgical approval on further conservative treatment (including interventional procedures) when objective evidence of structural neurological compromise (myelopathy with myelomalacia and UMN signs) is present.
3.  **Clinical Standard Override:** Generally accepted standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021) for myelopathy with myelomalacia indicate that surgical intervention is a strong primary treatment and that delaying surgery for further conservative treatment trials is not supported by evidence and may lead to neurological deterioration. Under CHSC §1374.32(b), services meeting these standards must be approved irrespective of more restrictive plan criteria.
