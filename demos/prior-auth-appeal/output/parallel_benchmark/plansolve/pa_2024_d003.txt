**II. Background**
The initial prior authorization request for an ACDF at C6-C7 was denied by HealthFirst on October 15, 2024. The denial reason code was MED-003, with the stated reason "Request denied based on peer-to-peer review. Plan medical director spoke with requesting physician on 10/12/2024. 1.  Denial criteria communicated verbally only; member never received written criteria.
2.  California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31 requires written criteria in the denial notice.
3.  Verbal peer-to-peer discussion cannot substitute for a written denial notice.

**III. Evaluation Plan and Findings**

The appeal was evaluated systematically following the established HealthFirst Appeal Evaluation Plan, utilizing a hierarchy of authority: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.

**1. Procedural Adequacy of the Initial Denial (CHSC § 1374.31(a) and (b))**
*   **Finding:** The initial denial issued by HealthFirst on October 15, 2024, is procedurally defective.
*   **Rationale:** The denial explicitly stated "Verbal only — peer-to-peer call" as the basis for the written denial and indicated that an "IMR notice was included: false." California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(a)(2) mandates that a denial notice based on medical necessity "SHALL...Include in the denial notice: the specific criteria applied, the specific clinical reason for the denial, and notice of IMR rights." Furthermore, CHSC § 1374.31(b) specifies that "A denial notice that fails to specify the clinical criteria applied or the specific clinical reason for the denial is DEFECTIVE." As the initial denial failed to provide specific written criteria, the clinical reason in writing, and omitted the required IMR notice, it constitutes a clear procedural defect under state law.

**2. Verify Member's Primary Diagnosis and Clinical Severity**
*   **Finding:** The provided clinical record documents a "presenting_diagnosis" of "Cervical radiculopathy C6-C7." Imaging (MRI 2024-09-01) showed "C6-C7 disc herniation, right C7 nerve root compression, moderate foraminal stenosis." Neurological exam (2024-09-05) revealed "Right C7 dermatomal numbness and tingling, tricep reflex diminished, grip 4+/5."
*   **Rationale:** These findings are consistent with cervical radiculopathy. The clinical documentation *does not* provide objective evidence for moderate-to-severe cervical myelopathy, such as spinal cord compression, upper motor neuron signs (e.g., Hoffman sign, hyperreflexia, gait disturbance, bilateral symptoms), or myelomalacia (T2 signal change) on MRI. Therefore, based on the submitted records, the primary diagnosis is determined to be cervical radiculopathy.

**3. Review Application of Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2)**
*   **Finding:** Due to the procedural defect identified in Step 1, it is impossible to definitively determine how HealthFirst applied its criteria, as no specific criteria were cited in writing.
*   **Hypothetical Analysis (assuming denial based on conservative treatment for radiculopathy):** If the denial was based on the standard conservative treatment requirements for radiculopathy (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2), the member's record shows 5 weeks of physical therapy (6 weeks required), pharmacotherapy with Meloxicam for 3 months (meets 4-week minimum for one agent, but Prednisone taper for 2 weeks does not meet 4-week minimum for a second agent, indicating only one agent meeting criteria), and no interventional treatment (one required). Given the documented diagnosis of radiculopathy, the Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A) would not apply. Thus, the member would not meet all standard conservative treatment requirements. However, this hypothetical analysis cannot confirm the plan's *actual* application of criteria due to the defective denial.

**4. Evaluate State Law Overrides for Conservative Treatment Requirements (CIC §10169.5, DMHC APL 22-014)**
*   **Finding:** State law overrides regarding conservative treatment for urgent neurological conditions or myelopathy do not apply to this case based on the provided clinical documentation.
*   **Rationale:**
    *   **DMHC APL 22-014** (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions) specifically addresses conditions with "objective imaging evidence [of] progressive pathology (cord signal change, myelomalacia)" requiring waiver of conservative treatment. As established in Step 2, the clinical record *does not* indicate myelomalacia or other objective evidence of progressive cord pathology.
    *   **CIC § 10169.5(a)(3)** (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite) exempts PT completion if "The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address," such as myelomalacia with cord compression. This is not documented in the clinical record.
    *   **CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)** allows an exemption if the member reached a "functional plateau." While the treating physician "declares plateau in appeal letter," this is not formally documented by a therapist, and the member completed 5 weeks of PT (short of the plan's 6-week minimum for radiculopathy). Without clearer documentation, this override is not strongly established.

**5. Review Consistency with Generally Accepted Clinical Standards (AANS/CNS 2023, AO Spine 2021, CHSC §1374.32(b))**
*   **Finding:** For a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy without evidence of myelopathy or myelomalacia, generally accepted clinical standards typically support a trial of conservative treatment that is longer than what has been documented.
*   **Rationale:** The AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Guidelines (2020) categorize radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment as a "Reasonable Indication" (TIER 2) for surgery. The member has completed 5 weeks of PT, 3 months of one pharmacotherapy agent, but no interventional treatment. For radiculopathy, generally accepted standards do not strongly indicate surgery at this stage of conservative treatment. The compelling evidence supporting immediate surgery or waiver of conservative treatment, such as for moderate-severe myelopathy with myelomalacia (TIER 1 indications), is not present in the member's clinical record.

**IV. Final Disposition Determination**

Based on the comprehensive review and adherence to the evaluation plan, the final disposition of this appeal is **REMAND**.

**Rationale for REMAND:**
The initial denial of prior authorization PA-2024-D003 is procedurally defective under California Health & Safety Code § 1374.31(a) and (b). The absence of a written denial notice specifying the clinical criteria applied, the specific clinical reason for the denial, and the lack of an IMR notice are critical omissions. This procedural defect prevents the member from fully understanding the basis of the denial and properly exercising their appeal rights.

While the clinical review (Steps 2-5) indicates that the documented diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy and not myelopathy, and that the conservative treatment course for radiculopathy is incomplete per HealthFirst's criteria and general clinical standards, we cannot definitively UPHOLD or OVERTURN the initial denial on medical necessity grounds due to the fundamental procedural flaws in how the denial was communicated. A proper denial notice is required for the member to fully exercise appeal rights and for HealthFirst to ensure its review process is compliant.
