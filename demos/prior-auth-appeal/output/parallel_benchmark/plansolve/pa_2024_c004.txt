**EVALUATION PLAN AND FINDINGS:**

Our review was conducted according to a structured evaluation plan, applying a hierarchy of authority where State Law supersedes Clinical Standards, and Clinical Standards supersede Plan Criteria.

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice**
*   **Purpose:** To determine if the initial denial notice met California regulatory requirements (CHSC §1374.31(b)).
*   **Finding:** **Positive Finding.** The denial notice specified "CC-SPINE-2024" as the clinical criteria applied and clearly stated the specific clinical reason for denial ("Cervical stenosis documented but no neurological deficit on examination. Member has axial neck pain only. No radiculopathy, no myelopathy documented. Surgical intervention not indicated for axial pain without neurological deficit under CC-SPINE-2024."). This meets the requirements of CHSC §1374.31(b).

**Step 2: Initial Review of Denial Reason Against Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024)**
*   **Purpose:** To ascertain if the plan's stated denial reason is internally consistent with its own criteria, assuming the factual basis provided in the denial is accurate.
*   **Finding:** **Positive Finding.** The plan's denial reason is consistent with its own criteria (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.1 and Section 3.1). Section 3.1 of the plan's criteria explicitly states that authorization will be denied if "Surgery is requested solely for axial neck pain without documented radiculopathy or myelopathy." The clinical record confirms the member's neurological examination was "Completely normal" with "No radiculopathy. No myelopathy signs. No UMN signs. Axial neck pain only."

**Step 3: Myelopathy Classification and Application of Plan's Myelopathy Exception**
*   **Purpose:** To critically assess whether the member's clinical presentation meets the definition of cervical myelopathy and if the plan correctly considered or applied its own "Myelopathy Exception" (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A)).
*   **Finding:** **Negative Finding.** Based on a thorough review of the clinical record:
    *   The neurological examination dated 2024-09-20 explicitly states, "Completely normal neurological examination. No radiculopathy. No myelopathy signs. No UMN signs. Axial neck pain only, 5/10."
    *   The MRI dated 2024-09-15 shows "C5-C6 severe central canal stenosis. Cord contact without compression. No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia."
    *   According to generally accepted domain knowledge, cervical myelopathy requires spinal cord compression on MRI *in conjunction with* upper motor neuron signs (e.g., Hoffman, hyperreflexia), bilateral symptoms, or gait disturbance, and often includes myelomalacia (cord signal change).
    *   As there are no documented objective neurological deficits, upper motor neuron signs, or myelomalacia, the clinical evidence does not support a diagnosis of active cervical myelopathy per either clinical standards or HealthFirst's Section 2.1(b). Therefore, the Plan's Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A) does not apply.

**Step 4: State Law Overrides (CIC 10169.5, DMHC APL 22-014)**
*   **Purpose:** To determine if California state law mandates approval or waiver of conservative treatment, regardless of plan criteria.
*   **Finding:** **Negative Finding.** While the member has severe central canal stenosis with cord contact, which is significant structural pathology, the specific conditions under state law for overriding conservative treatment requirements are not met for this presentation:
    *   **CIC §10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):** This provision waives PT requirements for conditions that PT cannot address if there is "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise." However, this is typically applied when there is an *active neurological deficit* (e.g., myelopathy, radiculopathy) that PT cannot resolve. In this case, there is no active neurological deficit to address with PT; the member's primary complaint is axial neck pain, for which PT did provide modest benefit. The request is for *prophylactic* surgery due to future risk rather than treatment of an existing neurological compromise.
    *   **DMHC APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):** This APL primarily addresses cases of myelopathy with objective progressive pathology, specifically identifying myelomalacia, as conditions that may warrant waiver of conservative treatment. The member does not have documented myelomalacia, progressive neurological deficits, or a current diagnosis of myelopathy. Therefore, this APL does not mandate approval or waiver of conservative treatment for the member's current clinical presentation.

**Step 5: Clinical Standard Overrides (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021)**
*   **Purpose:** To determine if the requested service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice that would override plan criteria.
*   **Finding:** **Negative Finding.** Generally accepted clinical guidelines do not support surgical intervention for the member's current clinical presentation:
    *   The AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) classify "Axial neck pain without radiculopathy or myelopathy" and "Imaging findings without correlating symptoms" as TIER 3 — Surgery NOT INDICATED.
    *   The member's MRI shows severe stenosis with cord contact but *no T2 signal change (myelomalacia)* and the neurological exam is *completely normal*, with *no signs or symptoms of radiculopathy or myelopathy*.
    *   While the surgeon's note emphasizes the risk of future injury due to the critically narrow canal and the member's occupation, the provided clinical guidelines for cervical spine surgery focus on *existing* symptomatic conditions (radiculopathy, myelopathy with objective signs, or myelomalacia) as indications for surgery. Prophylactic surgery for severe stenosis *without current neurological deficits or myelomalacia* is not explicitly identified as a strong or reasonable indication (Tier 1 or Tier 2) in these guidelines.

**Step 6: Cross-Source Conflict Resolution**
*   **Purpose:** To explicitly apply the hierarchy of authority (State law > Clinical standard > Plan criteria) to resolve any conflicts.
*   **Finding:** **Negative Finding.** No higher authority (State Law or Clinical Standard) mandates approval of the requested surgery in this specific case. Both state law provisions and generally accepted clinical standards do not override the plan's criteria for this presentation, as there is no documented active myelopathy, radiculopathy, or other neurological deficit, nor myelomalacia, that would trigger an override of the plan's medical necessity criteria. The plan's denial for "axial neck pain only" without documented neurological deficit or myelopathy is consistent with all applicable sources.

**DETERMINATION:**

Based on the comprehensive review of the provided clinical information, the HealthFirst PPO Gold plan criteria, relevant California state laws, and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice, the original denial for ACDF C5-C6 for Raymond Flores (Case ID: PA-2024-C004) is **UPHELD**.

The medical record demonstrates severe cervical stenosis with cord contact, but critically, it lacks objective evidence of neurological deficits (radiculopathy or myelopathy) or myelomalacia at the time of the request. The member's presenting symptoms are primarily axial neck pain, and the neurological examination is entirely normal. While the risk of future injury is a valid concern, particularly given the member's occupation, current medical necessity criteria and generally accepted clinical guidelines do not support surgical intervention for this specific condition in the absence of current neurological symptoms or signs of spinal cord injury (e.g., myelomalacia).
