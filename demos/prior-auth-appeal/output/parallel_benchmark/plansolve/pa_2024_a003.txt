Dear Ms. Osei,

This letter serves as the formal determination of your appeal regarding the denial of prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at levels C4-C5, requested by Dr. Michael Torres, MD. HealthFirst PPO Gold's Appeals Review Department has completed a comprehensive review of your appeal, the clinical documentation provided, and HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria, in accordance with applicable state and federal regulations.

**Background:**

Your request for CPT code 22551 (ACDF C4-C5) was initially denied on October 15, 2024, by Dr. Alan Foster, MD (Denial ID: MED-003). The reason for denial was stated as: "Member has not completed required conservative treatment. Physical therapy: 5 weeks completed, 6 required. No cervical epidural steroid injection on record. Member does not meet Section 2.2 requirements."

Your appeal asserts that:
1.  You have a permanent pacemaker, which medically contraindicates a cervical epidural steroid injection (CESI).
2.  Your physician has documented this interventional contraindication in your chart.
3.  Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014 myelopathy provisions apply, given documented myelomalacia.

**Clinical Review Summary:**

The comprehensive clinical record includes:
*   **Presenting Diagnosis:** Progressive cervical myelopathy C4-C5 with myelomalacia.
*   **Comorbidities:** Permanent pacemaker (implanted 2019).
*   **Imaging:** MRI dated August 20, 2024, performed with a pacemaker-compatible protocol, revealed severe central cord compression at C4-C5 with confirmed T2 signal change (myelomalacia), indicating progressive cord injury.
*   **Neurological Exam:** Dated August 25, 2024, findings included bilateral lower extremity spasticity, hyperreflexia, impaired fine motor function in both hands, and a positive Lhermitte sign – all consistent with cervical myelopathy.
*   **Conservative Treatment History:**
    *   **Physical Therapy:** 5 weeks (15 sessions) completed. Treatment was stopped due to pacemaker precautions limiting certain modalities and the physician's determination that further PT was not addressing neurological progression.
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Trials of Baclofen and Methylprednisolone taper provided only partial or temporary relief.
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** A cervical epidural steroid injection (CESI) was *not* performed. The clinical record explicitly documents that fluoroscopy-guided CESI is *contraindicated* due to your permanent pacemaker, as confirmed by anesthesiology consultation (Dr. Rivera, 2024-09-10), with this contraindication documented in your chart.

**Evaluation and Findings:**

Our review followed the HealthFirst Appeals Review Evaluation Plan, adhering to the established hierarchy of authority: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.

1.  **Procedural Adequacy of Initial Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b)):**
    The initial denial notice specified the clinical criteria applied (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2) and the specific clinical reasons for denial. Based on this, the denial notice is considered procedurally adequate.

2.  **Determine Primary Diagnosis: Cervical Myelopathy vs. Radiculopathy:**
    The medical records, including presenting diagnosis, MRI findings (severe central cord compression, myelomalacia), and neurological exam findings (bilateral spasticity, hyperreflexia, Lhermitte sign), consistently and unequivocally confirm **progressive cervical myelopathy C4-C5 with myelomalacia** as the primary diagnosis. This classification guides the application of myelopathy-specific criteria and regulations.

3.  **Review Plan's Application of its Standard Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2):**
    The initial denial correctly identified that, under the **standard** Section 2.2 criteria, the member had completed 5 out of the required 6 weeks of physical therapy and did not have a cervical epidural steroid injection on record. Therefore, the plan's factual assessment against its *standard* criteria was accurate.

4.  **Evaluate Plan's Application of Myelopathy Exception (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A)):**
    Given that primary cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia has been confirmed (Step 2), HealthFirst's own clinical coverage criteria, **CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY**, is applicable. This exception modifies the conservative treatment requirements for members with confirmed cervical myelopathy:
    *   **Physical Therapy:** The requirement is reduced to a minimum of **4 weeks**. Your medical record documents 5 weeks of supervised physical therapy completion, which *exceeds* this reduced minimum requirement.
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** This requirement (Section 2.2(c)) is **waived** if the treating physician documents that interventional treatment poses a risk of neurological deterioration. Your clinical record explicitly documents that fluoroscopy-guided CESI is **contraindicated** due to your permanent pacemaker, a risk factor confirmed by anesthesiology consultation and documented in your chart. This fully meets the waiver condition of Section 2.2(A).

The initial denial failed to apply this specific myelopathy exception outlined in HealthFirst's own criteria. When applied correctly, the conservative treatment requirements for cervical myelopathy, as specified in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(A), have been fully satisfied.

**Determination:**

Based on the thorough review of the medical record and application of HealthFirst's own clinical coverage criteria, the denial for CPT 22551 (ACDF C4-C5) is hereby **OVERTURNED**.

**Rationale for Determination:**

The original denial for lack of conservative treatment (specifically, 6 weeks of PT and a CESI) was based on the standard criteria in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2. However, your diagnosis of progressive cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia triggers the **EXCEPTION 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY** within the same document. This exception reduces the physical therapy requirement to 4 weeks (you completed 5 weeks) and waives the interventional treatment requirement if the treating physician documents a risk of neurological deterioration. Your pacemaker provides a clear, documented medical contraindication, satisfying this waiver condition. Therefore, you meet the conservative treatment requirements as modified by HealthFirst's own myelopathy exception.
