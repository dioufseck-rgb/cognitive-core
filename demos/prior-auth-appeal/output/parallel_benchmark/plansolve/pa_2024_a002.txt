**SUBJECT: Appeal Determination for Prior Authorization Denial of CPT 22551 (ACDF C6-C7, one level) for Marcus Webb**

Dear Mr. Webb, Dr. Chen, and HealthFirst Health Plan,

This letter serves as the formal determination for the appeal of the prior authorization denial for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 for Mr. Marcus Webb (Member ID: M-223344). This appeal review was conducted in accordance with HealthFirst’s appeal procedures, California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

**I. Summary of Initial Denial**

HealthFirst Health Plan initially denied authorization for the requested ACDF procedure on November 5, 2024. The denial reason, as stated in the denial notice, was: "Member completed 4 weeks of physical therapy. Standard pathway requires 6 weeks minimum. Myelopathy exception requires myelopathy as primary diagnosis — treating physician's most recent note lists 'cervical spondylosis with radiculopathy' as primary diagnosis. Member does not qualify for the myelopathy exception." This denial referenced HealthFirst's Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) and Exception 2.2(A).

**II. Member/Provider Appeal Basis**

The appeal submitted on behalf of Mr. Webb asserted the following:
1.  Myelomalacia is documented on MRI, triggering regulatory overrides regardless of diagnosis labeling.
2.  The treating physician used "cervical spondylosis with radiculopathy" as a billing code, but clinical notes clearly document myelopathy.
3.  DMHC APL 22-014 mandates waiver of conservative treatment when myelomalacia is present.

**III. Review Standards**

This appeal was evaluated based on the following hierarchy of authority for conflict resolution, where higher authority governs:
1.  California State Law (e.g., California Insurance Code (CIC), California Health & Safety Code (CHSC), Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) All-Plan Letters (APLs))
2.  Generally Accepted Clinical Standards of Medical Practice (e.g., AANS/CNS Guidelines, AO Spine Guidelines, NASS Guidelines, Cochrane Reviews)
3.  HealthFirst’s Internal Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024)

**IV. Evaluation Findings**

The appeal was evaluated systematically following the provided evaluation plan:

**1. Procedural Adequacy Check of Denial Notice (CHSC §1374.31(b))**
    *   **Finding:** The initial denial notice clearly specified the clinical criteria applied (standard PT pathway, myelopathy exception) and the specific clinical reason for the denial (4 weeks PT completed, 6 weeks required; myelopathy not listed as primary diagnosis).
    *   **Conclusion:** The denial notice was procedurally adequate. Proceed to Step 2.

**2. Review of Plan's Internal Criteria Application (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) and Exception 2.2(A))**
    *   **Finding:** HealthFirst's policy states, "The myelopathy exception applies only when myelopathy is the PRIMARY diagnosis. Cases where myelopathy is documented as a finding but radiculopathy is the primary complaint are subject to standard conservative treatment requirements." The denial noted that the treating physician's most recent note *listed* "cervical spondylosis with radiculopathy" as the primary diagnosis. Based on this strict interpretation of the plan's written policy, the initial reviewer applied HealthFirst's internal rule correctly *as written* to deny the myelopathy exception and require the full 6 weeks of PT.
    *   **Conclusion:** The plan's initial reviewer correctly applied the internal criteria *as written* based on the information available at the time of the initial review. Proceed to Step 3, as higher authorities may override these criteria.

**3. State Law Override Check (CIC §10169.5 and DMHC APL 22-014)**
    *   **Finding:** The medical record was thoroughly reviewed for objective evidence of neurological compromise and documentation related to state law requirements.
        *   **Clinical Presentation:** Initial presentation notes from 2024-07-01 describe bilateral hand weakness, coordination problems, and presence of Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) signs. The neurological exam from the same date documents "Bilateral grip weakness 3/5, Hoffman sign positive bilateral, hyperreflexia bilateral upper extremities, tandem gait impaired." These are classic objective findings consistent with cervical myelopathy.
        *   **Imaging:** The MRI dated 2024-07-10 shows "C6-C7 disc herniation with moderate cord compression. T2 signal change within cord consistent with early myelomalacia." Myelomalacia signifies existing spinal cord injury.
        *   **Conservative Treatment:** The member completed 4 weeks of physical therapy. The treating physician halted further PT after myelomalacia was confirmed on MRI, explicitly stating that further PT was contraindicated.
        *   **Diagnosis Discrepancy:** While an ICD-10 billing code for "cervical spondylosis with radiculopathy" was used on PT referrals, the treating physician's narrative notes consistently describe cervical myelopathy and myelomalacia on MRI. The physician stated this billing code was an administrative artifact.

    *   **Analysis against California State Law:**
        *   **CIC §10169.5(a)(3):** This statute prohibits a plan from conditioning approval of surgery on physical therapy completion where there is "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." The presence of "T2 signal change within cord consistent with early myelomalacia" on MRI, combined with "moderate cord compression" and documented objective UMN signs (Hoffman sign, hyperreflexia, bilateral weakness, gait impairment), constitutes clear and objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address. Furthermore, the treating physician documented that further PT was contraindicated after myelomalacia was confirmed. This explicitly triggers the waiver of physical therapy requirements under CIC §10169.5(a)(3).
        *   **DMHC APL 22-014, II.2 & II.3:** This All-Plan Letter mandates that for conditions where delay risks irreversible harm (specifically identifying cervical myelopathy with cord compression and myelomalacia as urgent), plans "SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment" when objective imaging shows progressive pathology (myelomalacia), the treating physician documents conservative failure, and surgery is indicated under generally accepted guidelines. The MRI finding of myelomalacia meets the objective imaging criterion for progressive pathology. The treating physician's decision to halt PT due to myelomalacia and document it as contraindicated demonstrates conservative treatment failure in the context of neurological urgency. Surgical intervention for myelopathy with myelomalacia is a Tier 1 strong indication under AANS/CNS 2023 guidelines. Additionally, APL 22-014, II.3 states that plans shall not apply internal criteria more restrictive than generally accepted standards. HealthFirst's strict "primary diagnosis" clause and mandatory 6-week PT for myelopathy with myelomalacia are more restrictive than the APL's guidance and established clinical standards (AO Spine 2021, AANS/CNS 2023), which contraindicate extended conservative trials in such cases.
    *   **Conclusion:** Both CIC §10169.5(a)(3) and DMHC APL 22-014 unequivocally override HealthFirst's 6-week PT requirement and its strict "primary diagnosis" clause for the myelopathy exception. The objective clinical and imaging findings clearly establish a condition that warrants immediate surgical consideration, irrespective of conservative treatment duration or billing code nomenclature.

**V. Final Determination**

Based on the comprehensive review of the medical record, HealthFirst's internal criteria, California state law, and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice, the initial denial is **OVERTURNED**.

The medical necessity for the Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 (CPT 22551) for Mr. Marcus Webb is established due to the objective evidence of cervical myelopathy with moderate cord compression and myelomalacia, which, under California law (CIC §10169.5(a)(3) and DMHC APL 22-014), overrides HealthFirst’s requirements for a specific duration of conservative physical therapy or a strict "primary diagnosis" label. Delaying surgery in the presence of myelomalacia and established myelopathy is contrary to generally accepted standards of medical practice and state regulatory guidance, which prioritize timely intervention to prevent irreversible neurological deterioration.

**VI. Action Required**

HealthFirst Health Plan is hereby directed to **approve** the prior authorization for CPT 22551 (ACDF C6-C7, one level) for Mr. Marcus Webb.
