**Subject: Appeal Determination for Prior Authorization Denial for Revision ACDF C5-C6**

Dear Mr. Harris,

This letter serves as the formal determination regarding your appeal for prior authorization of CPT 22551, Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) for revision at C5-C6, initially denied by HealthFirst on October 30, 2024.

**I. Summary of Initial Denial**

On October 30, 2024, HealthFirst denied the request for CPT 22551, citing reason code MED-003. The denial stated: "Revision surgery not covered under standard ACDF criteria. Member had prior C5-C6 ACDF in 2021. Revision criteria require documentation of hardware failure, pseudarthrosis, or new pathology at previously treated level. Documentation submitted does not confirm hardware failure." The initial denial was issued by Dr. Patricia Huang, MD.

**II. Summary of Appeal Basis**

Your appeal, submitted by Dr. Karen Williams, MD, asserted that the denial was incorrect, providing the following basis:
*   CT scan confirms pseudarthrosis (failure of fusion) at C5-C6.
*   Symptoms have returned and worsened since the 2021 fusion.
*   Revision criteria are met per the treating surgeon.

**III. Documents Reviewed**

The following documents were reviewed in making this determination:
*   HealthFirst PPO Gold Plan benefits and coverage information.
*   HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria, Document CC-SPINE-2024.
*   California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31(b), §1374.32(b).
*   California Insurance Code (CIC) §10169.5.
*   DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014.
*   Clinical Reference Summary: AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023), NASS Guidelines (2020), AO Spine International Clinical Practice Guideline (2021), Cochrane Review (2021).
*   Member's clinical record, including presenting diagnosis, prior surgical history, imaging reports (MRI 2024-09-01, CT 2024-09-15), neurological exam findings (2024-09-20), and conservative treatment summaries (physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, interventional procedures).

**IV. Findings of Appeal Review**

Our comprehensive review, following the established evaluation plan, found the following:

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy Check of Denial Notice (CHSC §1374.31(b))**
The denial notice dated October 30, 2024, referenced "Revision criteria" requiring documentation of hardware failure, pseudarthrosis, or new pathology. Upon review of HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, these "Revision criteria" are not explicitly detailed or published as a distinct section within the plan's official policy document. While specific criteria were stated in the denial, their absence from the published policy raises concerns about the procedural adequacy of the denial.

**Step 2: Evaluation of Plan's Application of its Own Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024)**
*   **2a. Presence of "Revision criteria" in CC-SPINE-2024:** As noted in Step 1, HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 does not contain a specific section or clause outlining "Revision Surgery" criteria as cited in the denial (i.e., documentation of hardware failure, pseudarthrosis, or new pathology). This indicates the plan may have applied unpublished or unstated criteria.
*   **2b. Member's condition meeting general medical necessity criteria (Sections 2.1-2.4):** Despite the absence of specific revision criteria, we evaluated the request against the general ACDF criteria within HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024:
    *   **2.1 Diagnosis Requirement:** The member has a confirmed diagnosis of pseudarthrosis C5-C6 following prior ACDF, presenting with recurrent right C6 radiculopathy (bicep weakness 4/5, diminished reflex, C6 dermatomal numbness). This meets criteria 2.1(a) Cervical Radiculopathy and/or 2.1(c) Cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit.
    *   **2.2 Conservative Treatment Requirement:** The member completed 8 weeks of supervised physical therapy (exceeding the 6-week minimum), trialed Naproxen for 4 months and Gabapentin for 3 months (meeting the 2 agents, 4-week minimum), and received a cervical epidural steroid injection. The conservative treatment requirements under Section 2.2 are fully met. The Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A) is not applicable as the primary symptoms are radicular, not myelopathic.
    *   **2.3 Imaging Requirement:** A CT scan dated 2024-09-15, without artifact limitation, demonstrated "incomplete bridging bone formation" and "lucency around hardware suggesting pseudarthrosis" at C5-C6, consistent with failed fusion. This meets the imaging requirement.
    *   **2.4 Physician Documentation:** The submitted clinical record provides sufficient physician documentation to support the request.

Therefore, the member's condition fully satisfies all relevant sections of HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 based on the general criteria for ACDF, considering the structural pathology and recurrent radiculopathy.

**Step 3: Evaluation against State Law Overrides (DMHC APL 22-014, CIC §10169.5, CHSC §1374.32(b))**
*   **3a. Waiver of conservative treatment under DMHC APL 22-014:** The member's condition, while requiring revision, does not primarily present with objective evidence of myelomalacia or progressive herniation as specifically addressed by APL 22-014 for waiving *standard* conservative treatment. However, the conservative treatment requirements *were* met, making this specific waiver unnecessary.
*   **3b. Waiver of physical therapy requirements under CIC §10169.5:** The member completed 8 weeks of physical therapy with documented "no improvement." The treating physician notes that PT cannot address the structural hardware/fusion failure. Furthermore, the CT scan confirms pseudarthrosis at C5-C6, which is "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." Therefore, the conditions for waiving PT requirements under CIC §10169.5(a)(1) and (3) are met, affirming that further PT was not beneficial or medically appropriate.
*   **3c. Medical necessity under generally accepted standards per CHSC §1374.32(b):** Symptomatic pseudarthrosis following spinal fusion, as confirmed by the CT scan and clinical presentation of recurrent radiculopathy not responsive to conservative measures, is a recognized and medically necessary indication for revision surgical intervention under generally accepted standards of medical practice. This overrides any more restrictive or unstated plan criteria.

**Step 4: Evaluation against Clinical Standard Overrides (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021)**
*   **4a. Surgical indication per AANS/CNS and AO Spine guidelines:** The member's diagnosis of symptomatic pseudarthrosis at C5-C6 with objective neurological deficits (recurrent right C6 radiculopathy and weakness) after an adequate trial of conservative management constitutes a strong surgical indication (TIER 2 per AANS/CNS guidelines: "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" and "Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care").
*   **4b. Inconsistency of denial with clinical standard:** The plan's denial stated "Documentation submitted does not confirm hardware failure." However, the denial itself listed "pseudarthrosis" as a revision criterion, and the CT scan clearly confirms "pseudarthrosis." By focusing solely on hardware failure and overlooking the documented pseudarthrosis (which is a valid indication per the plan's *own stated criteria* in the denial and clinical standards), the plan's denial is inconsistent with both its own implied criteria and generally accepted clinical standards. Revision surgery for symptomatic pseudarthrosis is a cornerstone of spine care.

**V. Conclusion and Determination**

Based on a thorough review of the submitted appeal, the member's clinical record, HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria, applicable state laws, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, the initial denial for CPT 22551 is **OVERTURNED**.

Our review found that:
1.  The HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 policy does not explicitly publish "Revision criteria" as cited in the denial, creating a procedural inadequacy in the initial denial notice.
2.  The member's condition of symptomatic pseudarthrosis with objective radiculopathy meets HealthFirst's *general* medical necessity criteria for ACDF (Sections 2.1-2.4), including all conservative treatment requirements.
3.  California law (CIC §10169.5) permits waiver of physical therapy requirements when a structural neurological compromise (pseudarthrosis) exists that PT cannot address, or when a functional plateau has been reached, both of which are present in this case.
4.  The requested revision ACDF for symptomatic pseudarthrosis is medically necessary and in accordance with generally accepted standards of medical practice (CHSC §1374.32(b), AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine guidelines). The plan's denial, by focusing exclusively on hardware failure and ignoring the confirmed pseudarthrosis, misapplied even its own stated criteria and is inconsistent with clinical standards.

For these reasons, the requested procedure, CPT 22551, is determined to be medically necessary.
