**I. BACKGROUND**
Steven Clark, a 46-year-old member of HealthFirst PPO Gold, sought prior authorization for an ACDF at C5-C6. **II. EVALUATION PLAN EXECUTION**

This appeal was evaluated rigorously following the provided numbered evaluation plan, adhering strictly to its sequential steps and the Hierarchy of Authority for conflict resolution (State law > Clinical standard > Plan criteria).

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy Check of Initial Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b))**

*   **Objective:** To determine if the initial denial notice was procedurally adequate according to California law, specifically requiring the notice to explicitly cite the clinical criteria applied and clearly articulate the specific clinical reason for the denial.
*   **Case Details:** The initial denial notice (dated 2024-10-10) stated the reason as "Conflicting physical therapy documentation... Total verifiable: uncertain. Plan requires verification of claimed PT duration before approval."
*   **Finding:** While the denial notice did articulate a specific reason for the denial relating to documentation, it **failed to explicitly cite the specific section of HealthFirst's clinical criteria (e.g., HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) or 2.2(A))** that was applied to Steven Clark's case.
*   **Conclusion for Step 1:** This absence of an explicit citation of the governing clinical criteria constitutes a **Negative Finding** under Step 1 of the evaluation plan. California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b) mandates that a denial notice must specify the clinical criteria applied. The evaluation plan directs that if Step 1 results in a Negative Finding, the process must proceed directly to a **REMAND** disposition.

---

**III. DISPOSITION**

Based on the mandatory execution of Step 1 of the evaluation plan:

**DISPOSITION: REMAND**

**Reasoning:**
The initial denial notice issued by HealthFirst on October 10, 2024, is procedurally defective. It failed to explicitly cite the specific clinical criteria from HealthFirst's own policy (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024) that formed the basis for denying the prior authorization request for ACDF C5-C6. This non-compliance with the requirement to specify clinical criteria renders the denial procedurally inadequate, as stipulated by California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b).

Per the instructions of the evaluation plan, a negative finding in Step 1 necessitates a direct remand, prior to any substantive review of the clinical merits or the member's appeal. This ensures that the member receives a transparent and legally compliant explanation for any denial, safeguarding their appeal rights.

**Action Required:**
HealthFirst Health Plan must **re-issue a procedurally compliant denial notice** to Steven Clark. This re-issued notice must explicitly cite the specific clinical criteria applied (e.g., HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) or 2.2(A)) and clearly articulate the specific clinical reason for any denial. The plan must also ensure the notice includes comprehensive information regarding the member's Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights, as required by state law.

Upon re-evaluation, HealthFirst should consider all submitted documentation, including the complete Coastal Physical Therapy records provided with this appeal, to determine if the conservative treatment requirements have been met. This remand addresses the procedural defect of the initial denial and does not prejudice any future determination of medical necessity.
