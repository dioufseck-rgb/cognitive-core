**II. Evaluation Process:**

This appeal was reviewed in accordance with the HealthFirst PPO Gold evaluation plan, applying the established hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria) for conflict resolution. The following steps were executed:

**Step 1: Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice (CHSC §1374.31(b))**
*   **Finding:** Negative (Denial is Procedurally Defective). The initial denial notice of 2024-10-05 was found to be procedurally defective in violation of California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) §1374.31(b). Specifically:
    *   The notice failed to specify the exact clinical criteria applied, only providing a generic reference to "plan criteria" without section numbers or specific criteria.
    *   The notice failed to include a notice of your right to Independent Medical Review (IMR).
*   **Rationale:** CHSC §1374.31(b) explicitly states that a denial notice failing to specify the clinical criteria applied or the specific clinical reason for denial is defective. The absence of an IMR notice is also a procedural violation. These procedural flaws prevent the denial from standing as issued.

**Step 2: Confirm Primary Diagnosis and Clinical Presentation**
*   **Finding:** Negative (Radiculopathy without Myelomalacia or Spinal Cord Compression). The clinical record, including the presenting diagnosis ("Cervical radiculopathy C6-C7"), MRI findings ("C6-C7 disc herniation, moderate right C7 nerve root compression. No cord contact. No myelomalacia."), and neurological exam findings (right C7 dermatomal numbness, tricep weakness 4/5 right, tricep reflex diminished) are consistent with cervical radiculopathy. There is no evidence of spinal cord compression or myelomalacia, which would indicate cervical myelopathy.
*   **Rationale:** This distinction is critical as cervical radiculopathy and cervical myelopathy have different treatment pathways and urgency considerations under both plan criteria and generally accepted medical standards.

**Step 3: Review Plan's Application of Its Own Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024)**
*   **Finding:** Negative (Plan Criteria Not Met for Radiculopathy). Based on the diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy, the standard conservative treatment requirements outlined in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, are applicable (the Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A) does not apply here due to the absence of myelopathy and myelomalacia).
    *   **Physical Therapy:** The plan requires a minimum of 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy. Your record shows 4 weeks completed. **(Requirement Not Met)**
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** The plan requires a trial of at least two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each. Your record shows 6 weeks of Ibuprofen 800mg TID (one agent). **(Requirement Not Met)**
    *   **Interventional Treatments:** The plan requires one interventional treatment. Your record indicates no interventional treatments were performed. **(Requirement Not Met)**
*   **Rationale:** The documentation indicates that the conservative treatment requirements specified in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, for cervical radiculopathy have not been fully met.

**Step 4: Review Against State Law Overrides (CIC §10169.5, DMHC APL 22-014)**
*   **Finding:** Negative (State Law Overrides Do Not Apply to This Specific Scenario).
    *   **CIC §10169.5 (Physical Therapy):** This section allows waiver of PT requirements if the physician documents PT is contraindicated, unlikely to benefit, a functional plateau has been reached, or there's objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that PT cannot address (e.g., myelomalacia with cord compression).
        *   The record does not indicate physician documentation that PT is contraindicated or unlikely to benefit.
        *   While you claim a functional plateau, the PT discharge note does not use that language, and the clinical standard definition requires specific criteria (e.g., <10% improvement over 3 sessions or therapist documentation of maximum benefit) which are not clearly met.
        *   The critical condition of "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address" (such as myelomalacia with cord compression) is explicitly absent from your MRI findings.
    *   **DMHC APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):** This All-Plan Letter specifically addresses prior authorization for time-sensitive neurological conditions, particularly cervical myelopathy with objective evidence of cord compression and myelomalacia. Since your diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy and myelomalacia is explicitly absent, this state guidance does not apply to override the conservative treatment requirements in your case.
*   **Rationale:** The specific conditions for state law overrides related to conservative treatment (especially for PT or urgent neurological conditions) are not met in your clinical presentation.

**Step 5: Review Against Clinical Standards (Generally Accepted Medical Practice)**
*   **Finding:** Negative (Does Not Fully Meet Clinical Standards for Immediate Surgical Intervention for Radiculopathy). Generally accepted standards of medical practice (e.g., AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine) for cervical *radiculopathy* typically recommend a robust trial of conservative management, often lasting 6-12 weeks, prior to considering surgical intervention as a "reasonable option" (Tier 2 indication). For radiculopathy, physical therapy, pharmacotherapy (typically multiple agents), and interventional injections are considered standard conservative care.
*   **Rationale:** While you have objective findings consistent with radiculopathy, the documented conservative treatment (4 weeks PT, one pharmacotherapy, no interventional procedures) falls short of the duration and breadth generally recommended before proceeding to surgery for radiculopathy. The arguments for surgical urgency and waiver of conservative treatment based on myelomalacia are specific to myelopathy and do not apply here.

---

**III. Conflict Resolution and Rationale for Disposition:**

This appeal presents a scenario where the initial denial was procedurally defective (a violation of State Law, which holds the highest authority), yet the clinical documentation, when reviewed against both the plan's criteria and generally accepted clinical standards for radiculopathy, does not fully support an immediate approval of surgery.

*   The procedural flaws in the initial denial, specifically the failure to cite precise criteria and the omission of IMR rights (CHSC §1374.31(b)), mean the denial cannot stand. This necessitates reversing the original denial.
*   However, a direct "OVERTURN" to approve the requested ACDF is not supported by the current clinical documentation, as the conservative treatment for cervical radiculopathy appears incomplete according to both HealthFirst's standard criteria (Section 2.2) and generally accepted standards of medical practice. State law overrides specific to urgent neurological conditions or functional plateaus in PT are also not applicable here.

Therefore, the appropriate disposition is **REMAND**. This allows HealthFirst to issue a new, procedurally compliant determination for your requested service. If a new denial is issued, it must clearly articulate the specific clinical criteria, the precise reasons for the denial, and your full appeal rights, including the right to IMR, as required by California law. This will allow you to understand the basis of any denial and pursue further appeal rights if you choose.

---

**IV. Determination:**

The appeal for prior authorization of CPT code 22551 (ACDF C6-C7, one level) for Sandra White (M-334466) is **REMANDED**.
