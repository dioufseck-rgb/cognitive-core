**INTRODUCTION:**
This letter serves as the formal determination of the appeal for prior authorization request PA-2024-C001, for Ms. Frances Kim's requested anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6. This review was conducted in accordance with HealthFirst Health Plan's appeal policies and all applicable California state laws and regulations.

**SUMMARY OF INITIAL DENIAL:**
HealthFirst initially denied the request for ACDF C5-C6 on 2024-10-30, citing reason code MED-003. The denial stated that the "Myelopathy exception does not apply. Imaging shows mild cord contact only — no cord compression, no cord signal change. Upper motor neuron signs on exam are not conclusive for myelopathy in the absence of imaging confirmation. Standard 6-week PT requirement applies; member completed 5 weeks." The initial denial was issued by Dr. Alan Foster, MD.

**SUMMARY OF APPEAL BASIS:**
Ms. Kim's appeal asserts the following:
1.  Member has three positive upper motor neuron (UMN) signs — clinical myelopathy is established regardless of imaging.
2.  Mild cord contact on MRI plus UMN signs = myelopathy per clinical definition.
3.  5 weeks PT completed under myelopathy pathway (4 weeks required).

**FINDINGS OF THE APPEAL REVIEW:**

The review was conducted following the hierarchy of authority: State law > Clinical standard > Plan criteria.

**STEP 1: Procedural Adequacy of the Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b))**
The initial denial notice stated a specific reason ("Myelopathy exception does not apply. Imaging shows mild cord contact only... UMN signs on exam are not conclusive... Standard 6-week PT requirement applies...") and clearly indicated the specific plan criterion (PT duration) and the clinical rationale for the denial. While not explicitly citing "CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(A)", the specific clinical reasons provided and criteria referenced (myelopathy exception, 6-week PT) were sufficiently clear to comply with CHSC § 1374.31(b).
*   **Finding:** The denial notice is deemed procedurally adequate.

**STEP 2: Confirmation of Diagnosis: Myelopathy vs. Radiculopathy (Domain Knowledge; Plan Criteria 2.1(b); Clinical Standard Section 1.1)**
The initial denial asserted that the "Myelopathy exception does not apply" because "Upper motor neuron signs on exam are not conclusive for myelopathy in the absence of imaging confirmation."
*   **Clinical Record Review:**
    *   **Imaging (MRI 2024-09-01):** Confirms "C5-C6 disc herniation. Mild cord contact. No measurable cord compression. No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia." This supports the denial's imaging assessment of no definitive cord compression or myelomalacia.
    *   **Neurological Exam (2024-09-05):** Objectively documents "Positive Hoffman bilateral. Hyperreflexia bilateral upper extremities. Sustained clonus at bilateral ankles (3 beats). Finger escape sign positive. Gait: mildly wide-based."
    *   **Clinical Standard Note (embedded in Clinical Record):** Explicitly states, "Clinical myelopathy diagnosis requires UMN signs on examination. Imaging confirmation supports but is not required for diagnosis. Imaging-negative myelopathy is a recognized entity where clinical signs precede MRI changes. Multiple positive UMN signs in the absence of other explanation = clinical myelopathy."
*   **Analysis:** While the MRI did not show measurable cord compression or myelomalacia, the neurological exam unequivocally demonstrates multiple objective upper motor neuron (UMN) signs (bilateral Hoffman, hyperreflexia, clonus, gait disturbance) consistent with cervical myelopathy. Per the AANS/CNS clinical standard (recognized in the Clinical Record and supported by Clinical Standard Section 1.1), clinical myelopathy can be diagnosed based on UMN signs even in the absence of overt imaging-confirmed cord compression or myelomalacia ("imaging-negative myelopathy"). The prior reviewer's assertion that UMN signs are "not conclusive... in the absence of imaging confirmation" is directly contradicted by established clinical standards.
*   **Finding:** Ms. Kim has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical myelopathy based on objective clinical findings, consistent with accepted clinical standards, despite the absence of definitive imaging changes.

**STEP 3: Application of Plan's Internal Myelopathy Exception (Plan Criteria 2.2(A))**
*   **Plan Criteria 2.2(A) (Myelopathy Pathway):** States that for confirmed cervical myelopathy (criterion 2.1(b)), the physical therapy requirement is reduced to a "4 weeks minimum."
*   **Clinical Record Review:** The conservative treatment summary confirms Ms. Kim "completed 5 weeks" of physical therapy.
*   **Analysis:** Since myelopathy is confirmed (from Step 2), the plan's own myelopathy exception (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A)) should have been applied. Ms. Kim completed 5 weeks of PT, which exceeds the 4-week minimum required by the plan's exception. Therefore, the denial based on failure to meet the standard 6-week PT requirement was incorrect under HealthFirst's own criteria.
*   **Finding:** HealthFirst's own myelopathy exception (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A)) was incorrectly not applied, and its conditions for PT duration were met by the member.

**STEP 4: State Law Override — Physical Therapy Requirement (CIC § 10169.5; DMHC APL 22-014)**
*   **CIC § 10169.5(a)(2):** A health insurer shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where "the member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
*   **Clinical Record Review:** The physical therapy summary states, "5 weeks PT under myelopathy protocol. Neurological signs unchanged. Physician halted PT citing myelopathy concern."
*   **Analysis:** After 5 weeks of PT, Ms. Kim's neurological signs remained unchanged, and her physician halted therapy due to myelopathy concern. This documentation strongly indicates that Ms. Kim reached a functional plateau for the myelopathic condition, as neurological symptoms of myelopathy are not expected to respond to physical therapy (Clinical Standard Section 5). Per CIC § 10169.5(a)(2), this mandates a waiver of any remaining PT requirement. While CIC § 10169.5(a)(3) (objective structural neurological compromise) and DMHC APL 22-014 (myelomalacia/progressive pathology) are not directly triggered by the imaging findings, the functional plateau provision of state law applies.
*   **Finding:** California state law (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)) mandates waiving the physical therapy requirement as the member reached a functional plateau, as documented by her treating physician.

**STEP 5: Clinical Standard Override (CHSC § 1374.32(b); DMHC APL 22-014; Clinical Standard Sections 2 & 3)**
*   **CHSC § 1374.32(b):** States that if a service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice, it "SHALL be approved regardless of whether the plan's internal criteria are more restrictive."
*   **Clinical Standard Section 2.1 (AO Spine, NASS):** "There is no high-quality evidence supporting a mandatory course of physical therapy prior to surgical intervention for patients with moderate to severe cervical myelopathy. Delaying surgery in such patients to complete a physical therapy trial is not supported by the evidence and may result in neurological deterioration." It further clarifies that applying radiculopathy-derived PT requirements to myelopathy patients is a "category error."
*   **Clinical Standard Section 3.1 (AANS/CNS):** Indicates that myelopathy with objective neurological deficits and persistent symptoms after an adequate conservative trial constitutes a "TIER 1 — STRONG INDICATION" or "TIER 2 — REASONABLE INDICATION" for surgery.
*   **Analysis:** As established in Step 2, Ms. Kim has clinically confirmed cervical myelopathy with multiple UMN signs. Clinical standards (AO Spine, NASS, AANS/CNS) clearly indicate that a mandatory 6-week (or even 4-week) physical therapy course for established myelopathy is not evidence-based and may be detrimental due to the risk of neurological deterioration (Clinical Standard Section 2.3). The plan's requirement for a specific duration of PT in this myelopathic context is more restrictive than generally accepted standards of medical practice.
*   **Finding:** Generally accepted clinical standards support surgical intervention for Ms. Kim's confirmed cervical myelopathy and explicitly state that mandatory physical therapy requirements for this condition are not evidence-based and may be detrimental. These clinical standards, per CHSC § 1374.32(b), override the plan's more restrictive criteria.

**APPEAL DETERMINATION:**
**OVERTURN**

**RATIONALE FOR DETERMINATION:**
The initial denial for ACDF C5-C6 for Ms. Frances Kim is hereby OVERTURNED. This decision is based on a comprehensive review of the clinical evidence and applicable state and clinical standards, which collectively override the original denial:

1.  **Clinical Diagnosis of Myelopathy Confirmed:** Despite imaging showing only mild cord contact, Ms. Kim presents with multiple unequivocal upper motor neuron signs (bilateral Hoffman, hyperreflexia, clonus, gait disturbance) which, according to AANS/CNS guidelines, establish a diagnosis of clinical myelopathy, even in the absence of imaging-confirmed cord compression or myelomalacia (AANS/CNS note in Clinical Record, Clinical Standard Section 1.1). The initial reviewer's assertion that UMN signs were "not conclusive for myelopathy in the absence of imaging confirmation" is contrary to accepted clinical standards.
2.  **Plan's Myelopathy Exception Met:** Once myelopathy is confirmed, HealthFirst's own Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(A)) reduce the physical therapy requirement to a 4-week minimum. Ms. Kim completed 5 weeks of physical therapy. Therefore, she met the plan's modified conservative treatment requirements for myelopathy, and the initial denial was incorrect based on the plan's own rules.
3.  **State Law Override on PT Requirement:** Ms. Kim's treating physician documented that her neurological signs were "unchanged" after 5 weeks of PT, and halted therapy due to "myelopathy concern." This constitutes documentation of reaching a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms, which, under California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2), mandates a waiver of any further physical therapy requirements.
4.  **Clinical Standard Override on PT Requirement:** Generally accepted clinical standards from organizations such as AO Spine, NASS, and AANS/CNS explicitly state that there is no high-quality evidence supporting a mandatory course of physical therapy prior to surgical intervention for patients with moderate to severe cervical myelopathy, and delaying surgery for such a trial may result in neurological deterioration (Clinical Standard Section 2.1). Therefore, HealthFirst's 6-week (or even 4-week) mandatory PT requirement for confirmed myelopathy is more restrictive than, and directly contradicted by, generally accepted standards of medical practice. Under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32(b), services meeting these accepted standards must be approved regardless of the plan's internal criteria.

Based on the hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria), the evidence strongly supports that Ms. Kim's condition meets the medical necessity criteria for surgery without requiring further physical therapy.
