**DISPOSITION: PARTIAL OVERTURN**

This letter constitutes the formal determination regarding the appeal of the prior authorization denial for an anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at three levels (C3-C4, C4-C5, C5-C6) for Ms. Catherine Mills. After a thorough review of the submitted clinical documentation, the initial denial rationale, the member's appeal basis, and application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted clinical standards, we have determined to **PARTIALLY OVERTURN** the initial denial.

**Specifically, authorization is APPROVED for ACDF at levels C4-C5 and C5-C6.**
**The denial for ACDF at level C3-C4 is UPHELD.**

---

**REVIEW FINDINGS:**

Our review was conducted in accordance with the Hierarchy of Authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria) and the provided Evaluation Plan.

1.  **Procedural Adequacy of Initial Denial Notice:**
    *   The initial denial notice dated 2024-11-25 explicitly stated the clinical reason for the denial: "Review of imaging and clinical record supports surgical intervention at one level (C4-C5) only. C3-C4 and C5-C6 do not demonstrate pathology of sufficient severity to warrant surgical intervention at this time." While it did not explicitly cite a specific plan criterion number, the reason provided was sufficiently specific regarding the clinical basis for the denial. Therefore, the initial denial is deemed procedurally adequate per **CHSC § 1374.31(b)**.

2.  **Diagnosis Classification:**
    *   The clinical record clearly establishes a primary diagnosis of cervical myelopathy at the C4-C5 level, evidenced by severe cord compression, T2 signal change (myelomalacia) on MRI (2024-10-01), and objective neurological findings including bilateral hyperreflexia, bilateral Hoffman sign, and wide-based gait on exam (2024-10-05). Additionally, there is evidence of cervical radiculopathy at the C5-C6 level with moderate left C6 nerve root compression and corresponding left bicep weakness. This meets **HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.1(b)** for myelopathy and 2.1(a) for radiculopathy. The presence of myelomalacia is a critical indicator of established cord injury. (**Clinical Standard 1.1, 1.2; DMHC APL 22-014 II.1**).

3.  **Pathology at Requested Levels:**
    *   **C4-C5:** MRI findings of "large disc herniation, severe cord compression, T2 signal change (myelomalacia)" combined with clinical myelopathic findings (bilateral hyperreflexia, Hoffman, gait disturbance, bilateral deltoid weakness) provide a **TIER 1 strong surgical indication** for ACDF at this level. This meets **HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.3** and **Clinical Standard 3.1(a, c, d)**.
    *   **C5-C6:** MRI findings of "moderate disc herniation, moderate left C6 nerve root compression, foraminal stenosis" with correlating "left bicep weakness 4-/5, bicep reflex diminished left" constitute a **TIER 2 reasonable surgical indication** for ACDF at this level. This meets **HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.3** and **Clinical Standard 3.1(b)**.
    *   **C3-C4:** MRI findings of "mild disc bulge, minimal foraminal narrowing" are noted. However, the neurological exam states, "C4 level: unclear — neck pain with C3-C4 palpation but no specific radicular pattern." While the overall myelopathy contributes to global symptoms, the objective imaging and specific clinical correlation for C3-C4 do not demonstrate pathology of sufficient severity to warrant surgical intervention at this level per generally accepted clinical standards. This aligns with **AANS/CNS TIER 3 guidelines** for imaging findings without correlating symptoms. Therefore, the medical necessity for surgery at C3-C4 is not supported.

4.  **Conservative Treatment Requirement & Exceptions:**
    *   **For C4-C5 (Myelopathy with Myelomalacia):**
        *   The member completed 9 weeks of physical therapy (27 sessions), exceeding the 4-week minimum specified in **HealthFirst Plan Criteria EXCEPTION 2.2(A)** for myelopathy. The PT note states, "Myelopathic symptoms stable but not improved," indicating a functional plateau for myelopathy.
        *   Two pharmacotherapy agents (Baclofen 3 months, Pregabalin 4 months) were used, meeting the Plan Criteria 2.2(b) requirement.
        *   One interventional treatment (epidural steroid injection) was performed, providing "modest improvement in radicular symptoms, no change in myelopathy signs."
        *   Furthermore, the presence of myelomalacia at C4-C5 constitutes "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address," which, per **CIC § 10169.5(a)(3)**, waives the requirement for completion of physical therapy. **DMHC APL 22-014 II.2** also specifically identifies myelomalacia as a finding that may support waiver of standard conservative treatment requirements.
        *   **Clinical Standard 2.1, 2.3, 3.2** from AANS/CNS and AO Spine guidelines indicates that delaying surgery for moderate-severe myelopathy with myelomalacia to complete a predefined course of physical therapy is not supported by evidence and may result in neurological deterioration.
        *   Therefore, conservative treatment requirements for C4-C5 are met and, if not, are overridden by state law and clinical standards.
    *   **For C5-C6 (Radiculopathy):**
        *   The member completed 9 weeks of physical therapy, exceeding the 6-week minimum required by **HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.2(a)**. The PT note indicates "C5-C6 radicular symptoms persist."
        *   Two pharmacotherapy agents were used for sufficient duration, meeting Plan Criteria 2.2(b).
        *   One interventional treatment (epidural) was performed, meeting Plan Criteria 2.2(c).
        *   Therefore, all standard conservative treatment requirements for radiculopathy at C5-C6 were met.

5.  **Physician Documentation Requirements:**
    *   The appeal basis confirms that Dr. Torres has documented reasoning for three-level fusion, attestation of conservative treatment completion, and that all three levels are symptomatic. The initial denial did not indicate any deficiencies in the treating physician's documentation per **HealthFirst Plan Criteria 2.4**.

---

**CONCLUSION:**

Based on the comprehensive review, the initial denial for ACDF at C4-C5 and C5-C6 is inconsistent with Ms. Mills' clinical presentation, the completed conservative treatment, HealthFirst Plan Criteria, California state regulations, and generally accepted clinical standards.

*   The C4-C5 level exhibits severe cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia and objective neurological deficits, meeting a TIER 1 strong surgical indication. Conservative treatment requirements were met, and regulatory/clinical standards would override any unmet requirements.
*   The C5-C6 level exhibits cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits and persistent symptoms after a complete course of conservative treatment, meeting a TIER 2 reasonable surgical indication.
*   The C3-C4 level, however, lacks sufficient objective pathology and correlating clinical symptoms to justify surgical intervention per generally accepted clinical standards.

Therefore, the decision is to **PARTIALLY OVERTURN** the denial. ACDF is approved for C4-C5 and C5-C6, and the denial is upheld for C3-C4.
