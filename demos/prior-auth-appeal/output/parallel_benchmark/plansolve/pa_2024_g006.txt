**Re: Appeal Determination for Prior Authorization Denial of ACDF C5-C6 (CPT 22551)**

Dear Ms. Kamau,

This letter serves as the formal determination of your appeal regarding the prior authorization denial for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6, requested by Dr. Victor Osei, MD, to be performed at UCSF Medical Center.

Your original request for CPT code 22551 was denied on 2024-11-20 by Dr. Alan Foster, MD, HealthFirst Utilization Management. The stated reason for denial was: "Request denied. Member has a prior authorization denial history for cervical spine surgery (three-level fusion denied May 2024). Review of current request in context of prior denial pattern suggests the member's cervical pathology has not materially changed since the prior denial. Current request does not demonstrate sufficient change in clinical status to warrant reconsideration."

You appealed this denial, asserting that:
1.  The prior denial was for a three-level fusion, a different procedure than the current single-level request.
2.  Your clinical status has materially changed since May 2024, supported by a new MRI and a new conservative treatment course.
3.  The current request meets all clinical criteria independently of any prior authorization history.
4.  Using prior PA history as a denial basis is not authorized under HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria (CC-SPINE-2024).

We have thoroughly reviewed your appeal, the submitted clinical documentation, HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), relevant California state laws (California Health & Safety Code (CHSC), California Insurance Code (CIC)), Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) All-Plan Letters, and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice. Our review adheres to the hierarchy of authority: **State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.**

---

**EVALUATION FINDINGS:**

**1. Procedural Adequacy of Denial Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b))**
    *   **Finding:** Negative. The original denial notice, while providing a reason, failed to specify clinical criteria applied from HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024. Instead, it cited "Prior PA history" as the basis. California Health & Safety Code § 1374.31(b) requires that a denial notice specify the *clinical criteria applied* and the *specific clinical reason* for the denial. The absence of a valid, cited clinical criterion from the plan's policy renders the denial procedurally defective under state law.
    *   **Citation:** CHSC § 1374.31(b).

**2. Validity of the Denial Basis as Applied by the Plan (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024; CHSC § 1374.32(b))**
    *   **Finding:** Negative. The stated reason for denial, "Prior PA history" and an assertion of "no material change since the prior denial" based on this history, is *not* a valid criterion or exclusion explicitly authorized within HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 document. Furthermore, HealthFirst's internal reviewer acknowledged this, noting the denial basis as "Prior PA history — NOT a valid criterion under CC-SPINE-2024." Under state law, a plan's internal criteria cannot be more restrictive than generally accepted standards of medical practice, and criteria must be valid and applied appropriately.
    *   **Citation:** HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 (absence of criteria), CHSC § 1374.32(b) (standard of review).

**3. Determination of Primary Diagnosis and Supporting Evidence (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1; Domain Knowledge)**
    *   **Finding:** Negative (for myelopathy). The medical records primarily support a diagnosis of **cervical radiculopathy C5-C6** (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1(a)) with an objective neurological deficit.
        *   The presenting diagnosis is "Cervical radiculopathy C5-C6, progressive, persistent after conservative treatment."
        *   Neurological exam findings (right C6 dermatomal numbness, absent right bicep reflex, right bicep weakness 3+/5) are consistent with C6 radiculopathy.
        *   The current MRI (October 2024) shows "C5-C6 large disc herniation with moderate-severe right C6 nerve root compression" and "New foraminal stenosis at C5-C6."
        *   There is no documentation of myelopathic symptoms such as gait disturbance, bilateral upper extremity weakness, hyperreflexia, positive Hoffman sign, or myelomalacia on MRI, which would indicate cervical myelopathy as the primary diagnosis per HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1(b) and generally accepted clinical definitions.
    *   **Citation:** HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1(a) and (b), Domain Knowledge (Myelopathy vs. Radiculopathy Distinction).

**4. Review of Conservative Treatment Requirements and Applicable Exceptions/Overrides (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2; CIC § 10169.5)**
    *   **Finding:** Positive. The member has satisfied the standard conservative treatment requirements for cervical radiculopathy as outlined in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, and additionally, state law overrides apply.
        *   **Physical Therapy:** An 8-week course of supervised PT was completed from July-September 2024, exceeding the plan's 6-week minimum. The documentation explicitly states a "functional plateau" was reached, with "no further improvement expected, neurological deficit persistent and worsening."
        *   **Pharmacotherapy:** The member completed trials of Gabapentin (6 months) and two courses of Methylprednisolone, meeting the plan's requirement of two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each.
        *   **Interventional Treatment:** A right C6 epidural was performed on 2024-11-05, providing only 50% relief for 2 weeks before symptoms returned to baseline, satisfying the plan's requirement for one interventional procedure.
    *   **State Law Overrides:** Even if HealthFirst's criteria were interpreted more restrictively, California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 would override. The treating physician documented that the member reached a functional plateau (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)). Furthermore, the condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise (large disc herniation with moderate-severe nerve root compression, worsening C6 weakness) that physical therapy cannot address (CIC § 10169.5(a)(3)).
    *   **Citation:** HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, CIC § 10169.5(a)(2) and (a)(3).

**5. Assessment Against Generally Accepted Clinical Standards (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020; CHSC § 1374.32(b))**
    *   **Finding:** Positive. The requested ACDF C5-C6 is medically necessary and strongly indicated under generally accepted standards of medical practice.
        *   The member presents with persistent and progressive cervical radiculopathy C5-C6, characterized by severe pain (8/10), objective neurological deficits (C6 weakness 3+/5, absent bicep reflex), and significant functional impairment ("Unable to continue working full-time").
        *   Imaging (October 2024 MRI) confirms a "large disc herniation with moderate-severe right C6 nerve root compression" and notes "interval increase in size" and "new foraminal stenosis," indicating progressive pathology.
        *   All conservative treatment options, as documented in the medical record, have been exhausted and failed to provide lasting relief or arrest the progression of neurological deficits.
        *   According to the AANS/CNS Joint Section on Disorders of the Spine (2023 Update) and NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020), surgery is a **Tier 2 Reasonable Indication** for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after an adequate (6-12 week) conservative trial, particularly when accompanied by progressive neurological deficit and significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care. This aligns directly with Ms. Kamau's clinical presentation.
        *   CHSC § 1374.32(b) mandates that if a service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice, it shall be approved regardless of whether the plan's internal criteria are more restrictive.
    *   **Citation:** AANS/CNS Joint Section on Disorders of the Spine: Guidelines for Management of Degenerative Cervical Myelopathy (2023 Update) Section 3.1 Tier 2; NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020); CHSC § 1374.32(b).

**DISPOSITION DECISION:** **OVERTURN**

**Rationale for Overturn:**
Based on the comprehensive review and the findings above, the initial denial is **OVERTURNED**.

The original denial was procedurally defective and based on an invalid criterion. Specifically:
1.  **Procedural Defect:** The denial notice failed to cite specific clinical criteria from HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, a violation of California Health & Safety Code § 1374.31(b).
2.  **Invalid Denial Basis:** The use of "Prior PA history" as a basis for denial is not a valid criterion within HealthFirst's own CC-SPINE-2024 policy, a fact even acknowledged by HealthFirst's internal reviewer. This constitutes an inappropriate application of criteria.

Furthermore, a thorough clinical review demonstrates that the requested ACDF C5-C6 is medically necessary:
1.  **Conservative Treatment Met:** Ms. Kamau has completed and failed a comprehensive course of conservative treatment that meets (and exceeds) HealthFirst's requirements for cervical radiculopathy.
2.  **State Law Overrides:** California Insurance Code § 10169.5 provides specific overrides for physical therapy requirements when a functional plateau is reached or when objective structural neurological compromise is present, both of which apply in this case.
3.  **Clinical Standards Met:** The procedure is well-supported by generally accepted standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS, NASS) for persistent and progressive cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits and functional impairment refractory to conservative management. Under California law (CHSC § 1374.32(b)), services meeting these standards must be approved.

Therefore, the requested Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 (CPT 22551) for Ms. Theresa Kamau is deemed medically necessary.
