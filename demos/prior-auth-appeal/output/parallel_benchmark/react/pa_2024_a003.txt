### APPEAL REVIEW

**1. Clinical Summary & Presenting Condition:**
Patricia Osei is a 55-year-old member diagnosed with **progressive cervical myelopathy C4-C5 with myelomalacia**. Her neurological exam on 2024-08-25 revealed bilateral lower extremity spasticity, hyperreflexia, impaired fine motor function in both hands, and a positive Lhermitte sign \u2014 all consistent with significant upper motor neuron involvement. Recent MRI (2024-08-20), performed with a pacemaker-compatible protocol, confirmed severe central cord compression at C4-C5 with T2 signal change (myelomalacia), indicating progressive cord injury. The member has a permanent pacemaker (implanted 2019).

**2. Review of Conservative Treatment History:**
*   **Physical Therapy:** 5 weeks (15 sessions) completed. Therapy was stopped due to pacemaker precautions limiting certain modalities and the physician's determination that further PT was not addressing the neurological progression.
*   **Pharmacotherapy:** A trial of Baclofen 10mg TID for 3 months (partial spasticity relief) and two courses of Methylprednisolone taper (temporary improvement only).
*   **Interventional Treatment:** No cervical epidural steroid injection or other interventional procedure was performed because it is **medically contraindicated** due to the member's permanent pacemaker. This contraindication was confirmed by an anesthesiology consultation (Dr. Rivera, 2024-09-10) and documented in the member's chart.

**3. Application of Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024):**
The initial denial stated the member did not meet Section 2.2 requirements. However, a comprehensive review of the HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 policy reveals the following:

*   **Section 2.1 (Diagnosis Requirement):** The member clearly meets criterion 2.1(b) for "Cervical myelopathy \u2014 spinal cord compression with myelopathic symptoms." The MRI confirms structural pathology consistent with symptoms. **MET.**
*   **Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment Requirement):**
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** The member completed a trial of two agents (Baclofen and Methylprednisolone), meeting Section 2.2(b). **MET.**
    *   **Myelopathy Exception (Section 2.2(A)):** This critical exception applies directly to Patricia Osei's case, as cervical myelopathy is her PRIMARY diagnosis. Under this exception:
        *   **Physical Therapy:** The requirement is reduced to a "4 weeks minimum." The member completed 5 weeks of physical therapy. **MET.**
        *   **Interventional Treatment:** This requirement "is WAIVED if treating physician documents that interventional treatment poses risk of neurological deterioration." The member's permanent pacemaker renders fluoroscopy-guided cervical epidural steroid injections medically contraindicated, as documented by the anesthesiologist and the treating physician. This clearly represents a risk. **WAIVED.**

**Conclusion on Plan Criteria:** The initial denial failed to correctly apply HealthFirst's own Section 2.2(A) Myelopathy Exception. When this exception is properly applied, Patricia Osei **meets all conservative treatment requirements** within the HealthFirst policy.

**4. Application of California Regulatory Framework:**
The denial also conflicts with California state law designed to protect members with neurological compromise:

*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):** This guidance explicitly states that plans "SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment" when objective imaging (like myelomalacia and progressive cord injury) demonstrates progressive pathology, the physician documents conservative treatment failure, and surgery is indicated by generally accepted guidelines. The presence of myelomalacia specifically supports waiver of standard conservative requirements. Patricia Osei's condition with progressive cervical myelopathy and myelomalacia falls squarely under this provision, requiring that her case be treated as urgent and standard conservative treatment requirements be waived.
*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):** This statute prohibits conditioning surgical approval on PT completion where:
    *   (a)(1) "The member's treating physician has documented... physical therapy... unlikely to benefit the member given the nature and severity of the condition." (Physician documented PT "not addressing neurological progression.")
    *   (a)(2) "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau." (5 weeks completed, physician determination supports this.)
    *   (a)(3) "The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." (Progressive cervical myelopathy, severe cord compression, and myelomalacia definitively meet this criterion.)
    This law overrides HealthFirst's mechanical 6-week PT requirement for this member's condition.
*   **California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32(b):** This provision clarifies that for Independent Medical Review (IMR), medical necessity is determined by "generally accepted standards of medical practice," not necessarily the plan's internal criteria if those criteria are more restrictive. This establishes that generally accepted clinical standards take precedence over restrictive plan criteria.

**5. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**
Generally accepted clinical standards strongly support surgical intervention for Patricia Osei's condition:

*   **Diagnosis & Urgency:** Cervical myelopathy is distinct from radiculopathy and has limited efficacy for conservative treatment, especially when moderate-to-severe. **Myelomalacia (T2 signal change) indicates existing spinal cord injury, a higher risk of permanent deficits if treatment is delayed, and is considered a strong indication for surgical intervention and a contraindication to extended conservative treatment trials.**
*   **Physical Therapy for Myelopathy:** The AO Spine Guidelines (2021) and NASS Guidelines (2020) state there is *no high-quality evidence* supporting a mandatory course of physical therapy prior to surgery for moderate to severe cervical myelopathy. They explicitly warn that **delaying surgery in such patients to complete a physical therapy trial is not supported by evidence and may result in neurological deterioration.** The 6-week PT requirement is derived from radiculopathy evidence, not myelopathy.
*   **Surgical Indication:** Patricia Osei's condition (moderate-severe myelopathy with objective cord compression, progressive neurological deficit, and myelomalacia) meets the **TIER 1 \u2014 STRONG INDICATION** for surgery per the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023).

**6. Conflict Resolution and Hierarchy Application (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria):**

*   The initial denial's reasoning is invalid under HealthFirst's *own* criteria when the appropriate myelopathy exception (Section 2.2(A)) is applied.
*   Furthermore, California state law (DMHC APL 22-014 and CIC § 10169.5) explicitly overrides any plan criteria that would mechanically enforce conservative treatment requirements in the presence of progressive cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia.
*   Finally, generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice unequivocally support surgical intervention for this condition and deem mandatory extended conservative trials unsupported by evidence and potentially harmful.

All three sources in the hierarchy (Plan Criteria - correctly applied, State Law, and Clinical Standard) support the approval of the requested surgery.

### RATIONALE

The requested Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) for Patricia Osei is medically necessary based on a comprehensive review of the clinical record, HealthFirst's own clinical coverage criteria, applicable California state law, and generally accepted standards of medical practice.

1.  **Compliance with Plan Criteria:** The member fully satisfies HealthFirst's Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), including the specific "Myelopathy Exception" (Section 2.2(A)). This exception waives the interventional treatment requirement due to the documented medical contraindication (pacemaker) and reduces the physical therapy requirement to 4 weeks (met by 5 weeks completed). The initial denial failed to properly apply this crucial exception within HealthFirst's own policy.
2.  **Compliance with California State Law:** California Insurance Code § 10169.5 and DMHC All-Plan Letter 22-014 prohibit health plans from requiring the completion of physical therapy or a full course of conservative treatment when there is objective evidence of structural neurological compromise (such as myelomalacia and severe cord compression as seen in Ms. Osei), progressive pathology, and when surgery is indicated under generally accepted guidelines. These state regulations mandate that clinical standards prevail over more restrictive plan criteria in such cases.
3.  **Adherence to Clinical Standard:** Major spine society guidelines (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021) classify moderate-to-severe cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia and objective neurological deficits as a Tier 1 strong surgical indication. These guidelines explicitly state that there is no high-quality evidence to support mandatory physical therapy trials for this condition and that delaying surgery in such cases risks irreversible neurological deterioration. Myelomalacia specifically signifies existing cord injury and urgency for decompression.

Given the member's progressive cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia, objective neurological deficits, and documented contraindication to interventional treatment, the requested ACDF is medically necessary to prevent further irreversible neurological damage.
