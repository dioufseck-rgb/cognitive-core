**APPEAL DETERMINATION LETTER**

**Procedure:** ACDF C5-C6 (CPT 22551)
**Date of Appeal Review:** 2024-11-15
**Reviewer:** Expert Health Plan Appeals Reviewer

**BACKGROUND:**
The member, Kevin Watkins, a 41-year-old professional violinist, requested prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6. The request was denied by HealthFirst PPO Gold on 2024-11-10 (initial level review by Dr. Patricia Huang, MD).

**DENIAL REASON (MED-003):** "Member meets conservative treatment requirements. However, surgical request is premature. Member's symptoms have been present for only 6 weeks. Plan criteria require at least 6 weeks of symptoms before surgical consideration. Recent onset radiculopathy has a high rate of spontaneous resolution."

**APPEAL BASIS:** The member's appeal highlights that his right hand weakness is career-ending, all conservative treatment was completed and failed within the 6-week period, a functional plateau was reached, and his physician recommends surgery due to the significant functional impairment.

**CLINICAL RECORD SUMMARY:**
*   **Diagnosis:** Acute cervical radiculopathy C5-C6 with functional impairment.
*   **Occupation:** Professional violinist, right hand function essential.
*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-10-01):** C5-C6 large disc herniation with moderate right C6 nerve root compression. Foraminal stenosis. *No cord contact. No myelomalacia.*
*   **Neurological Exam (2024-10-01):** Right C6 dermatomal numbness, diminished bicep reflex, grip strength 4/5 right. Unable to perform violin fingering tasks. Functional impairment documented by occupational therapy.
*   **Conservative Treatment (6 weeks total):**
    *   **Physical Therapy:** 6 weeks intensive (18 sessions). Documented functional plateau, no improvement in fine motor deficits or radicular symptoms. Occupational therapy confirms inability to perform professional duties.
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Methylprednisolone dose pack x3 (6 weeks, temporary partial relief), Gabapentin 600mg TID (5 weeks, partial pain relief, no motor improvement).
    *   **Interventional:** Right C6 epidural (2024-10-28, 25% temporary relief, no motor improvement, returned to baseline in 2 weeks).

**ANALYSIS (Applying Hierarchy: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria):**

**1. Primary Clinical Presentation:**
The clinical record describes a diagnosis of **Cervical Radiculopathy**, confirmed by imaging showing nerve root compression (C6) and unilateral neurological deficits (right C6 dermatome, diminished bicep reflex, grip weakness). The MRI explicitly states "No cord contact. No myelomalacia." This is a critical distinction from cervical myelopathy, which carries different treatment algorithms and policy exceptions. Therefore, myelopathy-specific provisions in plan criteria or state regulations (e.g., DMHC APL 22-014 regarding myelomalacia) do not apply to this case.

**2. Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**
*   **Section 2.1 (Diagnosis Requirement):** The member has confirmed cervical radiculopathy (2.1a) and cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit (2.1c), supported by MRI. **MET.**
*   **Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment Requirement):**
    *   (a) Physical therapy: "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy." The member completed 6 weeks of intensive PT. The plan's denial states, "Member meets conservative treatment requirements," which directly acknowledges this criterion was met.
    *   (b) Pharmacotherapy: "trial of at least two... for minimum 4 weeks each." The member received Methylprednisolone (6 weeks) and Gabapentin (5 weeks). **MET.**
    *   (c) Interventional treatment: "One of the following." The member received a right C6 epidural. **MET.**
    *   **Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A):** Not applicable as this is a radiculopathy case.
*   **Sections 2.3 (Imaging) and 2.4 (Physician Documentation):** These requirements are clearly met by the submitted clinical record. **MET.**

**Plan's Denial Reasoning vs. Plan Criteria:**
The denial states, "surgical request is premature. Member's symptoms have been present for only 6 weeks. Plan criteria require at least 6 weeks of symptoms before surgical consideration."
*   **Discrepancy:** The HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 (Section 2.2(a)) requires "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy," which the member completed. The plan criteria *do not* explicitly state a requirement for "at least 6 weeks of symptoms before surgical consideration" as a separate prerequisite. The denial's stated reason relies on a criterion not clearly articulated in its own policy. This raises concerns about the procedural adequacy of the denial.

**3. Application of California Regulatory Requirements (State Law):**
*   **CHSC § 1374.31(b) (Denial Notice Adequacy):** "A denial notice that fails to specify the clinical criteria applied or the specific clinical reason for the denial is DEFECTIVE." The plan's denial cites a requirement for "6 weeks of symptoms" not explicitly found in its policy, making the denial potentially defective.
*   **CIC § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):** This statute prohibits plans from conditioning surgical approval on PT completion where:
    *   (a)(2) "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
    *   (c) "A plan's internal requirement for a minimum number of weeks of physical therapy shall not be applied mechanically without reference to whether the member has achieved a functional plateau or whether the condition involves neurological urgency..."
    *   **Application:** The member completed the plan's 6-week PT requirement, and the clinical record explicitly documents a "Functional plateau... No improvement in fine motor deficits or radicular symptoms. OT confirms inability to perform professional duties." This directly invokes CIC § 10169.5(a)(2) and (c). California law therefore **overrides** any mechanical application of a fixed conservative treatment duration when a functional plateau has been reached, especially with significant functional impairment.

**4. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**
*   **Diagnosis & Urgency:** The case is clearly radiculopathy. While radiculopathy often resolves with conservative care, the clinical record documents objective motor deficits (grip strength 4/5, inability to perform violin fingering) and career-ending functional impairment.
*   **Conservative Treatment Duration:** Clinical guidelines (NASS 2020, Section 4 Table) suggest 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment for radiculopathy before surgery. The member has completed 6 weeks of intensive, multi-modal conservative treatment.
*   **Functional Plateau:** The clinical standard for functional plateau (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 5) is met: "No improvement in fine motor deficits or radicular symptoms" and "therapist documents maximum benefit reached" (implied by OT confirming inability to perform professional duties after 6 weeks of intensive PT).
*   **Surgical Indications:** AANS/CNS 2023 Guidelines (Tier 2) indicate surgery is a "reasonable option" for radiculopathy with:
    *   "(b) persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx"
    *   "(c) Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care."
    *   **Application:** The member has completed 6 weeks of conservative treatment, has persistent motor deficits, and suffers from significant, career-ending functional impairment that has not responded to conservative care. This aligns with the "reasonable indication" for surgery under generally accepted clinical standards. The general statement about spontaneous resolution for recent onset radiculopathy does not apply in a case of documented objective motor deficit, functional failure, and professional livelihood impact.

**5. Conflict Resolution (Hierarchy: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria):**
*   **State Law over Plan Criteria:** California law (CIC § 10169.5) explicitly permits surgical approval when a functional plateau has been reached after a course of physical therapy, regardless of a plan's minimum duration requirements. The member has met this legal criterion.
*   **Clinical Standard over Plan Criteria:** Generally accepted clinical standards support surgical consideration for radiculopathy with significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care, especially when a functional plateau is reached. The 6-week mark, combined with intensive multi-modal therapy failure and career-ending impairment, satisfies clinical criteria for considering surgery.
*   **Procedural Defect:** The plan's denial cites a requirement for "6 weeks of symptoms" which is not explicitly present in its own policy for surgical authorization, making the denial defective per CHSC § 1374.31(b).

**CONCLUSION:**
The denial is unsupported by the HealthFirst plan's own published criteria, is in direct conflict with California state law, and is inconsistent with generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice. The member has completed the plan's actual physical therapy duration requirement, has undergone a comprehensive course of conservative treatment, and has demonstrably reached a functional plateau with persistent, career-ending neurological deficits.

**DETERMINATION:**
The prior authorization denial for CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for Kevin Watkins (PA-2024-B004) is **OVERTURNED**.

**WARRANT:**
1.  **Procedural Defect:** The denial's stated reason, "Plan criteria require at least 6 weeks of symptoms before surgical consideration," cites a criterion not explicitly found in HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2. The actual criterion, "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy," was met. A denial based on an unstated or misquoted criterion is procedurally defective per CHSC § 1374.31(b).
2.  **State Law Override:** Even if the plan's denial were based on a valid criterion regarding conservative treatment duration, California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) states that a health insurer cannot condition approval of surgery on completion of physical therapy when the member has completed a course of physical therapy and reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms. The clinical record explicitly confirms the completion of 6 weeks of intensive PT and the documentation of a functional plateau by both PT and OT, with no improvement in motor deficits or ability to perform professional duties. This state law directly mandates approval in this circumstance.
3.  **Clinical Standard Alignment:** The member's condition, cervical radiculopathy with objective and career-ending motor deficits (4/5 grip strength, inability to perform violin fingering tasks) that have failed to improve after 6 weeks of intensive, multi-modal conservative therapy, meets generally accepted clinical standards for surgical consideration (AANS/CNS 2023 Guidelines, Tier 2, for "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" and "Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care"). The clinical definition of a functional plateau (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 5) has been met, supporting the cessation of further conservative efforts.

Based on the hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria), the provisions of California law and generally accepted clinical standards supersede the plan's restrictive interpretation or misapplication of its own criteria.

**Action:** HealthFirst PPO Gold is directed to approve the prior authorization for CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for Kevin Watkins.
