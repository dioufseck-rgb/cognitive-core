### **Clinical Background Summary:**

Marco Reyes is a 49-year-old male with a presenting diagnosis of persistent right-sided C6-C7 cervical radiculopathy.

*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-08-20):** Reveals a C6-C7 large disc herniation with moderate right C7 nerve root compression. Importantly, there is **no cord contact** and **no myelomalacia**. Right foraminal stenosis is confirmed.
*   **Neurological Exam (2024-08-25):** Documents right C7 dermatomal numbness, diminished right tricep reflex, and right grip strength 4/5. **No bilateral findings or upper motor neuron (UMN) signs** were noted.
*   **Conservative Treatment History:**
    *   **Physical Therapy:** A total of 9 weeks of supervised physical therapy was completed across two providers (3 weeks at Valley Physical Therapy from 2024-06-01 to 2024-06-21, followed by 6 weeks at Capital Physical Therapy from 2024-07-08 to 2024-08-16). Records for both were submitted with the original PA request. The discharge summary from Capital Physical Therapy explicitly documents a functional plateau, stating: "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected from continued PT."
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Trials of Gabapentin 600mg TID for 4 months (partial pain relief, radicular symptoms persist) and Naproxen 500mg BID for 3 months (inadequate response) were completed.
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** One right C7 epidural steroid injection was performed on 2024-09-15, providing 35% temporary relief that returned to baseline in 3 weeks.

### **Denial Summary & Plan's Factual Claim Under Test:**

The original denial stated that the member completed "only 3 weeks of physical therapy" and that "functional plateau not documented," thereby failing to meet the 6-week minimum conservative treatment requirement under HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2. The denial's factual claim that "3 weeks PT completed" is asserted by the appeal to be incorrect.

### **Appeal Review Findings:**

**1. Primary Clinical Presentation:**
Based on the provided clinical record (imaging showing nerve root compression without cord contact/myelomalacia, and neurological exam demonstrating unilateral dermatomal symptoms without bilateral findings or UMN signs), Mr. Reyes's primary clinical presentation is clearly **Cervical Radiculopathy**, not Myelopathy. This distinction is critical as different treatment algorithms and conservative treatment requirements apply.

**2. Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**
The original denial cited non-compliance with Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment Requirements), specifically regarding physical therapy duration and documentation of functional plateau.

*   **2.2(a) Physical Therapy:** The plan requires a "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy with documented functional outcomes."
    *   The appeal correctly points out that the member completed 3 weeks at Valley PT and an additional 6 weeks at Capital PT, for a **total of 9 weeks of physical therapy**. Both sets of records were submitted with the original request.
    *   The Capital PT discharge summary explicitly states "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected from continued PT," which directly fulfills the plan's definition of "functional plateau" (Section 2.2 Note, and Clinical Standard Section 5).
    *   **Finding:** The member **has completed** the minimum 6 weeks of PT (in fact, 9 weeks) and a functional plateau is explicitly documented. The factual basis of the plan's denial regarding PT duration and plateau documentation is **incorrect**.
*   **2.2(b) Pharmacotherapy:** The plan requires a trial of "at least two... for minimum 4 weeks each."
    *   The member completed Gabapentin for 4 months and Naproxen for 3 months.
    *   **Finding:** This criterion **is met**.
*   **2.2(c) Interventional Treatment:** The plan requires "one of the following."
    *   The member completed a right C7 epidural steroid injection.
    *   **Finding:** This criterion **is met**.
*   **Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A):** This exception reduces PT to 4 weeks and waives interventional treatment for confirmed myelopathy as the primary diagnosis.
    *   **Finding:** As Mr. Reyes's primary diagnosis is radiculopathy, not myelopathy, this exception **does not apply**. The plan was correct not to apply it, but their assessment of the standard conservative treatment was flawed.
*   **Other Criteria:** Based on the clinical record, Section 2.1 (Diagnosis), 2.3 (Imaging), and 2.4 (Physician Documentation) also appear to be met, as the denial focused solely on 2.2.

**Conclusion on Plan Criteria:** The member meets all applicable HealthFirst plan criteria for conservative treatment for radiculopathy. The denial was based on a factual error in reviewing the submitted documentation.

**3. Application of California Regulatory Framework:**

*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):**
    *   This state law prevents plans from conditioning approval of surgery on PT completion where: "(2) The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
    *   **Finding:** Mr. Reyes completed 9 weeks of PT, and a functional plateau was explicitly documented by the physical therapist. Therefore, even if the plan's 6-week minimum had not been met (which it was), denying coverage based on further PT would violate CIC § 10169.5(a)(2). Furthermore, section (c) states that a plan's minimum PT requirement "shall not be applied mechanically without reference to whether the member has achieved a functional plateau." The original denial failed to adhere to this by incorrectly claiming no plateau was documented.
*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014:** This APL primarily concerns myelopathy and urgent conditions, specifically myelomalacia. While Mr. Reyes's diagnosis is radiculopathy, not myelopathy with myelomalacia, the overarching principle in Section II.3 states that "Plans shall not apply internal criteria that are more restrictive than generally accepted standards of medical practice." This reinforces the importance of aligning with clinical guidelines.
*   **CHSC § 1374.32(b) (IMR Standard):** This states that IMR decisions shall be based on "generally accepted standards of medical practice" and not solely on a plan's internal criteria if they are more restrictive.
    *   **Finding:** While the plan's criteria, when correctly applied, are met in this case, this provision ensures that if there were a conflict, the generally accepted clinical standard would govern.

**4. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**

*   **Diagnosis & Conservative Treatment:** Clinical guidelines distinguish radiculopathy from myelopathy, with different treatment algorithms. For radiculopathy, conservative treatment is typically recommended first.
*   **Surgical Indications (AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines 2023, Section 3.1):**
    *   Mr. Reyes's presentation aligns with **TIER 2 — REASONABLE INDICATION** for surgery, specifically:
        *   "(b) Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx." Mr. Reyes has completed 9 weeks of PT, 4 months of pharmacotherapy, and an injection, with persistent symptoms.
        *   "(c) Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care." His persistent neurological deficits (numbness, weakness, diminished reflex) despite comprehensive conservative treatment and documented functional plateau meet this.
*   **Functional Plateau (Clinical Standard Section 5):** The explicit documentation by Capital PT that "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected from continued PT" aligns perfectly with the clinical standard definition of a functional plateau.

**Conclusion on Clinical Standard:** The clinical presentation and conservative treatment history of Mr. Reyes align with generally accepted standards of medical practice for considering surgical intervention for cervical radiculopathy.

### **Conflict Resolution & Hierarchy Application:**

The primary issue in this appeal is a **factual error** in the initial denial. The member **did complete 9 weeks of physical therapy**, and **functional plateau was explicitly documented** in the submitted records, directly contrary to the original denial's claims.

1.  **Plan Criteria:** When the factual errors are corrected, Mr. Reyes **fully meets** HealthFirst's own CC-SPINE-2024 criteria for conservative treatment, diagnosis, and imaging for cervical radiculopathy.
2.  **State Law:** Furthermore, California Insurance Code § 10169.5 mandates that a plan cannot deny based on uncompleted PT if a functional plateau has been reached. This law takes precedence over any mechanical application of plan criteria regarding PT duration, and in this case, the law supports approval as a functional plateau was documented.
3.  **Clinical Standard:** Generally accepted clinical standards for radiculopathy (AANS/CNS 2023) indicate that surgery is a reasonable option after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment with persistent symptoms and functional impairment, which Mr. Reyes clearly demonstrates.

In this instance, all three levels of the hierarchy (Plan Criteria, State Law, Clinical Standard) converge to support approval of the requested procedure. The plan's initial denial was based on an inaccurate assessment of the clinical record.

### **Determination:**

**OVERTURN**

### **Warrant:**

The denial for CPT 22551 (ACDF C6-C7) for Marco Reyes is **OVERTURNED**. The original denial, citing insufficient physical therapy (3 weeks completed) and lack of documented functional plateau, was based on an inaccurate review of the submitted clinical documentation.

1.  **Factual Error in Denial:** The clinical record clearly demonstrates that Mr. Reyes completed a total of 9 weeks of supervised physical therapy across two providers (3 weeks at Valley PT and 6 weeks at Capital PT), exceeding HealthFirst's minimum 6-week requirement under CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a). Furthermore, the discharge summary from Capital Physical Therapy explicitly documents that "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected from continued PT," which satisfies the plan's own definition of a functional plateau and the clinical standard for plateau (Clinical Reference Summary Section 5). Both records were submitted with the original prior authorization request.
2.  **Compliance with Plan Criteria:** With the correction of the factual errors, Mr. Reyes has demonstrably met HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 criteria, including:
    *   **2.2(a) Physical Therapy:** 9 weeks completed, with functional plateau documented.
    *   **2.2(b) Pharmacotherapy:** Gabapentin (4 months) and Naproxen (3 months) completed.
    *   **2.2(c) Interventional Treatment:** Right C7 epidural injection completed.
    *   **2.1 Diagnosis & 2.3 Imaging:** Confirmed cervical radiculopathy with consistent imaging findings.
3.  **Compliance with California State Law:** California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) prohibits health insurers from conditioning surgical approval on further physical therapy once a member has completed a course of PT and reached a documented functional plateau. Mr. Reyes's documented 9 weeks of PT with explicit functional plateau fulfills this legal requirement, overriding any mechanical application of a minimum PT duration.
4.  **Alignment with Clinical Standards:** Mr. Reyes's persistent cervical radiculopathy, with objective neurological findings and documented failure of comprehensive conservative treatment (9 weeks PT, two pharmacotherapy agents, one interventional procedure) with a functional plateau, meets the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) TIER 2 "Reasonable Indication" for surgical intervention (Section 3.1).

For these reasons, the requested ACDF C6-C7 is determined to be medically necessary and should be approved.
