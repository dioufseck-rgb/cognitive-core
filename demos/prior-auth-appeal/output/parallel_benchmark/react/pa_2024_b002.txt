**I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

The prior authorization denial for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 (CPT 22551) for member Thomas Reed is **OVERTURNED**.

The initial denial was based on the assertion that "Member does not meet criteria for ACDF. Imaging shows C6-C7 disc herniation with mild cord contact but no measurable compression. No myelopathy findings on exam. PT not yet completed to functional plateau." While the clinical record correctly indicates an absence of myelopathy, the denial's premise regarding the completion of physical therapy to a functional plateau is not supported by the clinical documentation and is contrary to California state law. **III. REVIEW AND ANALYSIS**

This appeal determination applies the three-source hierarchy: State law > Clinical standard > Plan criteria.

**A. Primary Clinical Presentation (Radiculopathy vs. Myelopathy)**

Based on the clinical record, the member's primary diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy.
*   **Findings for Radiculopathy:** Unilateral right C7 dermatomal numbness, diminished right tricep reflex, and mild right C7 foraminal stenosis are consistent with cervical radiculopathy.
*   **Absence of Myelopathy:** The neurological exam explicitly states "No bilateral findings. No upper motor neuron signs." The MRI explicitly notes "no cord signal change. No myelomalacia." While "mild cord contact" is present, the absence of myelopathic symptoms and cord signal changes means the diagnostic criteria for cervical myelopathy (as defined by HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1(b) and Clinical Reference Summary Section 1.1) are not met. Therefore, the more lenient conservative treatment exceptions applicable to myelopathy, as outlined in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(A) and DMHC APL 22-014, do not apply in this case.

**B. Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024)**

1.  **Diagnosis Requirement (Section 2.1):**
    *   **2.1(a) Cervical radiculopathy:** **Met.** The member has persistent right C7 radiculopathy with objective neurological deficit (diminished reflex, numbness) and supporting imaging (disc herniation, foraminal stenosis).
    *   **2.1(b) Cervical myelopathy:** **Not Met.** As established above, myelopathy criteria are not present.
    *   **2.1(c) Cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit:** **Met.** Disc herniation at C6-C7 with objective neurological deficit (diminished tricep reflex).

2.  **Conservative Treatment Requirement (Section 2.2):**
    *   **2.2(a) Physical therapy:** Requires "minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy with documented functional outcomes." The member completed **7 weeks of PT (21 sessions)**. The PT discharge summary explicitly states "functional plateau reached – patient has not improved over final 3 sessions." HealthFirst's criteria define functional plateau as "<10% improvement over 3 sessions OR therapist documents maximum benefit reached" (Domain Knowledge). The member meets this definition. **Therefore, the PT requirement is met.**
    *   **2.2(b) Pharmacotherapy:** Requires "trial of at least two... for minimum 4 weeks each." The member used Pregabalin for 4 months and Meloxicam for 3 months. **This requirement is met.**
    *   **2.2(c) Interventional treatments:** Requires "one of the following." The member received a right C7 epidural steroid injection. **This requirement is met.**

3.  **Imaging Requirement (Section 2.3):** **Met.** Current MRI (2024-08-01) demonstrates pathology consistent with symptoms.

4.  **Physician Documentation (Section 2.4):** The appeal basis indicates the surgeon believes ACDF is indicated for persistent radiculopathy, implying this requirement is met.

**Plan Criteria Conclusion:** The initial denial asserted "PT not yet completed to functional plateau." This is a misapplication of HealthFirst's own criteria. The member completed 7 weeks of PT, exceeding the 6-week minimum, and a functional plateau was documented, which satisfies HealthFirst's criteria for PT completion. All other conservative treatment requirements were also met.

**C. Application of California Regulatory Requirements**

1.  **CIC § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):**
    *   Section 10169.5(a)(2) states that a health insurer shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
    *   The clinical record explicitly confirms the member completed 7 weeks of PT and reached a documented functional plateau. **This state law directly applies and overrides any plan requirement for further PT if a functional plateau has been met.**
    *   Section 10169.5(a)(3) (objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that PT cannot address) does not apply specifically in the context of myelomalacia, as myelomalacia is not present. However, chronic radicular compression persisting after PT to plateau represents a structural compromise that PT alone has failed to address.

2.  **DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):**
    *   This APL specifically addresses myelopathy with objective evidence of cord compression and *myelomalacia*. As myelomalacia is not present and myelopathy is not the primary diagnosis, the specific "urgent treatment" and "conservative treatment exception" provisions of this APL **do not apply** to this case.

3.  **CHSC § 1374.32(b) (Standard of Review for IMR):** This section emphasizes that the IMR standard is "generally accepted standards of medical practice," which can supersede more restrictive plan criteria. Even if plan criteria were more restrictive, the service would be reviewed against this standard.

**Regulatory Conclusion:** California state law (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)) explicitly prohibits plans from denying surgery due to PT requirements when a functional plateau has been reached. This directly invalidates the primary reason for HealthFirst's denial.

**D. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021)**

1.  **Diagnostic Classification (Section 1.1):** The clinical guidelines distinguish between radiculopathy and myelopathy. This case is clearly cervical radiculopathy, not myelopathy (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 1.1).
2.  **Conservative Treatment for Radiculopathy (Section 2.1 - 2.2 for Myelopathy, but general principles apply to radiculopathy):** The clinical standard for functional plateau aligns with both the plan's definition and the documented clinical findings. The "Functional Plateau Definition" (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 5) specifies "therapist documentation that patient has reached maximum therapeutic benefit," which is explicitly stated in the PT discharge summary.
3.  **Surgical Indications (Section 3.1):**
    *   AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) identify "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" as a **TIER 2 — REASONABLE INDICATION** for ACDF (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3.1).
    *   The member has completed 7 weeks of PT (to plateau), 4 months of Pregabalin, 3 months of Meloxicam, and an epidural that provided only temporary relief. This course of conservative treatment (exceeding 6-12 weeks for pharmacotherapy and interventional) with persistent symptoms aligns with the clinical standard for surgical intervention for radiculopathy.

**Clinical Standard Conclusion:** The requested ACDF is consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice for cervical radiculopathy that has failed an adequate trial of conservative treatment, including physical therapy to a functional plateau, pharmacotherapy, and interventional pain management.

**E. Conflict Resolution and Final Determination**

The hierarchy of authority is State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.

The initial denial by HealthFirst was based on the premise that the member's physical therapy was "not yet completed to functional plateau." This premise is directly contradicted by the clinical record, which explicitly documents a functional plateau was reached after 7 weeks of PT.

Furthermore, California Insurance Code § 10169.5(a)(2) explicitly states that a plan cannot condition approval on completion of PT when a functional plateau has been reached. This state law takes precedence over any potentially more restrictive or misapplied plan criteria.

Finally, surgical intervention for persistent cervical radiculopathy after a documented failure of a reasonable course of conservative management (including PT to functional plateau, pharmacotherapy, and injection therapy) is a generally accepted clinical indication per AANS/CNS guidelines.

**IV. DISPOSITION**

**OVERTURN**

**V. WARRANT**

The denial for CPT 22551 (ACDF C6-C7) for member Thomas Reed is OVERTURNED. The initial denial's primary justification, "PT not yet completed to functional plateau," is erroneous for the following reasons:

1.  **Misapplication of Plan Criteria:** HealthFirst's own Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(a)) requires a minimum of 6 weeks of physical therapy. The member completed 7 weeks of PT, exceeding this minimum. Crucially, the PT discharge summary explicitly states "functional plateau reached – patient has not improved over final 3 sessions," which satisfies HealthFirst's internal definition of a functional plateau (Domain Knowledge). Therefore, the plan's initial reviewer misapplied HealthFirst's own criteria.
2.  **Violation of California State Law:** California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) explicitly prohibits a health insurer from conditioning approval of surgery on further physical therapy when "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms." The clinical record unequivocally demonstrates that this condition has been met, rendering the plan's denial reason invalid under state law, which supersedes plan criteria.
3.  **Consistency with Clinical Standards:** For cervical radiculopathy, generally accepted clinical guidelines (e.g., AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines 2023, Clinical Reference Summary Section 3.1) consider surgical intervention a "reasonable indication" (Tier 2) after 6-12 weeks of failed conservative treatment. The member has endured persistent symptoms despite 7 weeks of PT to a functional plateau, 4 months of neuropathic medication, 3 months of an NSAID, and an epidural injection that provided only transient relief, thus meeting the criteria for failed conservative management.

The member's clinical presentation, combined with the documented failure of an adequate and appropriate course of conservative treatment, fully supports the medical necessity for the requested ACDF under HealthFirst's criteria (when correctly applied), California state law, and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice.
