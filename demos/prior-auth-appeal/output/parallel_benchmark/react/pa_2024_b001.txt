**I. Overview of Appeal and Initial Denial**
The member, Linda Park, is a 45-year-old female diagnosed with cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 and adjacent C6-C7 degenerative disease. A prior authorization request for a two-level anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 and C6-C7 was denied by HealthFirst Health Plan. The denial reason cited that the two-level ACDF was not medically necessary, as imaging showed pathology at C5-C6 only, and C6-C7 exhibited mild degenerative changes without significant compression or neurological deficit attributable to that level.

The appeal argues that the member meets all plan criteria for at least the C5-C6 level, the surgeon recommends both levels to prevent adjacent segment disease, and appropriate conservative treatment has been completed.

**II. Clinical Presentation Analysis**
The clinical record indicates:
*   **Diagnosis:** Cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 with symptoms primarily affecting the right C6 dermatome (numbness, tingling, diminished bicep reflex, grip strength 4/5).
*   **Neurological Exam:** Confirms right C6 findings. Crucially, it explicitly states, "No C7 findings documented. No bilateral findings. No upper motor neuron signs." This rules out cervical myelopathy.
*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-09-01):**
    *   **C5-C6:** "large disc herniation with moderate-severe nerve root compression, moderate foraminal stenosis. Clear correlation with C6 radicular symptoms."
    *   **C6-C7:** "mild disc bulge, minimal foraminal narrowing, no cord contact, no nerve root compression identifiable."
*   **Conservative Treatment:** Member completed 8 weeks of physical therapy (24 sessions), 4 months of Gabapentin with partial relief, 6 weeks of Cyclobenzaprine with minimal benefit, and a right C6 epidural steroid injection that provided 50% temporary relief for 4 weeks. Symptoms persist for C6 radiculopathy.

Based on these findings, the primary clinical presentation is persistent, symptomatic **cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6**. There is no evidence of cervical myelopathy or symptomatic radiculopathy at the C6-C7 level.

**III. Procedural Adequacy of Denial**
The denial notice provided a specific clinical reason (lack of significant pathology/neurological deficit at C6-C7). While it did not explicitly cite plan criteria by section number, it conveyed a clear rationale for medical necessity deficiency at the second level. This notice is considered procedurally adequate under CHSC § 1374.31(b).

**IV. Application of HealthFirst Clinical Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024)**

**A. C5-C6 Level (for Radiculopathy):**
1.  **2.1 Diagnosis Requirement:** Met. The member has confirmed cervical radiculopathy with objective C6 neurological deficits correlating with C5-C6 large disc herniation and moderate-severe nerve root compression on MRI.
2.  **2.2 Conservative Treatment Requirement:** Met.
    *   (a) Physical therapy: 8 weeks completed (exceeds 6-week minimum).
    *   (b) Pharmacotherapy: Gabapentin (4 months) and Cyclobenzaprine (6 weeks) completed (exceeds 2 agents for 4+ weeks each).
    *   (c) Interventional treatment: Right C6 epidural steroid injection completed.
    *   The "Myelopathy Exception 2.2(A)" is not applicable as myelopathy is not the primary diagnosis.
3.  **2.3 Imaging Requirement:** Met. MRI demonstrates C5-C6 structural pathology consistent with C6 radicular symptoms.
4.  **2.4 Physician Documentation:** Met. Dr. Wilson's note and the appeal basis confirm completion of conservative treatment and clinical rationale for surgical intervention.

**Conclusion for C5-C6:** All HealthFirst plan criteria for cervical surgery appear to be met for the C5-C6 level.

**B. C6-C7 Level (for Prophylactic Adjacent Segment Disease):**
1.  **2.1 Diagnosis Requirement:** Not met. There are no documented C7 findings, no nerve root compression, and no objective neurological deficits attributable to C6-C7. The criteria require existing radiculopathy, myelopathy, or disc herniation with objective neurological deficit.
2.  **2.3 Imaging Requirement:** Not met. Imaging shows "mild disc bulge, minimal foraminal narrowing, no cord contact, no nerve root compression identifiable." This does not demonstrate structural pathology consistent with *symptoms* at this level, as there are no C7 symptoms.
3.  **Surgeon's Rationale:** The surgeon recommends fusion at C6-C7 "to prevent adjacent segment disease." HealthFirst's criteria do not include prophylactic fusion of an asymptomatic level as a medical necessity criterion in Section 2.

**Conclusion for C6-C7:** HealthFirst plan criteria for diagnosis and imaging are not met for the C6-C7 level.

**V. Application of California Regulatory Requirements**

**A. CIC § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):**
The member completed 8 weeks of physical therapy, and C6 radicular symptoms persist despite this and other conservative measures. This satisfies CIC § 10169.5(a)(2), which states that a plan cannot condition approval of surgery on PT completion where the member has completed PT and reached a functional plateau without adequate symptom resolution. In this case, the plan's 6-week minimum for PT was exceeded, and persistent symptoms demonstrate the plateau. This provision supports the member's eligibility for surgery regarding the C5-C6 level's PT requirement.

**B. DMHC APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):**
This All-Plan Letter specifically addresses conditions like cervical myelopathy with objective cord compression and myelomalacia. As established in the clinical analysis, Linda Park does not have myelopathy or myelomalacia. Therefore, the conservative treatment exceptions outlined in APL 22-014 do not apply to this case.

**C. CHSC § 1374.32(b) (Standard of Review for IMR):**
This statute mandates that services meeting "generally accepted standards of medical practice" shall be approved regardless of more restrictive plan criteria. This means the appeal must also be evaluated against clinical guidelines.

**VI. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021)**

**A. C5-C6 Level (for Radiculopathy):**
*   **Diagnosis:** The member's presentation (unilateral C6 dermatomal symptoms, weakness, diminished reflex, corresponding nerve root compression on MRI) aligns with the definition of cervical radiculopathy (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 1.1).
*   **Surgical Indication:** AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023 Update) classify "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative treatment" and "Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care" as a TIER 2 "reasonable indication" for surgery (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3.1). Linda Park's 8 weeks of comprehensive conservative treatment with persistent, functionally limiting C6 radicular symptoms clearly meets this clinical standard.
*   **Conservative Treatment Duration:** The 6-12 week conservative trial timeframe is well-established for radiculopathy, and the member's 8 weeks of PT plus other therapies falls within this (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 2.2, 4).

**Conclusion for C5-C6:** ACDF at the C5-C6 level is medically necessary and supported by generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice.

**B. C6-C7 Level (for Prophylactic Adjacent Segment Disease):**
*   **Lack of Symptomatic Pathology:** The clinical guidelines emphasize surgical intervention for *existing* symptomatic pathology (myelopathy or radiculopathy) or progressive neurological deficit (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3.1, TIER 1 & TIER 2). Linda Park's C6-C7 level is asymptomatic, with only mild degenerative changes and no nerve root or cord compression on MRI.
*   **Prophylactic Surgery:** While adjacent segment disease is a recognized concern, the provided clinical guidelines (AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine) do not establish prophylactic fusion of an asymptomatic level with only mild degenerative changes as a "generally accepted standard of medical practice" or a medically necessary indication for surgery. The rationale of preventing future disease, without current symptoms or significant pathology, does not meet the established criteria for surgical intervention in these guidelines.

**Conclusion for C6-C7:** ACDF at the C6-C7 level for prophylactic purposes is not supported by generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice.

**VII. Resolution of Conflicts and Final Determination**

**Hierarchy of Authority:** State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria

**A. For the C5-C6 Level (Radiculopathy):**
*   HealthFirst's plan criteria are met.
*   California state law provisions regarding PT requirements are met (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)).
*   Generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice strongly support surgical intervention for refractory cervical radiculopathy after an adequate conservative trial (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020).
*   **Decision:** The denial for the C5-C6 level is not justified.

**B. For the C6-C7 Level (Prophylactic Fusion):**
*   HealthFirst's plan criteria are *not* met for an asymptomatic level without significant pathology.
*   Generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice do *not* support prophylactic fusion of an asymptomatic, mildly degenerative level to prevent adjacent segment disease.
*   California state law, particularly CHSC § 1374.32(b), emphasizes adherence to generally accepted standards of medical practice, which in this case, do not support surgery on the C6-C7 level.
*   **Decision:** The denial for the C6-C7 level is justified.

**VIII. Disposition**

**Disposition: PARTIALLY OVERTURNED**

**Warrant:**
The initial denial for a two-level ACDF is **OVERTURNED** for the C5-C6 level and **UPHELD** for the C6-C7 level.

**Justification for Overturn (C5-C6):**
Linda Park presents with clear, persistent C6 radiculopathy (right C6 dermatomal numbness/tingling, diminished bicep reflex, grip strength 4/5) directly correlating with a large disc herniation and moderate-severe nerve root compression at C5-C6 on MRI (Clinical Record: Neurological Exam, Imaging). She has completed an extensive conservative treatment regimen over 8 weeks of physical therapy, 4 months of neuropathic agents (Gabapentin), 6 weeks of muscle relaxants (Cyclobenzaprine), and a targeted epidural steroid injection, with inadequate and temporary relief (Clinical Record: Conservative Treatment). These efforts meet and exceed HealthFirst's conservative treatment requirements (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2). The persistence of symptoms after this course of therapy indicates a functional plateau, satisfying the conditions under California Insurance Code § 10169.5(a)(2) which prohibits conditioning surgery on further physical therapy. Furthermore, generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice, as outlined in the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023 Update), indicate surgery as a reasonable option (TIER 2 indication) for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment and significant functional impairment (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3.1). Therefore, surgical decompression at C5-C6 is medically necessary.

**Justification for Uphold (C6-C7):**
The requested fusion at the C6-C7 level is based solely on the surgeon's recommendation to prophylactically prevent adjacent segment disease. The clinical record clearly states "No C7 findings documented. No bilateral findings. No upper motor neuron signs," and the MRI at C6-C7 shows only "mild disc bulge, minimal foraminal narrowing, no cord contact, no nerve root compression identifiable" (Clinical Record: Neurological Exam, Imaging). This completely lacks any symptomatic radiculopathy or myelopathy, or objective neurological deficit correlating to pathology at C6-C7. As such, the C6-C7 level does not meet HealthFirst's diagnosis or imaging criteria for surgical intervention (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Sections 2.1, 2.3). Critically, generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021) do not support prophylactic fusion of an asymptomatic and mildly degenerative spinal level simply to prevent potential future adjacent segment disease (Clinical Reference Summary, Section 3). Surgical indications in these guidelines are predicated on existing, symptomatic, and objectively confirmed pathology, not on preventive measures for asymptomatic levels. Therefore, the medical necessity for ACDF at the C6-C7 level is not supported by either plan criteria or clinical standards.

**Action:** Authorization for Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at the **C5-C6 level only** is approved. The denial for the C6-C7 level remains upheld.
