**HEALTHFIRST HEALTH PLAN — PRIOR AUTHORIZATION APPEAL DETERMINATION**

**Case ID:** PA-2024-G005
**Member:** Gerald Obi (M-G005), Age 58
**Procedure:** ACDF C6-C7, one level (CPT 22551)
**Date of Review:** [Insert Current Date]

**BACKGROUND:**
Mr. Gerald Obi, a 58-year-old member, requested prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 due to cervical radiculopathy with functional impairment. The initial request was denied by HealthFirst Utilization Management (Dr. Patricia Huang, MD) on 2024-11-08 with two stated reasons:
1.  Member did not meet conservative treatment requirements, specifically citing 5 weeks of physical therapy completed against a requirement of 6 weeks.
2.  Member has poorly controlled type 2 diabetes (HbA1c 8.2%) which presents perioperative risk and wound healing concerns that must be addressed prior to surgical authorization.

The member's appeal asserts that:
1.  7 weeks of physical therapy were completed, not 5, and the denial miscounted.
2.  Diabetes control is not a criterion for ACDF authorization under HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 policy.
3.  All other cervical spine criteria are met.
4.  Perioperative diabetes management is the surgical team's responsibility and not a prior authorization criterion.

**CLINICAL PRESENTATION SUMMARY:**
Mr. Obi presents with cervical radiculopathy at C6-C7, documented by MRI showing a large disc herniation with moderate left C7 nerve root compression (no cord contact, no myelomalacia). Neurological exam confirms left C7 dermatomal numbness, diminished tricep reflex, and 4/5 tricep weakness, leading to reduced working hours. The primary diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy, not myelopathy.

Conservative treatment included:
*   **Physical Therapy (PT):** 7 weeks (21 sessions) completed from 2024-07-01 through 2024-08-16, with functional plateau documented: "no further functional improvement expected."
*   **Pharmacotherapy:** Pregabalin 150mg BID for 5 months (partial pain relief) and Duloxetine 60mg for 3 months (moderate neuropathic benefit).
*   **Interventional:** Left C7 epidural steroid injection on 2024-10-10, providing 45% temporary relief that returned to baseline in 4 weeks.

Comorbidities include Type 2 Diabetes with current HbA1c of 8.2% (target <7.5%). The endocrinologist noted suboptimal control and recommended optimization prior to elective surgery due to elevated wound healing risk. The treating surgeon noted that diabetes management would be optimized preoperatively as part of standard protocol, and affirmed it is not a PA criterion.

**REVIEW AND ANALYSIS:**

**1. Primary Clinical Presentation:**
Based on the MRI findings (nerve root compression, *no cord contact, no myelomalacia*) and neurological examination (unilateral C7 radicular signs, *no upper motor neuron signs*), Mr. Obi's primary diagnosis is **Cervical Radiculopathy C6-C7**. This distinction is critical as it determines the applicable criteria and exceptions. The myelopathy exception outlined in HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 (Section 2.2(A)) and specific state guidance for myelopathy with myelomalacia (DMHC APL 22-014) do not apply to this case.

**2. Application of HealthFirst's Clinical Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**

*   **2.1 DIAGNOSIS REQUIREMENT:**
    *   Mr. Obi has confirmed cervical radiculopathy (2.1(a)) and cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit (2.1(c)) supported by MRI and neurological exam. **CRITERION MET.**
*   **2.2 CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT REQUIREMENT:**
    *   **(a) Physical Therapy:** The plan criteria require a minimum of 6 weeks. The initial denial stated 5 weeks. However, the clinical record definitively documents 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy (2024-07-01 to 2024-08-16), exceeding the 6-week requirement. Furthermore, a "functional plateau" was documented at discharge, indicating no further functional improvement was expected. **CRITERION MET (and exceeded), denial claim is factually incorrect.**
    *   **(b) Pharmacotherapy:** Mr. Obi completed trials of Pregabalin (5 months) and Duloxetine (3 months), meeting the requirement for at least two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each. **CRITERION MET.**
    *   **(c) Interventional Treatment:** Mr. Obi received a Left C7 Epidural Steroid Injection. **CRITERION MET.**
    *   The member has met the conservative treatment requirements as per the standard pathway in CC-SPINE-2024.
*   **2.3 IMAGING REQUIREMENT:**
    *   Current MRI (within 6 months) demonstrates structural pathology consistent with symptoms and the proposed surgical level. **CRITERION MET.**
*   **2.4 PHYSICIAN DOCUMENTATION:**
    *   The treating physician's notes indicate appropriate documentation has been provided. **CRITERION MET (implicitly).**

**3. Application of California Regulatory Requirements:**

*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) — Physical Therapy as Prerequisite:** This state law explicitly states that a health insurer cannot condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where "The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
    *   **Analysis:** The clinical record confirms that Mr. Obi completed 7 weeks of PT and reached a functional plateau, with the therapist documenting "no further functional improvement expected." Therefore, even if the plan's 6-week minimum were still an issue, state law prohibits denial based on further PT requirements. This provision supersedes the plan's mechanical application of a minimum PT duration.
*   **California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b) — Plan Obligations on Denial:** This code indicates a denial notice is defective if it fails to specify clinical criteria. Conversely, denying on grounds *not* specified in the plan's criteria for the requested service is problematic.
    *   **Analysis:** The denial cited "poorly controlled type 2 diabetes (HbA1c 8.2%)" as a reason. HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 policy (Section 2: Medical Necessity Criteria, Section 3: Exclusions) for cervical spinal surgery *does not include diabetes control or specific HbA1c levels as a criterion for authorization or denial*. While diabetes management is critical for patient safety, it is a perioperative management consideration and not a valid prior authorization criterion for the medical necessity of the procedure itself under the plan's policy. The plan cannot introduce new criteria during the review process that are not part of its publicly available and approved clinical coverage criteria.

**4. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**

*   **Surgical Indications for Radiculopathy:** According to AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023), surgery is a "Reasonable Indication" (Tier 2) for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment or significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care.
    *   **Analysis:** Mr. Obi has persistent radicular symptoms, objective neurological deficits, functional impairment, and has completed a significant course of conservative treatment (7 weeks PT, 5 months pharmacotherapy, epidural injection) without adequate resolution. This aligns with generally accepted standards for surgical intervention for radiculopathy.
*   **Functional Plateau:** The clinical standard for a functional plateau is less than 10% improvement over 3 sessions or therapist documentation of maximum benefit. Mr. Obi's therapist documented maximum benefit, consistent with this standard.

**CONCLUSION:**
The initial denial for ACDF C6-C7 for Mr. Gerald Obi was based on two grounds, both of which are found to be invalid upon review:

1.  **Physical Therapy Shortfall:** The denial incorrectly stated 5 weeks of PT were completed. The clinical record clearly demonstrates that Mr. Obi completed 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy, exceeding the plan's 6-week requirement. Furthermore, the treating therapist documented a functional plateau, meaning no further benefit was expected from continued PT. Under California Insurance Code § 10169.5(a)(2), a plan cannot deny surgical authorization on the basis of requiring further PT when a functional plateau has been reached.
2.  **Diabetes Control:** Poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes (HbA1c 8.2%) was cited as a reason for denial due to perioperative risk. However, HealthFirst's own Clinical Coverage Criteria for Cervical Spinal Surgery (CC-SPINE-2024) does not list diabetes control or specific HbA1c levels as a criterion for medical necessity, authorization, or exclusion for this procedure. While optimizing diabetes is crucial for patient safety, it is a matter of perioperative management by the surgical team and not a valid prior authorization criterion for the procedure's medical necessity. Denying coverage based on unstated criteria is not permissible.

Mr. Obi meets all applicable medical necessity criteria under HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 policy for cervical radiculopathy, and the denial reasons are contrary to both the factual clinical record and California state law. The requested service is medically necessary and appropriate given his diagnosis, failed conservative treatment, and functional impairment, consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice.

**DISPOSITION:**
**OVERTURN**

**WARRANT:**
The denial is OVERTURNED because:
1.  **Factual Inaccuracy Regarding Conservative Treatment:** The initial denial incorrectly stated Mr. Obi completed only 5 weeks of physical therapy. The comprehensive clinical record confirms 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy were completed, exceeding HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) requirement for a minimum of 6 weeks.
2.  **Violation of State Law on Functional Plateau:** Mr. Obi's physical therapist documented that he reached a functional plateau, indicating no further functional improvement was expected. Under California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2), a health plan cannot deny a surgical procedure based on a physical therapy requirement when a functional plateau has been documented. This state law takes precedence over the plan's criteria.
3.  **Denial Based on Unstated Criteria:** The denial cited poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes as a reason for denial. However, HealthFirst's published Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 for ACDF (CPT 22551) does not include diabetes control or specific HbA1c levels as a medical necessity criterion or exclusion. Denying a medically necessary service based on criteria not specified in the plan's own policy is impermissible (implicitly supported by CHSC § 1374.31(b)). While perioperative diabetes management is vital for patient safety, it is a clinical management responsibility of the surgical team, not a prior authorization criterion for the medical necessity of the procedure itself.
4.  **Medical Necessity Supported by Clinical Standards:** Mr. Obi has documented cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits and functional impairment, which has failed a comprehensive course of conservative treatment, including 7 weeks of PT, 5 months of pharmacotherapy, and an epidural injection with only temporary relief. This presentation meets the criteria for surgical intervention as a "Reasonable Indication" (Tier 2) under the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020) for persistent radiculopathy not responding to conservative care.

Therefore, prior authorization for CPT 22551 for Mr. Gerald Obi is approved.
