**DISPOSITION: OVERTURN DENIAL**

---

**I. BACKGROUND**

Michael Green, a 57-year-old member, had a prior authorization request for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 denied by HealthFirst Health Plan on November 8, 2024. The stated reason for denial was that the "Member does not meet criteria for requested procedure under plan policy," referencing an attached criteria document that was reportedly not provided to the member or provider. An Independent Medical Review (IMR) notice was also not included with the denial. The appeal basis centers on these procedural defects and the inability to evaluate the denial without the applied criteria.

The clinical record indicates a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 with functional impairment, confirmed by an MRI showing a large disc herniation and moderate left C6 nerve root compression, with no spinal cord contact. Neurological exam findings include left C6 dermatomal numbness, diminished bicep reflex, and 4/5 left bicep weakness. The member completed 7 weeks of physical therapy, 4 months of Gabapentin, 3 months of Naproxen, and a left C6 epidural injection, with persistent symptoms and a documented plateau in physical therapy at week 6.

**II. REVIEW CRITERIA AND HIERARCHY**

This review applies a three-source hierarchy:
1.  **State Law:** California Insurance Code (CIC), California Health & Safety Code (CHSC), and Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) All-Plan Letters (APLs).
2.  **Clinical Standard:** Generally accepted medical practice as evidenced by professional society guidelines (e.g., AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine).
3.  **Plan Criteria:** HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024.

Where sources conflict, the higher authority governs.

**III. FINDINGS AND ANALYSIS**

**A. Procedural Adequacy of Denial**

The initial denial by HealthFirst was procedurally defective based on California state law:
*   **Failure to Provide Specific Criteria:** The denial notice referenced an attached criteria document (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024), but this document was not provided to the member or the treating provider. California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b) explicitly states that a denial notice "that fails to specify the clinical criteria applied or the specific clinical reason for the denial is DEFECTIVE and shall be treated as if no denial was issued for purposes of appeal timelines."
*   **Failure to Include IMR Notice:** The appeal basis confirms that an IMR notice was not included. CHSC § 1374.31(a)(2) mandates that the denial notice shall "Include... notice of IMR rights."

These procedural defects alone are sufficient grounds to overturn the denial, as the member was prevented from understanding the basis of the denial and their full appeal rights.

**B. Clinical Presentation and Diagnosis**

The clinical record clearly identifies the member's primary diagnosis as **cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6**, not myelopathy. The MRI explicitly notes "No cord contact," and the neurological findings are consistent with nerve root compression. This distinction is critical because myelopathy (spinal cord compression) has different treatment algorithms and conservative treatment requirements compared to radiculopathy, as highlighted in the Clinical Standard Knowledge and the plan's own criteria (Section 2.2(A) Exception Note). Therefore, the myelopathy-specific exceptions or waivers of conservative treatment requirements (e.g., DMHC APL 22-014 related to myelomalacia/cord compression urgency) do not directly apply here for waiving all conservative treatment.

**C. Review of Conservative Treatment Requirements**

Despite the procedural defects, a substantive review of the conservative treatment, assuming the plan's denial was based on this category, reveals that the member meets the requirements:

1.  **Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2 for Radiculopathy):**
    *   **(a) Physical Therapy:** "minimum 6 weeks... with documented functional outcomes." The member completed 7 weeks (21 sessions) of PT, exceeding the 6-week minimum. The PT notes document a "plateau at week 6" with persistent radicular symptoms.
    *   **(b) Pharmacotherapy:** "trial of at least two... for minimum 4 weeks each." The member completed 4 months of Gabapentin and 3 months of Naproxen, meeting this requirement.
    *   **(c) Interventional Treatment:** "One of the following..." The member received a Left C6 epidural, meeting this requirement.

2.  **State Law Override (CIC § 10169.5 - Physical Therapy):**
    *   Even if HealthFirst interpreted "documented functional outcomes" to imply a greater level of symptom resolution than achieved, California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) states that a health insurer "shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where... The member has completed a course of physical therapy and the treating physician documents that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms."
    *   The clinical record explicitly states that a "plateau at week 6" was reached. Furthermore, the Clinical Standard (Section 5) defines functional plateau as "less than 10% improvement over 3 consecutive therapy sessions, OR therapist documentation that patient has reached maximum therapeutic benefit." The documented plateau after 6 weeks of PT satisfies this definition.
    *   Therefore, any denial based on insufficient physical therapy is superseded by state law.

3.  **Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine):**
    *   For radiculopathy, the clinical standard supports surgical intervention for "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" and "Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care" (AANS/CNS TIER 2 indication, Section 3.1).
    *   Michael Green has undergone 7 weeks of PT (reaching a plateau), 4 months of neuropathic agents (partial relief), 3 months of NSAIDs (inadequate relief), and an epidural injection (temporary relief), with persistent C6 numbness and 4/5 weakness. This comprehensive trial of conservative treatment, with documented failure to resolve significant symptoms, aligns with the clinical standard for proceeding to surgical intervention.

**D. Conclusion on Medical Necessity**

Based on the review, the member has completed a thorough course of conservative treatment that has failed to adequately resolve symptoms and functional deficits. The physical therapy requirement, in particular, has been satisfied under the interpretation provided by California state law, which prioritizes the attainment of a functional plateau over arbitrary duration requirements. The clinical presentation meets established guidelines for surgical intervention for cervical radiculopathy.

**IV. CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND RATIONALE FOR DISPOSITION**

The denial by HealthFirst Health Plan is in direct conflict with California state law and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice.

1.  **State Law Precedence:** The denial was procedurally inadequate as per CHSC § 1374.31(a)(2) and (b), failing to provide criteria or IMR notice. This alone warrants overturning. Furthermore, any denial based on an inadequate course of physical therapy is directly superseded by CIC § 10169.5(a)(2), as the member achieved a documented functional plateau.
2.  **Clinical Standard Alignment:** The member's persistent symptoms, objective neurological deficits, and documented failure of an appropriate course of conservative therapy meet the clinical standards for ACDF for cervical radiculopathy as outlined by AANS/CNS, NASS, and AO Spine guidelines (TIER 2 indications). CHSC § 1374.32(b) reinforces that services meeting generally accepted standards shall be approved regardless of more restrictive plan criteria.
3.  **Plan Criteria Application:** While the specific unmet plan criterion was not provided, the available clinical information demonstrates that the member *has met* HealthFirst's own criteria for conservative treatment for radiculopathy, especially when interpreted through the lens of state law regarding functional plateau.

**V. DETERMINATION**

For the reasons stated above, including significant procedural defects in the denial notice and the member's fulfillment of conservative treatment requirements as defined by state law and clinical standards, the denial of prior authorization for CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for Michael Green is **OVERTURNED**.

HealthFirst Health Plan is directed to approve the prior authorization for the requested procedure.
