## Prior Authorization Appeal Determination: PA-2024-D001

**Member:** Sandra White (M-334466)
**Procedure:** ACDF C6-C7 (CPT 22551)
**Date of Denial:** 2024-10-05
**Date of Appeal Review:** [Current Date]

**DISPOSITION: OVERTURNED**

---

**I. BACKGROUND**

Sandra White, a 44-year-old member of HealthFirst PPO Gold, requested prior authorization for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 (CPT 22551) for a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy. The request was denied by Dr. Alan Foster, MD, on 2024-10-05, citing "MED-003: Does not meet criteria. Conservative treatment incomplete. Physical therapy and medication requirements not satisfied." The denial notice contained a generic reference to plan criteria without specific section numbers or criteria and did not include notice of Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights.

The member's appeal is based on the procedural deficiencies of the denial notice, specifically the lack of specified criteria and the omission of the IMR notice, which prevents a meaningful appeal.

**II. CLINICAL SUMMARY**

*   **Presenting Diagnosis:** Cervical radiculopathy C6-C7.
*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-08-15):** C6-C7 disc herniation, moderate right C7 nerve root compression. **Notably, no cord contact and no myelomalacia are reported.**
*   **Neurological Exam (2024-08-20):** Right C7 dermatomal numbness, tricep weakness 4/5 right, tricep reflex diminished. These findings are consistent with cervical radiculopathy.
*   **Conservative Treatment:**
    *   **Physical Therapy (PT):** 4 weeks, 12 sessions completed. Member claims functional plateau, but the PT discharge note does not explicitly use this language. HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a) requires a minimum of 6 weeks.
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Ibuprofen 800mg TID for 6 weeks, with partial relief. HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(b) requires a trial of at least two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each.
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** None documented. HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(c) requires one interventional treatment.

**III. REVIEW AND ANALYSIS**

This appeal review applies the three-source hierarchy: **State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria**.

**A. Procedural Adequacy of Denial (State Law)**

1.  **CHSC § 1374.31(b) - Plan Obligations on Denial:** This state law mandates that a plan's denial notice must:
    *   Specify the exact clinical criteria applied.
    *   Include the specific clinical reason for the denial.
    *   Include notice of IMR rights.
    The law explicitly states, "A denial notice that fails to specify the clinical criteria applied or the specific clinical reason for the denial is DEFECTIVE and shall be treated as if no denial was issued for purposes of appeal timelines." (CHSC § 1374.31(b)).

2.  **Application to Case:** The initial denial notice for PA-2024-D001 explicitly states "Generic reference to plan criteria — no section number, no specific criterion" and confirms "imr_notice_included: false." These omissions constitute a clear violation of CHSC § 1374.31(b).

3.  **Conclusion on Procedural Adequacy:** The denial notice issued by HealthFirst is procedurally defective under California state law. This is a foundational flaw in the denial process.

**B. Clinical Classification of Condition**

1.  **Domain Knowledge - Classification:** The distinction between cervical radiculopathy and cervical myelopathy is critical, as they have different treatment algorithms and conservative treatment requirements. Myelopathy involves spinal cord compression with specific upper motor neuron signs and potentially myelomalacia, while radiculopathy involves nerve root compression with unilateral dermatomal symptoms.

2.  **Application to Case:** The clinical record consistently indicates **cervical radiculopathy**. The MRI shows nerve root compression, but explicitly states "No cord contact. No myelomalacia." The neurological exam findings (unilateral numbness, weakness, diminished reflex) are characteristic of radiculopathy, not myelopathy (which would typically involve bilateral symptoms, hyperreflexia, gait disturbance, etc.).

3.  **Conclusion on Clinical Classification:** Sandra White's condition is cervical radiculopathy. Therefore, any state laws or plan criteria specific to myelopathy (e.g., HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Exception 2.2(A), DMHC APL 22-014) do not apply to this case.

**C. Application of Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024) - *If procedurally adequate***

For cervical radiculopathy (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.1(a)), the standard conservative treatment requirements in Section 2.2 apply:
*   **Physical Therapy (2.2(a)):** 6 weeks minimum. Member completed 4 weeks. **Not met.**
*   **Pharmacotherapy (2.2(b)):** Two agents, 4+ weeks each. Member used one agent (Ibuprofen) for 6 weeks. **Not met.**
*   **Interventional Treatment (2.2(c)):** One required. Member had none. **Not met.**

The "MYELOPATHY PATHWAY" Exception 2.2(A) does not apply here as the primary diagnosis is radiculopathy. Thus, based *solely* on the plan's criteria and the clinical record for radiculopathy, the conservative treatment requirements were not satisfied.

**D. Application of California Regulatory Framework (State Law) - *Beyond Procedural Adequacy***

1.  **CIC § 10169.5 - Physical Therapy as Prerequisite:** This law prohibits conditioning surgical approval on PT completion where:
    *   (a)(1) Physician documents PT contraindicated/unlikely to benefit: Not documented in the record.
    *   (a)(2) Member completed PT and reached functional plateau: The member claims a functional plateau, but the PT discharge note does not confirm this language, and the record does not provide objective measures to meet the plan's definition of functional plateau (<10% improvement over 3 sessions OR therapist documentation of maximum benefit).
    *   (a)(3) Objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that PT cannot address: This provision typically applies to severe conditions like myelopathy with myelomalacia or cord compression. Given the diagnosis of radiculopathy without cord contact or myelomalacia, this provision is not clearly met in this case.

2.  **DMHC APL 22-014 - Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions:** This All-Plan Letter specifically addresses myelopathy with objective evidence of cord compression and myelomalacia. As the member has radiculopathy without myelomalacia or cord compression, this APL does not apply.

3.  **Conclusion on State Law Clinical Waivers:** Based on the information provided, the specific conditions for waiving conservative treatment under California state law (CIC 10169.5 and DMHC APL 22-014) are not definitively met for this specific presentation of radiculopathy.

**E. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS, NASS, AO Spine)**

1.  **Conservative Treatment for Radiculopathy:** The clinical guidelines suggest that ACDF is a reasonable option for radiculopathy *with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks of conservative treatment* (AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines 2023, Tier 2).
2.  **Application to Case:** The member has completed 4 weeks of PT and one pharmacotherapy agent, with no interventional treatment. This falls short of the typical 6-12 week comprehensive conservative trial often recommended for radiculopathy before surgical consideration, as well as the plan's specific requirements.
3.  **Conclusion on Clinical Standard:** If the denial had been procedurally adequate, a denial based on incomplete conservative treatment for radiculopathy, which is not subject to the myelopathy exceptions, would align with general clinical guidelines that often recommend a more extended trial (6-12 weeks) before surgery.

**IV. CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND RATIONALE FOR DISPOSITION**

The primary and overriding factor in this appeal is the **procedural defect** of the initial denial notice. California Health & Safety Code Section 1374.31(b) is a state law, and according to the established hierarchy (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria), it takes precedence.

The plan failed to provide the member with a lawful denial notice by:
1.  Not specifying the exact clinical criteria applied.
2.  Not providing the specific clinical reason for the denial in a clear, articulable manner by referencing specific criteria.
3.  Not including the required notice of the member's right to Independent Medical Review (IMR).

These failures mean the denial was not issued in compliance with state law. A defective denial notice cannot stand, regardless of the clinical merits of the case. The member's right to understand the basis of the denial and their appeal rights is paramount.

While a review of the clinical record against HealthFirst's criteria for radiculopathy suggests that the conservative treatment requirements were indeed incomplete, the plan's failure to communicate this denial legally requires an overturn. The plan must adhere to state regulations in its communications.

**V. DETERMINATION**

The denial of prior authorization for CPT 22551 for Sandra White (PA-2024-D001) is **OVERTURNED**.

**WARRANT:** The denial notice issued by HealthFirst on 2024-10-05 is procedurally defective as it failed to specify the exact clinical criteria applied, did not provide a specific clinical reason by referencing those criteria, and omitted the required notice of the member's right to Independent Medical Review (IMR). These omissions are in direct violation of California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) Section 1374.31(b), which mandates these elements for all denial notices. Per CHSC 1374.31(b), a defective notice "shall be treated as if no denial was issued for purposes of appeal timelines." This procedural deficiency at the State Law level necessitates overturning the denial to ensure compliance with state regulations and due process.

**INSTRUCTION TO HEALTH PLAN:** HealthFirst must process this request as if the original denial was never issued. If HealthFirst wishes to deny the service based on clinical criteria, it must issue a **new, procedurally compliant denial notice** that explicitly:
1.  States the specific clinical criteria (e.g., section and sub-criterion numbers from HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024) that were not met.
2.  Clearly explains how the member's clinical situation fails to meet each specified criterion.
3.  Includes the member's right to Independent Medical Review (IMR) and the process for initiating it, as required by CHSC § 1374.31(b).

Any future denial must also acknowledge that the member's diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy, meaning the myelopathy-specific exceptions in plan criteria and state law (e.g., DMHC APL 22-014) are not applicable. The plan should specifically address the completeness of conservative treatment, detailing which elements (PT duration, number of pharmacotherapy agents, interventional procedures) are considered outstanding under the applicable plan criteria for radiculopathy.
