**DISPOSITION: OVERTURN DENIAL**

---

**WARRANT:**

The denial for CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6, one level) for member Theresa Kamau (M-G006) is **OVERTURNED**. The initial denial was procedurally flawed and medically unjustified, failing to adhere to California state law, generally accepted clinical standards, and the HealthFirst PPO Gold plan's own clinical criteria (CC-SPINE-2024).

**1. Procedural Flaws in Initial Denial:**
The initial denial itself explicitly noted that its basis ("Prior PA history — NOT a valid criterion under CC-SPINE-2024") was invalid and that the "denial is procedurally flawed." Furthermore, the denial's assertion that "the member's cervical pathology has not materially changed since the prior denial" is directly contradicted by the clinical record. California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b) states that a denial notice failing to specify the clinical criteria or specific clinical reason for denial is defective. The denial clearly failed on both counts.

**2. Adherence to HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**
The requested procedure fully meets all applicable criteria outlined in HealthFirst's CC-SPINE-2024 for cervical spinal surgery:

*   **2.1 DIAGNOSIS REQUIREMENT:**
    *   The member has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy C5-C6, progressive, with objective neurological deficit (right C6 dermatomal numbness, absent bicep reflex, 3+/5 right bicep weakness). Current MRI (October 2024) demonstrates a large C5-C6 disc herniation with moderate-severe right C6 nerve root compression, consistent with symptoms. (Meets 2.1(a) and 2.1(c)).
*   **2.2 CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT REQUIREMENT:**
    *   **Physical Therapy:** The member completed a NEW 8-week course of supervised physical therapy (July-September 2024), exceeding the 6-week minimum requirement. The physical therapist explicitly documented a "functional plateau" with "no further improvement expected, neurological deficit persistent and worsening." (Meets 2.2(a)).
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** The member completed trials of Gabapentin (6 months) and Methylprednisolone (two courses), both for longer than the 4-week minimum each. (Meets 2.2(b)).
    *   **Interventional Treatment:** The member underwent a right C6 epidural steroid injection (November 2024), which provided only 50% relief for 2 weeks before returning to baseline. (Meets 2.2(c)).
    *   The Myelopathy Exception (2.2A) is not applicable as the primary diagnosis is radiculopathy, not myelopathy.
*   **2.3 IMAGING REQUIREMENT:**
    *   Current MRI (October 2024) shows C5-C6 large disc herniation with moderate-severe right C6 nerve root compression and new foraminal stenosis, directly correlating with the member's symptoms and the proposed surgical level. (Meets 2.3).
*   **2.4 PHYSICIAN DOCUMENTATION:**
    *   Documentation provided by Dr. Victor Osei outlining clinical rationale, functional limitations (unable to work full-time), and a statement of surgical risks and alternatives is presumed to be adequate, as no deficiencies were cited in the initial denial.
*   **Section 3 — Exclusions:** No listed exclusions apply to this case.

**3. Compliance with California Regulatory Framework:**

*   **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 — Physical Therapy as Prerequisite:** This state law explicitly prohibits a health insurer from conditioning approval of surgery on completion of physical therapy where:
    *   (a)(2) The member completed PT and reached a documented functional plateau (which occurred here after 8 weeks of new PT).
    *   (a)(3) The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that PT cannot address (e.g., progressive herniation and worsening weakness).
    *   The plan's denial implicitly attempting to require further PT (by claiming "pathology has not materially changed") is in direct violation of this state law.
*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014 — Conservative Treatment Exceptions:** This guidance, while primarily focused on myelopathy, applies principles relevant to other time-sensitive neurological conditions. It states that plans SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment when:
    *   (a) Objective imaging evidence demonstrates progressive pathology ("interval increase in C5-C6 herniation size," "new foraminal stenosis" on October 2024 MRI).
    *   (b) Treating physician documentation indicates conservative treatment has failed to arrest progression ("functional plateau," "neurological deficit persistent and worsening," "interval worsening" of bicep weakness).
    *   (c) Surgical intervention is indicated under generally accepted clinical guidelines (as confirmed below).
    *   These conditions are met, further overriding any potential plan criteria for additional conservative treatment.
*   **CHSC § 1374.32(b) — Standard of Review for IMR:** This section mandates that if a service meets generally accepted standards of medical practice, it SHALL be approved regardless of whether the plan's internal criteria are more restrictive. As shown below, the service meets generally accepted clinical standards.

**4. Alignment with Generally Accepted Clinical Standards:**

*   **AANS/CNS Joint Section on Disorders of the Spine (2023 Update), NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020), and AO Spine International (2021):**
    *   Surgery (ACDF) is indicated for cervical radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and objective neurological deficits after an adequate trial of conservative treatment (TIER 2 indication per AANS/CNS guidelines).
    *   The member's presentation includes *worsening neurological deficit* (bicep weakness 3+/5 and absent reflex, interval worsening from prior exam) and *progressive pathology* on imaging (interval increase in herniation size and new foraminal stenosis), which represents a deteriorating condition despite conservative care. This aligns with a strong surgical indication (TIER 1 (b) in AANS/CNS Guidelines for progressive neurological deficit).
    *   The clinical record documents a robust 8-week course of new physical therapy, two pharmacotherapy agents, and an injection, all demonstrating failure to resolve symptoms or prevent neurological decline. Clinical guidelines do not support delaying surgery in the face of progressive neurological deficit after such a trial.

**Conclusion:**

The initial denial by HealthFirst Utilization Management was based on an invalid premise and failed to accurately assess the clinical status and the member's fulfillment of coverage criteria. The member's clinical presentation, including progressive symptoms and objective neurological deficits unresponsive to comprehensive conservative treatment, coupled with objective evidence of progressive structural pathology on imaging, strongly indicates medical necessity for the requested ACDF C5-C6. This is supported by HealthFirst's own criteria, California state laws, and generally accepted clinical standards.

Therefore, the prior authorization for CPT 22551 (ACDF C5-C6, one level) is **APPROVED**.
