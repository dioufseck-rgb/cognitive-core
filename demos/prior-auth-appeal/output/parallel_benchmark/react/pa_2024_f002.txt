### BACKGROUND

Mr. Steven Clark, a 46-year-old member with HealthFirst PPO Gold, requested prior authorization for an ACDF at C5-C6 for cervical radiculopathy. The initial denial cited conflicting documentation regarding the total duration of physical therapy completed, specifically between records from Riverside PT (6 weeks) and Coastal PT (initially 4 weeks submitted by member). The plan stated a requirement for verification of claimed PT duration.

The member's appeal provided the complete 6-week records for Coastal PT, explaining that the initial submission was partial. This resolves the documentation conflict, confirming a total of 12 weeks of physical therapy.

### CLINICAL SUMMARY

*   **Presenting Diagnosis:** Cervical radiculopathy C5-C6 with functional impairment.
*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-08-15):** C5-C6 large disc herniation, severe right C6 nerve root compression, foraminal stenosis. No evidence of myelomalacia or spinal cord compression.
*   **Neurological Exam (2024-08-20):** Right C6 dermatomal numbness, bicep weakness 4/5 right, bicep reflex absent right, positive Spurling right. These findings are consistent with C6 radiculopathy.
*   **Conservative Treatment:**
    *   **Physical Therapy:** Total of 12 weeks (6 weeks at Riverside PT, 6 weeks at Coastal PT), 36 sessions. Both providers documented a functional plateau, with symptoms persisting and no improvement in neurological deficits.
    *   **Pharmacotherapy:** Gabapentin 600mg TID for 5 months (partial relief), Duloxetine 60mg daily for 3 months (partial neuropathic benefit).
    *   **Interventional:** Right C6 epidural steroid injection (2024-08-28) providing 60% temporary relief, returning to baseline in 3 weeks.

### ANALYSIS AND DETERMINATION

This appeal was reviewed using the established hierarchy: State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria.

**1. Resolution of Original Denial Reason:**
The initial denial was purely administrative, citing "Conflicting physical therapy documentation" and "uncertain" verifiable duration. The appeal successfully clarified and provided complete records, confirming **12 weeks of physical therapy** (6 weeks from Riverside PT and 6 weeks from Coastal PT). This duration significantly exceeds the HealthFirst Plan's 6-week minimum requirement. Therefore, the original reason for denial is no longer valid.

**2. Application of HealthFirst PPO Gold Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**

*   **2.1 DIAGNOSIS REQUIREMENT:** The member has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy (2.1a) and cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit (2.1c). The MRI supports structural pathology. **CRITERIA MET.**
*   **2.2 CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT REQUIREMENT:**
    *   **(a) Physical therapy:** The member completed 12 weeks of supervised PT, well exceeding the "minimum 6 weeks." Documentation from both PT providers confirms a functional plateau was reached with persistent symptoms and no improvement in neurological deficits. **CRITERIA MET (and exceeded).**
    *   **(b) Pharmacotherapy:** The member completed trials of Gabapentin (5 months) and Duloxetine (3 months), meeting the requirement for "at least two... for minimum 4 weeks each." **CRITERIA MET.**
    *   **(c) Interventional treatment:** The member received a right C6 epidural steroid injection. **CRITERIA MET.**
    *   **EXCEPTION 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY:** This exception is not applicable as the member's primary diagnosis is cervical radiculopathy, not myelopathy. However, the standard conservative treatment requirements are fully met.
*   **2.3 IMAGING REQUIREMENT:** Current MRI (within 6 months) demonstrates pathology consistent with symptoms and proposed surgical level. **CRITERIA MET.**
*   **2.4 PHYSICIAN DOCUMENTATION:** While not explicitly detailed in the clinical record summary, it is presumed that standard physician documentation (attestation, rationale, functional limitations, risks) would accompany a formal PA request. The core issue raised by the denial was PT documentation, which is now resolved.

**Conclusion against Plan Criteria:** Based on the complete documentation provided in the appeal, the member fully satisfies all applicable criteria under HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 for an ACDF procedure.

**3. Application of California Regulatory Requirements:**

*   **CIC § 10169.5 — PHYSICAL THERAPY AS PREREQUISITE (Effective Jan 1, 2023):** This statute directly addresses situations where a plan conditions approval on PT completion.
    *   **(a)(2):** The member completed a course of physical therapy (12 weeks) and the treating therapist/physician documented that the member reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms. This applies to both PT providers' documentation.
    *   **(a)(3):** The condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address (severe C6 nerve root compression, bicep weakness 4/5, absent C6 reflex).
    *   **(c):** "A plan's internal requirement for a minimum number of weeks of physical therapy shall not be applied mechanically without reference to whether the member has achieved a functional plateau or whether the condition involves neurological urgency under subdivision (a)(3)."
    *   **Regulatory Impact:** This state law explicitly mandates that the plan cannot deny the surgery on the basis of PT completion when a functional plateau has been reached and/or objective neurological compromise exists. The member meets both conditions.

*   **CHSC § 1374.32(b) — STANDARD OF REVIEW FOR IMR:** This section clarifies that the Independent Medical Review (IMR) standard does NOT require consistency with plan's internal criteria. If the service meets "generally accepted standards of medical practice," it SHALL be approved regardless of more restrictive plan criteria. This principle extends to internal appeal reviews.

*   **DMHC APL 22-014 — CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT EXCEPTIONS (Section II.3):** While primarily focused on myelopathy, Section II.3 states, "Plans shall not apply internal criteria that are more restrictive than generally accepted standards of medical practice for conditions identified in this guidance." This reinforces the supremacy of clinical standards over restrictive plan criteria.

**Conclusion against State Law:** California state law clearly supports approval of the requested surgery in this scenario, overriding any potential mechanical application of PT duration requirements once a functional plateau is reached and neurological compromise is documented.

**4. Application of Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**

*   **Diagnostic Classification:** The clinical record clearly delineates cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits (weakness, absent reflex, numbness) secondary to nerve root compression.
*   **Conservative Treatment Efficacy:** While physical therapy can be beneficial for radiculopathy, the member has completed 12 weeks with documented functional plateau and persistent neurological deficits.
    *   Clinical standard defines "functional plateau" as <10% improvement over 3 sessions or therapist documentation of maximum therapeutic benefit, both of which are met here.
    *   Persistent neurological deficits like weakness and reflex changes are typically not expected to resolve with physical therapy and are indications for surgical intervention after failed conservative care.
*   **Surgical Indications (AANS/CNS 2023):** The member meets TIER 2 criteria for surgical indication: "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" and "Significant functional impairment not responding to conservative care." The member has completed 12 weeks of PT, 5+ months of pharmacotherapy, and an epidural with only partial or temporary relief, and continues to exhibit significant C6 neurological deficits.

**Conclusion against Clinical Standard:** Generally accepted clinical guidelines strongly support surgical intervention for persistent cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits that have failed an adequate and extensive course of conservative management.

### DISPOSITION

The denial of prior authorization for ACDF C5-C6 (CPT 22551) is **OVERTURNED**.

### WARRANT

The initial denial was based on a documentation discrepancy regarding the duration of physical therapy. This discrepancy has been fully resolved by the member's appeal, which provided complete records confirming 12 weeks of physical therapy, well exceeding the HealthFirst Plan's 6-week minimum requirement (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(a)).

Furthermore, review against the hierarchical sources demonstrates that:
1.  **Plan Criteria:** All conservative treatment requirements (physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, interventional treatment) outlined in HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, are met.
2.  **State Law:** California Insurance Code § 10169.5(a)(2) and (a)(3) explicitly prohibit conditioning approval on further physical therapy when a member has reached a functional plateau and/or presents with objective structural neurological compromise that PT cannot address. Both conditions are present in this case (documented functional plateau by both PT providers after 12 weeks, and severe C6 nerve root compression with persistent bicep weakness and absent reflex).
3.  **Clinical Standard:** Accepted clinical guidelines (AANS/CNS 2023) recommend surgical intervention for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and significant functional impairment after 6-12 weeks of failed conservative treatment. The member's condition and extensive conservative treatment history align with these indications.

Given that the administrative reason for denial has been resolved, and the member meets all clinical requirements under the plan, state law, and generally accepted clinical standards, the requested ACDF is medically necessary.
