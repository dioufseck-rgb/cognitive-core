**WARRANT FOR DETERMINATION:**

This appeal determination upholds the initial denial for CPT code 22551 (ACDF C5-C6) for member Stephanie Oduya. The decision is based on a comprehensive review of the submitted clinical documentation, HealthFirst's clinical coverage criteria, applicable California state laws and regulations, and generally accepted clinical standards of medical practice.

**1. Primary Clinical Presentation:**
The clinical record confirms a diagnosis of **cervical radiculopathy C5-C6, right-sided**, not cervical myelopathy.
*   **Imaging (MRI 2024-09-10):** Findings include "C5-C6 mild to moderate disc herniation. Right C6 nerve root contact without measurable compression. **No cord contact. No cord compression. No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia.** Mild right foraminal narrowing." (Clinical Record, Imaging)
*   **Neurological Examination (2024-09-15):** Findings include "Right shoulder pain and neck pain 6/10. RIGHT C6 dermatomal tingling — mild. Bicep reflex 2+ bilateral, symmetric. Grip strength 5/5 bilateral. **No weakness. No UMN signs.** Functional assessment: working full-time, no activity limitations." (Clinical Record, Neurological Exam)
*   **Clinical Standard:** The AANS/CNS and NASS guidelines differentiate cervical radiculopathy (nerve root compression, unilateral symptoms, pain, sensory change, weakness in single nerve distribution) from cervical myelopathy (spinal cord compression, bilateral symptoms, upper motor neuron signs, gait disturbance). The member's presentation aligns with radiculopathy and lacks any objective signs of myelopathy (no cord compression, no myelomalacia, no UMN signs, no objective weakness). (Clinical Standard, Section 1.1)

**2. Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024):**
The request for ACDF does not meet HealthFirst's conservative treatment requirements for cervical spine surgery.
*   **2.2 Conservative Treatment Requirement:**
    *   **(a) Physical Therapy:** A minimum of 6 weeks of supervised PT is required. The member completed 4 weeks of PT. This criterion is not met. (Plan Criteria, Section 2.2(a); Clinical Record, Conservative Treatment)
    *   **(b) Pharmacotherapy:** A trial of at least two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each is required. The member had a trial of Naproxen for 8 weeks. Only one agent is documented. This criterion is not met. (Plan Criteria, Section 2.2(b); Clinical Record, Conservative Treatment)
    *   **(c) Interventional Treatment:** One interventional treatment (e.g., epidural steroid injection) is required. The member has not received any interventional treatments. This criterion is not met. (Plan Criteria, Section 2.2(c); Clinical Record, Conservative Treatment)
*   **EXCEPTION 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY:** This exception reduces the PT requirement to 4 weeks and waives interventional treatment for confirmed cervical myelopathy (criterion 2.1(b)) *when myelopathy is the PRIMARY diagnosis*. As established above, the member's primary diagnosis is radiculopathy, and objective evidence for myelopathy (cord compression, myelomalacia, UMN signs, weakness) is explicitly absent. Therefore, the myelopathy exception does not apply. (Plan Criteria, Section 2.2(A) NOTE)

**3. Application of California Regulatory Requirements:**
California state laws and regulations do not override the plan's conservative treatment requirements for this specific case.
*   **CIC § 10169.5 (Physical Therapy as Prerequisite):**
    *   **(a)(1) Physician documentation of contraindication or unlikelihood of benefit:** Dr. Walsh elected to halt PT but did not document that it was contraindicated or unlikely to benefit the member given the nature and severity of the condition. (Clinical Record, Conservative Treatment)
    *   **(a)(2) Functional plateau:** The member completed 4 weeks of PT, not the plan's 6-week minimum. Furthermore, the PT summary notes "Mild improvement in pain" but does not document that the member reached a functional plateau, defined as "<10% improvement over 3 sessions OR therapist documents maximum benefit reached." (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2), (b); Clinical Standard, Section 5)
    *   **(a)(3) Objective evidence of structural neurological compromise:** The MRI explicitly states "No cord compression. No T2 signal change. No myelomalacia." This exception, which specifically identifies myelomalacia with cord compression as applicable, does not apply here. (CIC § 10169.5(a)(3); Clinical Record, Imaging)
*   **DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014 (Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions):** This guidance applies to "cervical myelopathy with objective evidence of cord compression and myelomalacia on MRI" or "progressive pathology" where delay risks irreversible harm. Since the clinical record clearly refutes the presence of myelopathy, cord compression, myelomalacia, or progressive pathology, this APL does not mandate a waiver of conservative treatment for this member. (DMHC APL 22-014, Sections II.1, II.2(a))
*   **CHSC § 1374.31(b) (Denial Notice Adequacy):** The denial notice specifies the reason ("Conservative treatment pathway not completed") and references clinical findings ("no cord compression and no neurological deficit on examination"), meeting procedural adequacy. (Denial, Reason Text)

**4. Application of Clinical Standards (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**
Generally accepted clinical standards support conservative management prior to surgical intervention for cervical radiculopathy without myelopathy or objective neurological deficits.
*   **Radiculopathy vs. Myelopathy Treatment:** Clinical guidelines clearly state that "Conservative treatment typically effective (70-80% resolution)" for radiculopathy, while "conservative treatment has LIMITED efficacy for established myelopathy." Applying myelopathy-specific guidelines to a radiculopathy case is a "category error." (Clinical Standard, Section 1.1)
*   **Myelomalacia Significance:** The presence of myelomalacia (T2 signal change) is a "strong indication for surgical intervention and a contraindication to extended conservative treatment trials." The member's MRI explicitly shows **no myelomalacia**. (Clinical Standard, Section 1.2)
*   **Conservative Treatment for Radiculopathy:** For radiculopathy, the clinical standard supports a conservative trial. NASS Guidelines (2020) indicate that surgery is a "reasonable option" for "Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms after 6-12 weeks conservative tx" (Clinical Standard, Section 3.1, TIER 2(b)). The member has only completed 4 weeks of PT and one pharmacotherapy agent, which falls short of the recommended duration for a conservative trial prior to surgery for radiculopathy.
*   **Lack of Surgical Urgency:** The member's neurological exam shows "No weakness. No UMN signs. Functional assessment: working full-time, no activity limitations." This does not meet the "TIER 1 — STRONG INDICATION" criteria for immediate surgery, which would include moderate to severe myelopathy, progressive neurological deficit, myelomalacia, or acute severe myelopathy. (Clinical Standard, Section 3.1)

**5. Resolution of Conflicts and Conclusion:**
There is no conflict between the plan's criteria, California state law, and generally accepted clinical standards for this case. All sources are consistent in requiring further conservative treatment for a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy without evidence of myelopathy, myelomalacia, or objective neurological deficit. The treating surgeon's clinical judgment and advocacy, while respected, must be supported by objective clinical findings that meet established medical necessity criteria and generally accepted standards of medical practice. The clinical record, in this instance, does not support immediate surgical intervention over further conservative care.

Therefore, the denial of prior authorization for CPT 22551 is consistent with the member's clinical presentation, HealthFirst's medical necessity criteria, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted clinical standards of care.
