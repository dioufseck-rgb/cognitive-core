The prior authorization denial for a three-level Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) for Ms. Catherine Mills, initially denied by HealthFirst on November 25, 2024, is **OVERTURNED**.

**III. REVIEW FINDINGS AND DETERMINATION**

**A. Primary Clinical Presentation:**
Ms. Mills exhibits clear signs and symptoms of **moderate to severe cervical myelopathy**, primarily centered at the C4-C5 level, with associated radiculopathy. Key indicators include:
*   **MRI findings:** Severe cord compression and the presence of myelomalacia (T2 signal change) at C4-C5.
*   **Neurological exam:** Objective upper motor neuron signs (bilateral hyperreflexia, bilateral Hoffman sign, wide-based gait), and bilateral upper extremity weakness (C5, C6).
*   **Diagnosis:** Multi-level cervical spondylosis with myeloradiculopathy, with myelopathy being the predominant and most critical diagnosis given the cord compression and myelomalacia.

**B. Application of HealthFirst Plan Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024):**

1.  **2.1 DIAGNOSIS REQUIREMENT:** The member has confirmed cervical myelopathy (2.1(b)) and cervical radiculopathy (2.1(a)), with supporting MRI within 6 months. **Meets.**
2.  **2.2 CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT REQUIREMENT:**
    *   The member completed 9 weeks of PT (exceeds standard 6-week minimum), two pharmacotherapy agents for 3-4 months each (exceeds 4-week minimum), and one interventional procedure.
    *   Therefore, the member technically **meets** HealthFirst's conservative treatment duration and modality requirements, even without applying the myelopathy exception.
    *   *Note:* While met, the underlying validity of these requirements for myelopathy is challenged by higher authorities (see Section C & D below). The denial, however, did not cite failure of conservative treatment.
3.  **2.3 IMAGING REQUIREMENT:** The MRI demonstrates structural pathology at C4-C5 (severe cord compression, myelomalacia), and C5-C6 (moderate disc herniation, nerve root compression). For C3-C4, mild disc bulge and minimal foraminal narrowing are noted. The plan's denial was based on the interpretation that C3-C4 and C5-C6 did "not demonstrate pathology of sufficient severity."

**C. Application of California Regulatory Requirements:**

1.  **DMHC All-Plan Letter APL 22-014 (September 2022) — Myelopathy and Urgent Conditions:**
    *   This guidance mandates that cervical myelopathy with objective evidence of cord compression and myelomalacia (as seen in Ms. Mills at C4-C5) SHALL be treated as urgent (II.1).
    *   It further states that plans SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment where objective imaging shows myelomalacia, and the treating physician documents conservative failure and surgical indication (II.2). Ms. Mills' condition and the documentation meet these criteria.
    *   **This state regulation explicitly overrides HealthFirst's conservative treatment requirements for Ms. Mills' myelopathy.**
2.  **California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5 (Effective January 1, 2023) — Physical Therapy as Prerequisite:**
    *   A health insurer cannot condition surgery approval on PT completion where:
        *   **(a)(3)** The condition involves "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." Ms. Mills' myelomalacia and severe cord compression at C4-C5 clearly meet this criterion, rendering mandatory PT completion invalid for the myelopathic component.
        *   **(a)(2)** The member "reached a functional plateau without adequate resolution of symptoms." The PT summary explicitly notes "Myelopathic symptoms stable but not improved" and "C5-C6 radicular symptoms persist" after 9 weeks, indicating a functional plateau.
    *   **This state law overrides any mechanical application of a minimum PT duration.**
3.  **California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32(b) — Independent Medical Review Standard:**
    *   This crucial regulation states that an appeal must determine if the service is medically necessary using "generally accepted standards of medical practice," and that the plan's internal criteria SHALL NOT be the sole or determinative basis if they are more restrictive.
    *   **This means HealthFirst's restrictive "sufficient severity" criterion for C3-C4 and C5-C6 must be evaluated against broader clinical standards.**
4.  **CHSC § 1374.31(b) — Denial Notice Adequacy:** The denial notice provided a reason based on "review of imaging and clinical record" but failed to specify the *clinical criteria applied* or specific *sections of its own policy*. While not the primary basis for this overturn, this constitutes a procedural defect in the denial.

**D. Application of Clinical Standards (AANS/CNS 2023, NASS 2020, AO Spine 2021):**

1.  **Myelomalacia Significance:** Myelomalacia (T2 signal change) indicates existing cord injury, signifies a strong surgical indication, is a predictor of permanent deficit if surgery is delayed, and is a contraindication to extended conservative treatment trials (AANS/CNS 1.2, 2.3).
2.  **Conservative Treatment for Myelopathy:**
    *   The **AO Spine Guidelines (2021)** explicitly state, "There is no high-quality evidence supporting a mandatory course of physical therapy prior to surgical intervention for patients with moderate to severe cervical myelopathy. Delaying surgery in such patients to complete a physical therapy trial is not supported by the evidence and may result in neurological deterioration" (AO Spine 2.1).
    *   For moderate-severe myelopathy, physical therapy does NOT produce meaningful functional improvement (Cochrane Review 2021, NASS 2020 Grade B Evidence).
    *   **These guidelines clearly demonstrate that the clinical basis for requiring conservative treatment for Ms. Mills' myelopathy is absent, and delay is harmful.**
3.  **Surgical Indications for ACDF (CPT 22551):**
    *   Per **AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023)**, Ms. Mills' condition at C4-C5 (moderate to severe myelopathy with objective cord compression on MRI, and myelomalacia) meets **TIER 1 — STRONG INDICATION** for surgery (AANS/CNS 3.1(a), 3.1(c)).
    *   **Regarding the denied levels:**
        *   **C5-C6:** The member has moderate disc herniation, moderate left C6 nerve root compression, C6 weakness, diminished reflex, and persistent radicular symptoms despite a comprehensive conservative trial (9 weeks PT, 4 months pharmacotherapy, epidural). This unequivocally meets **TIER 2 — REASONABLE INDICATION** for surgery for radiculopathy that has failed conservative treatment (AANS/CNS 3.1(b)). The plan's denial of this level for "insufficient severity" directly conflicts with generally accepted clinical standards for symptomatic radiculopathy unresponsive to conservative care.
        *   **C3-C4:** While the imaging (mild disc bulge, minimal foraminal narrowing) and neurological exam (unclear C4 level contribution) are less severe than at other levels, the member's treating neurosurgeon has documented rationale for including this level in the fusion, citing "all three levels are symptomatic." In the context of a severe, multi-level myelopathic process requiring surgical intervention, the inclusion of an adjacent, mildly symptomatic level in a single-stage procedure is often considered a prudent clinical decision to achieve comprehensive decompression, stabilization, and prevent adjacent segment disease. Such surgical planning falls within "generally accepted standards of medical practice" (CHSC 1374.32(b)), which prioritizes the overall clinical benefit and long-term stability for a complex neurological condition, rather than a restrictive, isolated assessment of each level's pathology.

**E. Conflict Resolution and Disposition:**

Applying the three-source hierarchy (State law > Clinical standard > Plan criteria):

1.  **Conservative Treatment Requirements:** HealthFirst's conservative treatment requirements for myelopathy are superseded by California state law (DMHC APL 22-014, CIC § 10169.5) and current clinical standards (AO Spine, AANS/CNS) which emphasize the urgency of intervention for myelopathy with myelomalacia and negate the requirement for extended conservative trials. Even if this were the reason for denial, it would be overturned.
2.  **"Sufficient Severity" for C3-C4 and C5-C6:** HealthFirst's denial on the basis of "insufficient severity" for C3-C4 and C5-C6 is more restrictive than "generally accepted standards of medical practice" (CHSC § 1374.32(b)).
    *   For **C5-C6**, the objective findings (moderate disc herniation, nerve root compression, C6 weakness) and failure of a robust conservative trial strongly support surgical intervention per clinical guidelines.
    *   For **C3-C4**, while objectively milder, the treating neurosurgeon's professional judgment to include this level within a comprehensive three-level fusion for multi-level myelopathy, given documented patient symptomatology at all levels, is consistent with generally accepted surgical principles for optimizing outcomes and preventing future issues in such complex cases.

Therefore, the plan's denial is not supported by higher authorities.

**V. WARRANT**

The prior authorization denial for ACDF C3-C4, C4-C5, and C5-C6 for Ms. Catherine Mills is **OVERTURNED**.

Ms. Mills presents with objectively confirmed moderate to severe cervical myelopathy at C4-C5, marked by severe cord compression and myelomalacia on MRI, accompanied by definitive upper motor neuron signs. This condition is a **TIER 1 strong surgical indication** under AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Guidelines (2020), and myelomalacia specifically denotes an existing cord injury with a high risk of permanent deficit if surgery is delayed (AANS/CNS 1.2, 2.3). The AO Spine Guidelines (2021) explicitly contraindicate delaying surgery for physical therapy in such cases, stating it may result in neurological deterioration (AO Spine 2.1).

California state law further reinforces the urgency and treatment pathway:
1.  **DMHC APL 22-014 (II.1, II.2)** mandates that cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia be treated as urgent and waives the requirement for full conservative treatment.
2.  **CIC § 10169.5(a)(3)** exempts the physical therapy requirement when objective structural neurological compromise (like myelomalacia) is present. Additionally, the member has reached a functional plateau for myelopathic symptoms (CIC § 10169.5(a)(2)).

Regarding the denied levels (C3-C4 and C5-C6) due to "insufficient severity" by HealthFirst:
1.  **C5-C6:** The clinical record demonstrates moderate disc herniation, moderate left C6 nerve root compression, left C6 weakness, diminished reflex, and *persistent radicular symptoms* despite a thoroughly documented 9-week course of physical therapy, 4 months of pharmacotherapy, and an epidural. This presentation clearly meets criteria for surgical intervention for symptomatic radiculopathy unresponsive to conservative treatment, aligning with **TIER 2 indications** of AANS/CNS guidelines (3.1(b)). Denying this level based on "insufficient severity" is a more restrictive application of criteria than generally accepted standards of medical practice (CHSC § 1374.32(b)).
2.  **C3-C4:** While imaging findings at C3-C4 are milder, the treating neurosurgeon has documented reasoning for including this level in the three-level fusion, and the appeal states "all three levels are symptomatic." In the context of severe, multi-level cervical myelopathy that warrants surgical intervention, a surgeon's decision to include an adjacent, mildly symptomatic or contributing level in a single surgical stage is often considered prudent to provide comprehensive decompression, achieve spinal stability, and potentially mitigate future adjacent segment disease. This approach is consistent with "generally accepted standards of medical practice" for complex spinal conditions (CHSC § 1374.32(b)), which prioritizes the treating physician's clinical judgment in determining the optimal scope of a necessary procedure to address the overall pathology and prevent further neurological deterioration.

The plan's denial therefore fails to adequately account for California state law and generally accepted clinical standards, which dictate a more urgent and comprehensive approach to multi-level cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia. The internal plan criteria and its application are more restrictive than allowed by law and evidence.
