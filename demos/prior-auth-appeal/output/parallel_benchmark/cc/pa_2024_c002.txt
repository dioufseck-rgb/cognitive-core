Dear David Santos,

HealthFirst has completed the review of your appeal regarding the prior authorization request for Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 (CPT 22551). After reviewing the clinical records, plan criteria, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, we have determined that the initial denial was incorrect. The request for surgery is APPROVED.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 63-year-old male presenting with mixed cervical myelopathy and radiculopathy. Imaging via MRI (dated 09/01/2024) demonstrates moderate spinal cord compression at C6-C7 with associated T2 signal change, also known as myelomalacia. Your neurological examination (dated 09/05/2024) confirmed objective neurological deficits, including bilateral hyperreflexia, a positive right Hoffman sign, and a mildly wide-based gait, alongside right-sided C7 radicular weakness (4/5 triceps).

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
The initial denial (dated 10/05/2024) cited Section 2.2 of the Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), stating that the "Myelopathy Exception" did not apply because radiculopathy was considered your "primary diagnosis." 

However, this determination is overturned based on two distinct findings:
1. Factual Completion: Clinical records from your physical therapy provider confirm you completed 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy (18 sessions) using a myelopathy protocol. This satisfies the standard conservative treatment requirement under Section 2.2(a) for any cervical diagnosis, regardless of which is deemed "primary."
2. Criteria Misapplication: You have trialed two neuropathic/NSAID agents (Pregabalin and Naproxen) for over 4 weeks each and underwent a C7 epidural steroid injection on 09/28/2024. These actions satisfy Sections 2.2(b) and 2.2(c) of the plan criteria.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND HIERARCHY OF AUTHORITY
Under the hierarchy of authority, California State Law and Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC) directives supersede internal plan criteria. 

- California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3): This statute prohibits a health plan from conditioning surgical approval on the completion of physical therapy when the condition involves "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." Your documented myelomalacia (cord signal change) constitutes such compromise.
- DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: This directive states that plans SHALL NOT require a full course of conservative treatment for conditions where delay risks irreversible harm. The Department specifically identifies myelomalacia on MRI as a finding that supports the waiver of standard conservative treatment requirements.

The plan's internal "primary diagnosis" restriction is a violation of these regulatory frameworks. The presence of myelomalacia triggers a regulatory override of the plan's conservative treatment prerequisites.

CLINICAL STANDARD ANALYSIS
According to the AANS/CNS Joint Section Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Guidelines (2020), the presence of cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia is classified as a Tier 1 Strong Indication for surgical intervention. These standards note that conservative treatment has limited efficacy for established myelopathy and that delaying surgery in the presence of cord signal change increases the risk of permanent neurological deficit.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICT
The conflict between the plan's internal restriction (requiring myelopathy to be the "primary" diagnosis) and the clinical reality of your condition is resolved in favor of the member. The plan's restriction is found to be more restrictive than both California law (CIC § 10169.5) and generally accepted standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS 2023). Per CHSC § 1374.32, the clinical standard and state law govern the determination.

WARRANT
The prior authorization is overturned because the member has objectively documented structural neurological compromise (myelomalacia) which, under CIC § 10169.5 and DMHC APL 22-014, waives the plan's ability to mandate further conservative treatment. Furthermore, the member has factually satisfied the plan's most stringent 6-week physical therapy requirement.
