DISPOSITION: OVERTURNED

Dear Mr. Webb,

HealthFirst has completed the independent medical review of your appeal regarding the prior authorization request for an Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7 (CPT 22551). After a comprehensive review of the clinical records, internal plan criteria, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, the initial denial is OVERTURNED. Authorization for the requested procedure is granted.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
Clinical documentation dated July 1, 2024, describes a 47-year-old male presenting with bilateral hand weakness (3/5 grip strength), coordination difficulties, and an impaired tandem gait. Physical examination revealed objective upper motor neuron (UMN) signs, including a bilateral positive Hoffman sign and hyperreflexia. An MRI of the cervical spine dated July 10, 2024, confirmed a disc herniation at C6-C7 with moderate cord compression and T2 signal change within the spinal cord, diagnostic of early myelomalacia. You completed four weeks of physical therapy, which was halted by your treating physician, Dr. Sarah Chen, who documented that further therapy was contraindicated due to the risk of neurological deterioration.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
The initial denial (MED-003) was based on HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, which requires six weeks of physical therapy. The plan further asserted that the "Myelopathy Exception" (Section 2.2A), which reduces the requirement to four weeks, did not apply because the primary ICD-10 billing code used (M47.812) referred to radiculopathy rather than myelopathy. 

However, this review finds that the plan’s administrative reliance on billing codes over objective clinical findings is inconsistent with its own criteria. Section 2.1(b) defines cervical myelopathy based on symptoms (gait disturbance, weakness, hyperreflexia) and imaging findings, all of which are clearly documented in your clinical record. The clinical narrative and objective imaging findings of myelomalacia satisfy the Myelopathy Pathway requirements, rendering the 4-week PT trial sufficient under Section 2.2(A).

REGULATORY OVERRIDE AND COMPLIANCE
Independent of the plan's internal criteria, California law provides a mandatory override of conservative treatment requirements in this case:

1. California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3): This statute prohibits a health insurer from conditioning surgical approval on the completion of physical therapy when the condition involves "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." The documented myelomalacia (cord signal change) on your MRI constitutes definitive structural compromise, making the plan's 6-week PT requirement legally unenforceable.

2. DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: This regulatory mandate specifies that for conditions where delay risks irreversible harm, such as cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia, plans "SHALL NOT require completion of a full course of conservative treatment." The presence of myelomalacia is specifically identified as a finding that supports the waiver of standard requirements.

3. Procedural Defect (CHSC § 1374.31): The initial denial notice failed to include the required notice of Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights. Under California Health & Safety Code § 1374.31(b), a denial notice that fails to provide this information is considered defective. This procedural omission provides an independent basis for reversal of the denial.

CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Guidelines (2020), classify cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia as a "Tier 1 — Strong Indication" for surgical intervention. These guidelines state that for moderate-to-severe myelopathy, surgical decompression is the primary treatment and that delaying surgery to complete physical therapy is not supported by evidence and may result in permanent neurological damage.

WARRANT FOR OVERTURN
The denial is overturned because the plan’s mechanical application of a 6-week physical therapy requirement ignored both the objective clinical evidence of myelomalacia and the governing California regulatory framework. Under CIC § 10169.5 and APL 22-014, the presence of structural neurological compromise necessitates a waiver of conservative treatment prerequisites. Furthermore, the plan's failure to provide notice of IMR rights constitutes a procedural defect that invalidates the denial. The requested ACDF is medically necessary and clinically urgent.
