CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 58-year-old female presenting with symptoms and physical findings consistent with cervical myelopathy. A neurological examination on September 5, 2024, documented five objective upper motor neuron (UMN) signs: bilateral Hoffman sign, bilateral hyperreflexia in the upper extremities, sustained bilateral ankle clonus, a positive finger escape sign, and a wide-based gait. An MRI dated September 1, 2024, demonstrated a disc herniation at C5-C6 with documented "mild cord contact."

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
The initial denial was based on HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024. Specifically:
1. Section 2.1(b) requires "spinal cord compression" for a myelopathy diagnosis. The initial reviewer determined that "mild cord contact" did not meet this threshold.
2. Section 2.2 requires 6 weeks of physical therapy (PT). Because the myelopathy diagnosis was rejected, the reviewer applied the standard 6-week requirement rather than the 4-week Myelopathy Pathway (Exception 2.2(A)).

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL STANDARD ANALYSIS
Under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32, health plans must determine medical necessity based on generally accepted standards of medical practice. If a plan's internal criteria are more restrictive than these standards, the clinical standard must prevail (CHSC § 1374.32(b)).

1. Diagnostic Standard: Generally accepted clinical guidelines, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and AO Spine International Guidelines (2021), establish that a diagnosis of cervical myelopathy is primarily clinical, defined by the presence of objective UMN signs. These standards recognize "imaging-negative" or "minimal-contact" myelopathy where clinical signs precede quantifiable compression on standard MRI sequences. The presence of five objective UMN signs in your case is definitive for clinical myelopathy.
2. Conservative Treatment: California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3) prohibits conditioning surgical approval on the completion of PT when the condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise. Furthermore, AO Spine Guidelines (2021) state that delaying surgery for PT in patients with moderate-to-severe myelopathy is not supported by evidence and risks neurological deterioration.

CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND WARRANT
A conflict exists between the plan's requirement for measurable "compression" (CC-SPINE-2024 2.1(b)) and the generally accepted medical standard which recognizes UMN signs and cord contact as diagnostic. Per CHSC § 1374.32, the clinical standard governs. 

Because clinical myelopathy is established, the Myelopathy Pathway (Exception 2.2(A)) applies, reducing the PT requirement to 4 weeks. Your completion of 5 weeks of PT satisfies this requirement. Additionally, the severity of your neurological findings (sustained clonus and gait disturbance) renders the 6-week PT requirement invalid under CIC § 10169.5.

The evidence confirms you have a condition (cervical myelopathy) for which ACDF is the standard of care. The denial is overturned because the plan applied criteria more restrictive than California law and generally accepted medical practice allow.
