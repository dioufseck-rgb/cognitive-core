HealthFirst has completed the clinical appeal review for the requested two-level anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6 and C6-C7. Our decision is a PARTIAL OVERTURN.

DISPOSITION
- C5-C6 ACDF: OVERTURNED (Approved)
- C6-C7 ACDF: UPHELD (Denied)

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 45-year-old female with a diagnosis of C5-C6 cervical radiculopathy. Clinical records from Dr. James Wilson and imaging dated September 1, 2024, confirm a large disc herniation at C5-C6 causing moderate-to-severe nerve root compression. This correlates with your physical exam findings of right C6 dermatomal numbness, diminished bicep reflex, and 4/5 grip strength. You have completed 8 weeks of physical therapy, trialed Gabapentin and Cyclobenzaprine, and received a C6 epidural steroid injection with only temporary relief.

Regarding the C6-C7 level, imaging shows only a mild disc bulge and minimal foraminal narrowing without nerve root compression or cord contact. Your neurological exam did not identify any symptoms (such as C7-specific weakness or numbness) attributable to this level.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
For the C5-C6 level, the request meets HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
- Section 2.1: Confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy with correlating neurological deficits.
- Section 2.2: Completion of >6 weeks of supervised physical therapy, two pharmacotherapy agents, and one interventional treatment.
- Section 2.3: Imaging demonstrates structural pathology consistent with symptoms at the proposed level.

For the C6-C7 level, the request fails to meet criteria:
- Section 2.3 & 3.3: Imaging does not demonstrate pathology at the proposed surgical level consistent with the clinical presentation. The plan requires level-specific correlation for multi-level procedures.

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL STANDARD REVIEW
In accordance with California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32, we evaluated this request against generally accepted standards of medical practice. 

1. Clinical Standard: The AANS/CNS Joint Section Guidelines (2023) Tier 1 supports surgery for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and correlating imaging (satisfied for C5-C6). However, Tier 3 specifically states that surgery is NOT indicated for imaging findings without correlating symptoms (applicable to C6-C7).
2. Prophylactic Fusion: Dr. Wilson requested the C6-C7 level to prevent Adjacent Segment Disease (ASD). While ASD is a known surgical consideration, prophylactic fusion of an asymptomatic level with minimal imaging changes does not meet the definition of "medical necessity" under CHSC § 1374.32(a)(2), as it is not considered clinically appropriate or effective for treating an existing illness or injury at that specific site.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
California law mandates that clinical standards supersede restrictive plan criteria. In this case, both the HealthFirst criteria and the AANS/CNS clinical standards reach the same conclusion: surgical intervention is indicated for the symptomatic C5-C6 level but is not supported for the asymptomatic C6-C7 level.

WARRANT
The C5-C6 level is approved because you have exhausted conservative management and have objective imaging/clinical correlation. The C6-C7 level remains denied because there is no documented neurological deficit or significant compression at that level; prophylactic stabilization for ASD prevention, in the absence of current pathology, does not meet the medical necessity standard.
