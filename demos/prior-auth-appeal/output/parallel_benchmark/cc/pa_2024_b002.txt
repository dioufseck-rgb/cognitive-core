HealthFirst has completed the clinical and regulatory review of your appeal regarding the denial of prior authorization for a C6-C7 Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF). Authorization for the requested procedure is granted.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
Mr. Reed is a 52-year-old male presenting with persistent cervical radiculopathy at the C6-C7 level. Clinical documentation confirms a right C7 dermatomal distribution of numbness and a diminished right tricep reflex. Pain is reported at 7/10 at rest. Imaging (MRI dated 08/01/2024) demonstrates a C6-C7 disc herniation with mild cord contact and right C7 foraminal stenosis. Conservative treatment has included seven weeks of supervised physical therapy (21 sessions), four months of Pregabalin (150mg BID), three months of Meloxicam, and a right C7 epidural steroid injection (09/20/2024) which provided only transient relief.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION (CC-SPINE-2024)
The initial denial stated that physical therapy (PT) was not completed to a functional plateau and that imaging did not show measurable compression. Our review finds these conclusions inconsistent with the provided records:
1. Section 2.1(a): The member has a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy with correlating neurological deficits (C7 numbness/reflex change).
2. Section 2.2(a): The member completed seven weeks of PT, exceeding the six-week minimum. The PT discharge summary explicitly states a "functional plateau" was reached.
3. Section 2.2(b) & (c): Requirements for pharmacotherapy and interventional treatment were fully satisfied.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND HIERARCHY OF AUTHORITY
This determination is governed by the California Regulatory Framework, which supersedes internal plan criteria where conflicts exist.

1. California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5: This statute prohibits a health insurer from conditioning surgical approval on the completion of further PT if the member has already reached a functional plateau. The plan’s insistence on further PT despite the documented plateau in the PT discharge summary is a direct violation of CIC § 10169.5(a)(2).
2. California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b): The initial denial was based on a factual error (stating PT plateau was not reached). Under California law, a denial notice that relies on incorrect clinical facts is procedurally defective.
3. Hierarchy Resolution: While the procedural defect under CHSC § 1374.31 might typically suggest a REMAND for re-evaluation, the substantive violation of CIC § 10169.5 and the clear clinical evidence of medical necessity require an immediate OVERTURN.

CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020), support surgical intervention for cervical radiculopathy when symptoms persist after 6–12 weeks of failed conservative treatment and imaging demonstrates correlating structural pathology. 

The plan’s denial cited a lack of "measurable compression" on imaging. However, the clinical standard for ACDF is based on symptom-imaging correlation, not an arbitrary measurement of cord displacement. Mr. Reed’s C7 neurological deficits correlate directly with the C6-C7 herniation and foraminal stenosis identified on MRI. Per DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014, plans may not apply internal criteria that are more restrictive than these generally accepted clinical standards.

WARRANT FOR OVERTURN
The denial must be overturned because the member has exhausted all required conservative treatments, reached a documented functional plateau (satisfying CIC § 10169.5), and exhibits objective neurological deficits that correlate with structural pathology on imaging. The plan's more restrictive imaging threshold and factual error regarding PT status are overridden by California law and the clinical standard of care.
