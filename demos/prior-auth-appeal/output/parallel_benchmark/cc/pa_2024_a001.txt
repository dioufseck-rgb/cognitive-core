Dear Diana Torres,

HealthFirst Health Plan has completed the appeal review for the previously denied anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C5-C6. After reviewing the clinical records, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, we have determined that the initial denial must be OVERTURNED. The requested procedure is authorized.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
Ms. Torres is a 61-year-old female presenting with a four-month history of progressive cervical myelopathy. Clinical examination on August 10, 2024, documented significant neurological deficits, including bilateral Hoffman signs, hyperreflexia in the upper and lower extremities, positive bilateral Babinski signs, and gait ataxia. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) dated August 15, 2024, demonstrated severe central cord compression at C5-C6 with associated T2 hyperintensity within the spinal cord (myelomalacia), indicating objective structural injury.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
The initial denial was based on Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, which requires a minimum of 6 weeks of physical therapy (PT) and a trial of two pharmacotherapy agents. While Section 2.2(A) (Myelopathy Pathway) reduces the PT requirement to 4 weeks, the initial reviewer determined that the member’s completion of only 2 weeks of PT and one pharmacotherapy agent (methylprednisolone) did not satisfy these internal requirements.

REGULATORY PROVISIONS
Under California law, the plan’s internal conservative treatment requirements are superseded by the following provisions:
1. California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3): A health insurer shall not condition approval of a surgical procedure on completion of physical therapy where the condition involves objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address. The documented myelomalacia on the August 15 MRI constitutes such compromise.
2. CIC § 10169.5(a)(1): PT requirements are prohibited when the treating physician documents that PT is contraindicated. Dr. Kim’s declaration dated October 15, 2024, explicitly states PT is contraindicated due to the risk of irreversible cord injury.
3. DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: Plans shall not require completion of conservative treatment for conditions where delay risks irreversible harm, specifically citing myelomalacia as a finding that supports a waiver of such requirements.

CLINICAL STANDARD ALIGNMENT
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and AO Spine International Clinical Practice Guidelines (2021), classify moderate-to-severe cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia as a Tier 1 "Strong Indication" for surgical decompression. These guidelines state that delaying surgery to complete physical therapy is not supported by evidence and may result in permanent neurological deterioration.

CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND WARRANT
A conflict exists between the Plan’s internal criteria (CC-SPINE-2024) and California law. Per California Health & Safety Code § 1374.32(b), the clinical standard and state law govern over more restrictive internal plan criteria. The prior authorization denial is overturned because the plan’s requirement for continued physical therapy is legally unenforceable under CIC § 10169.5(a) and clinically contraindicated by the presence of myelomalacia and progressive neurological deficits.
