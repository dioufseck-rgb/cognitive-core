CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 44-year-old member with a diagnosis of C6-C7 cervical radiculopathy. An MRI dated August 15, 2024, confirmed a C6-C7 disc herniation with moderate right C7 nerve root compression. A neurological examination on August 20, 2024, documented objective neurological deficits, specifically 4/5 strength in the right tricep and a diminished tricep reflex. You completed 4 weeks (12 sessions) of physical therapy (PT) and a 6-week course of Ibuprofen with only partial relief. You reported reaching a functional plateau during PT, as your neurological symptoms (weakness) persisted despite treatment.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, the plan typically requires 6 weeks of physical therapy and a trial of two different medications before authorizing surgery. While your records show only 4 weeks of PT and one medication trial, California state law overrides these internal plan requirements in your specific clinical case.

REGULATORY DETERMINATION
This appeal is overturned based on the following mandatory provisions of California law:

1. Procedural Defect and Deemed Approval (CHSC § 1374.31(b) and § 1367.01): The initial denial notice issued on October 5, 2024, was legally defective. It failed to cite the specific clinical criteria applied and failed to include the mandatory notice of your right to an Independent Medical Review (IMR), as required by California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(a). Under CHSC § 1374.31(b), a defective notice is treated as if "no denial was issued." Because the plan failed to issue a valid decision within the 5-business-day window required by CHSC § 1367.01, the request is "DEEMED APPROVED" per the regulatory framework (Part IV.3).

2. Statutory Override of Physical Therapy Requirements (CIC § 10169.5): The plan’s insistence on 6 weeks of PT is legally unenforceable for two reasons:
   - Objective Neurological Compromise: California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3) prohibits a plan from conditioning surgery on the completion of PT when there is objective evidence of structural neurological compromise. Your documented 4/5 tricep weakness constitutes such compromise, waiving the PT requirement.
   - Functional Plateau: CIC § 10169.5(a)(2) states that PT requirements cannot be used to deny surgery if a member has reached a functional plateau. Your persistent 4/5 weakness after 12 sessions of therapy meets the clinical definition of a functional plateau (Clinical Evidence Document, Section 5).

CLINICAL STANDARD RESOLUTION
While generally accepted clinical standards (AANS/CNS 2023) often suggest 6–12 weeks of conservative care for radiculopathy, these guidelines are subordinate to the specific protections afforded by California statutes (CIC § 10169.5). The presence of objective motor weakness necessitates a more urgent surgical consideration than standard elective cases.

WARRANT
The denial is overturned because the plan issued a procedurally defective notice which, by law, results in a deemed approval of the service. Furthermore, the plan’s substantive basis for denial (failure to complete 6 weeks of PT) is prohibited by CIC § 10169.5 due to your documented neurological deficits and functional plateau.
