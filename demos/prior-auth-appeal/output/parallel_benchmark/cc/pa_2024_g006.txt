HealthFirst has completed the formal appeal review of the prior authorization denial for an anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at the C5-C6 level, requested by Dr. Victor Osei. 

DISPOSITION: OVERTURN

HealthFirst’s initial decision to deny this request is reversed. The requested procedure is medically necessary and is authorized for performance at UCSF Medical Center.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
Review of your clinical record demonstrates a confirmed diagnosis of progressive cervical radiculopathy at the C5-C6 level. A current MRI dated October 15, 2024, shows a large disc herniation with moderate-to-severe right C6 nerve root compression and new foraminal stenosis. This represents a documented interval increase in size compared to your prior MRI from April 2024. A neurological examination on October 20, 2024, documented objective deficits, including an absent right bicep reflex and 3+/5 bicep weakness, which indicates worsening clinical status. You have completed an 8-week course of physical therapy (July–September 2024) with a documented functional plateau, a 6-month trial of Gabapentin, two courses of oral steroids, and a C6 epidural steroid injection on November 5, 2024, which provided only temporary relief.

PLAN CRITERIA COMPLIANCE
The request satisfies all requirements of HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
- Section 2.1 (Diagnosis): Met via confirmed C5-C6 radiculopathy with dermatomal weakness and numbness.
- Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment): Met via completion of 8 weeks of supervised physical therapy (exceeding the 6-week requirement), two classes of pharmacotherapy (neuropathic agents and corticosteroids), and one interventional procedure (epidural injection).
- Section 2.3 (Imaging): Met via MRI within 6 months demonstrating pathology correlating with the C5-C6 surgical level.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
The initial denial issued on November 20, 2024, was found to be procedurally and legally deficient under California law:
1. CHSC § 1374.31(b): The denial is "defective" as it failed to cite valid clinical criteria. The plan’s reliance on "prior authorization history" is not an authorized denial basis under CC-SPINE-2024.
2. CIC § 10169.5: California law prohibits conditioning surgery on physical therapy when a member has reached a "functional plateau" or has "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise." Your record confirms both a plateau and significant neurological deficit (3+/5 weakness).

CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
Under generally accepted standards of medical practice (AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines, 2023), your presentation meets "Tier 1 — Strong Indication" for surgical intervention due to a progressive neurological deficit and imaging evidence of worsening cord/root compression. The clinical standard mandates intervention in these cases to prevent irreversible neurological damage.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
The initial reviewer’s assertion that your pathology had not "materially changed" is directly contradicted by the comparative MRI findings (Oct 2024 vs. April 2024) and the worsening neurological exam. Furthermore, the prior denial in May 2024 was for a different, three-level procedure; that history is irrelevant to the medical necessity of the current single-level request. Per the hierarchy of authority, California statutory requirements (CIC § 10169.5) and the clinical standard of care override the plan’s misapplication of internal administrative history.

WARRANT
The denial must be overturned because the member meets all internal plan criteria, the denial notice was procedurally defective under state law, and the clinical standard of care requires surgery for progressive neurological deficits to prevent permanent injury.
