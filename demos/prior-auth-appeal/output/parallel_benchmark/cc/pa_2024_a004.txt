HealthFirst Health Plan has completed the clinical appeal review for the emergency cervical surgery requested by Dr. Elena Marchetti. After reviewing the clinical records, applicable plan criteria, California state regulations, and national clinical standards, we have determined that the initial denial was incorrect. The request for ACDF C5-C6 (CPT 22551) is now APPROVED.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You presented to the emergency department on November 10, 2024, with a 72-hour history of rapidly progressive bilateral arm weakness (2/5 strength) and leg weakness (3/5 strength), accompanied by urinary urgency. An emergency MRI performed the same day revealed severe central cord compression at C5-C6 with extensive T2 hyperintensity (myelomalacia), indicating acute spinal cord injury. Your neurological exam confirmed an incomplete spinal cord syndrome with hyperreflexia and bilateral Babinski signs. Dr. Marchetti declared this a surgical emergency, noting that physical therapy was absolutely contraindicated and posed a risk of permanent paralysis.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
The initial denial was based on Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, which requires a minimum of 4 weeks of physical therapy and 8 weeks of pharmacotherapy for patients with myelopathy. Because you had completed 0 weeks of physical therapy and only one day of inpatient pharmacotherapy, the initial reviewer determined the criteria were not met. However, Section 2.2(a) of the plan criteria fails to account for acute neurological emergencies where conservative treatment is medically contraindicated.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
Under California law, the plan's internal requirements for conservative treatment are superseded in this case:
1. California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3): This statute prohibits a health plan from conditioning surgical approval on the completion of physical therapy when the condition involves "objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that physical therapy cannot address." Your MRI findings of extensive myelomalacia and severe cord compression constitute definitive evidence of such compromise.
2. DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: This regulation specifies that for conditions where delay risks irreversible harm, such as cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia, plans "shall not require completion of a full course of conservative treatment." The Department of Managed Health Care specifically identifies myelomalacia as a finding that supports the waiver of standard conservative treatment requirements.

CLINICAL STANDARD ANALYSIS
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and AO Spine Clinical Practice Guidelines (2021), classify your presentation as a "Tier 1 — Strong Indication" for immediate surgery. These guidelines state that for moderate-to-severe myelopathy with objective neurological deficits, surgical decompression is the primary treatment and that delaying surgery to attempt physical therapy is not supported by evidence and may result in permanent neurological deterioration.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
In accordance with the hierarchy of authority, California state law (CIC § 10169.5) and regulatory mandates (APL 22-014) take precedence over the plan's internal clinical coverage criteria. Furthermore, under CHSC § 1374.32, the clinical standard of care must govern when plan criteria are more restrictive than generally accepted medical practice. Therefore, the plan's requirement for 4 weeks of physical therapy is waived.

WARRANT
The denial is overturned because the member's condition (acute cervical myelopathy with myelomalacia and 2/5 motor weakness) constitutes a surgical emergency where physical therapy is medically contraindicated and legally prohibited as a prerequisite for authorization under CIC § 10169.5(a)(3).
