CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 53-year-old female with a diagnosis of multi-level cervical spondylosis with myeloradiculopathy. Clinical records demonstrate the following:
- Imaging: An MRI dated 10/01/2024 showed a large disc herniation at C4-C5 with severe cord compression and myelomalacia (spinal cord signal change). At C5-C6, there is a moderate disc herniation with moderate left C6 nerve root compression. At C3-C4, findings are limited to a mild disc bulge with minimal narrowing.
- Physical Examination: A neurological exam on 10/05/2024 documented myelopathic findings (hyperreflexia, positive Hoffman sign, and wide-based gait). Specific nerve deficits included bilateral deltoid weakness (4/5) and left bicep weakness (4-/5) with a diminished bicep reflex. Examination of the C4 level noted neck pain but no specific radicular pattern.
- Conservative Treatment: You completed 9 weeks of physical therapy (27 sessions), trialed Baclofen and Pregabalin for over 3 months, and received a cervical epidural steroid injection on 10/20/2024 with only modest, temporary relief.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
- Section 2.1(a) & (b): Level C4-C5 meets criteria for cervical myelopathy. Level C5-C6 meets criteria for cervical radiculopathy with an objective neurological deficit (left bicep weakness/reflex change).
- Section 2.2: You have satisfied the conservative treatment requirements, including more than 6 weeks of physical therapy, trials of two neuropathic/muscle relaxant agents, and an interventional procedure.
- Section 3.1: Level C3-C4 is excluded from coverage as the clinical record documents only axial neck pain at this level without a correlating radicular or myelopathic deficit.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
This determination complies with the California Regulatory Framework:
- CIC § 10169.5(a)(3): Approval for level C5-C6 is required because the clinical record provides objective evidence of structural neurological compromise (C6 nerve root compression with correlating weakness) that physical therapy cannot resolve.
- DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: The presence of myelomalacia at C4-C5 constitutes a time-sensitive neurological condition where surgical intervention is the clinical standard to prevent irreversible harm.
- CHSC § 1374.32(b): The clinical standard for medical necessity overrides internal plan criteria. While the initial reviewer suggested C5-C6 pathology was insufficient, the objective neurological deficit at that level meets the generally accepted standard for surgical intervention.

CLINICAL STANDARD APPLICATION
- AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023): Level C4-C5 meets Tier 1 (Strong Indication) due to severe cord compression and myelomalacia. Level C5-C6 meets Tier 2 (Reasonable Indication) due to persistent radiculopathy and objective deficit after failing a 6-week conservative trial. Level C3-C4 falls under Tier 3 (Not Indicated) as imaging findings do not correlate with clinical symptoms.
- NASS Guidelines (2020): Support surgical decompression for moderate myelopathy and persistent radiculopathy with objective deficits.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
The initial denial (MED-003) stated that C5-C6 did not demonstrate pathology of sufficient severity. However, the clinical record (MRI 10/01/2024 and Exam 10/05/2024) confirms a moderate herniation with a correlating C6 neurological deficit. Per California law (CHSC § 1374.32), the clinical standard and objective findings must take precedence over the plan's more restrictive interpretation. Conversely, for level C3-C4, both the plan criteria and clinical standards agree that surgery is not indicated for mild imaging findings and axial pain alone.

WARRANT
The authorization for level C5-C6 is warranted because you have a documented structural compression with a correlating neurological deficit that has failed to respond to a comprehensive 9-week course of conservative therapy. The denial for level C3-C4 is maintained because the clinical evidence does not support the necessity of surgery at that level at this time.
