Dear Priya Nair,

HealthFirst has completed the clinical appeal review for the requested cervical spinal surgery. After reviewing your medical records, the initial denial is OVERTURNED. Authorization for ACDF at C5-C6 is granted.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 56-year-old female with a confirmed diagnosis of moderate cervical myelopathy at C5-C6. Clinical records from September 2024 document bilateral Hoffman signs, bilateral upper extremity hyperreflexia, impaired tandem gait, and hand clumsiness. An MRI dated September 1, 2024, confirms moderate central cord compression at the C5-C6 level. Your treating neurosurgeon, Dr. James Chen, has documented that your condition requires surgical intervention to prevent further neurological deterioration.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
The initial denial was based on CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, which requires 6 weeks of physical therapy (PT). However, our review finds that the initial reviewer applied the incorrect clinical pathway. 

1. Pathway Application: Because your primary diagnosis is cervical myelopathy (Criterion 2.1(b)), the "EXCEPTION 2.2(A) — MYELOPATHY PATHWAY" applies. This pathway reduces the PT requirement to 4 weeks.
2. Physical Therapy: You completed 5 weeks of supervised PT. This exceeds the 4-week requirement under Section 2.2A.
3. Interventional Treatment: Section 2.2A waives the requirement for injections if the physician documents a risk of neurological deterioration. Dr. Chen provided this documentation, stating that interventional procedures were not indicated due to the severity of cord compression.
4. Pharmacotherapy: Section 2.2(b) requires two agents for a minimum of 4 weeks each. Your records show Baclofen (3 months) and Methylprednisolone (3 weeks). We acknowledge that the steroid trial fell one week short of the plan's internal 4-week duration requirement. 

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL STANDARD RESOLUTION
While the pharmacotherapy duration did not strictly meet the plan's internal 4-week threshold, the denial is overturned based on the following regulatory and clinical mandates which supersede internal plan policy:

- California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5: This statute prohibits the mechanical application of physical therapy or conservative treatment duration requirements when a member has objective evidence of structural neurological compromise that therapy cannot address. Your confirmed cord compression and upper motor neuron signs constitute such compromise.
- DMHC All-Plan Letter (APL) 22-014: The Department of Managed Health Care identifies cervical myelopathy with cord compression as a time-sensitive condition where delay risks irreversible harm. Plans are directed not to require a full course of conservative treatment when surgical intervention is indicated under generally accepted standards.
- Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS 2023): Generally accepted medical practice (Tier 1 Strong Indication) recommends surgical decompression as the primary treatment for moderate to severe myelopathy. Requiring an additional week of steroids before authorizing surgery is inconsistent with this clinical standard.

In accordance with California Health & Safety Code § 1374.32(b), where plan criteria are more restrictive than generally accepted standards of medical practice, the clinical standard must govern. 

WARRANT
The denial is overturned because the plan initially applied the wrong criteria pathway (Radiculopathy instead of Myelopathy). Although a minor shortfall exists in the plan's internal pharmacotherapy duration requirement, the presence of objective neurological deficits and cord compression triggers regulatory protections under CIC § 10169.5 and APL 22-014, which mandate approval based on the clinical standard of care for myelopathy.
