APPEAL DETERMINATION

DATE: November 22, 2024
CASE ID: PA-2024-D002
APPELLANT: Michael Green
PROVIDER: Dr. Sarah Kim, MD — Spine Surgery
PROCEDURE: Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) C5-C6 (CPT 22551)

DISPOSITION: OVERTURN

I. SUMMARY OF DETERMINATION
The initial denial of prior authorization for ACDF C5-C6 is OVERTURNED. A review of the clinical record, HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria, and California regulatory requirements demonstrates that the member satisfies all medical necessity requirements for the requested procedure. Furthermore, the initial denial was procedurally defective under California law.

II. CLINICAL FINDINGS
Mr. Green is a 57-year-old male with a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6 with functional impairment. Clinical evidence includes:
- Imaging: MRI (09/20/2024) demonstrating a large C5-C6 disc herniation with moderate left C6 nerve root compression.
- Physical Exam: Documented left C6 dermatomal numbness, diminished left bicep reflex, and objective bicep weakness (4/5).
- Conservative Treatment: Completion of 7 weeks of supervised physical therapy (21 sessions) with a documented functional plateau at week 6. Pharmacotherapy included Gabapentin (4 months) and Naproxen (3 months). Interventional treatment included a left C6 epidural steroid injection (10/28/2024) which provided only transient (3-week) relief.

III. APPLICATION OF PLAN CRITERIA
The request satisfies HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
- Section 2.1 (Diagnosis): Met via MRI evidence of nerve root compression and correlating radicular symptoms (weakness/numbness).
- Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment): Met. The member completed 7 weeks of PT (exceeding the 6-week requirement in 2.2(a)), trialed two neuropathic/NSAID agents for >4 weeks (satisfying 2.2(b)), and received a cervical epidural injection (satisfying 2.2(c)).
- Section 2.3 (Imaging): Met. MRI was performed within 6 months of the request and demonstrates pathology at the proposed surgical level.

IV. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND STATUTORY PROTECTIONS
The initial denial issued on 11/08/2024 violated the following California provisions:
- CHSC § 1374.31(a)(2) & (b): The denial notice failed to include the specific clinical criteria applied (referenced but not attached) and failed to include the required notice of Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights. Under § 1374.31(b), a denial that fails to specify the clinical criteria is "DEFECTIVE" and treated as if no denial was issued.
- CIC § 10169.5: This statute prohibits conditioning surgical approval on physical therapy when a member has reached a "functional plateau." Clinical records confirm a plateau at week 6; therefore, any further requirement for PT is legally unenforceable.

V. CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
Under the AANS/CNS 2023 Joint Guidelines for the Management of Degenerative Cervical Radiculopathy, Mr. Green meets "Tier 2 — Reasonable Indication" for surgical intervention. The clinical standard supports decompression when radicular symptoms and objective neurological deficits (4/5 weakness) persist despite a 6-12 week course of conservative management. Requiring further delay in the presence of objective motor weakness is inconsistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice (Clinical Evidence Document, Section 3.1).

VI. RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS AND WARRANT
The plan's initial denial stated the member did not meet criteria; however, the clinical record factually contradicts this assertion. Applying the hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria), the denial must be overturned because:
1. The member meets the plan's own internal criteria (CC-SPINE-2024).
2. The denial notice was procedurally defective under CHSC § 1374.31.
3. The clinical standard of care (AANS/CNS 2023) supports the medical necessity of the procedure to prevent further neurological deterioration.

VII. NEXT STEPS
The plan is directed to authorize CPT 22551 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center as requested by Dr. Sarah Kim. This authorization is valid for 90 days per plan policy Section 3.5.
