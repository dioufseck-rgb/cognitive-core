FINAL DISPOSITION: OVERTURN

Dear Mr. Harris,

HealthFirst has completed the clinical appeal review for the requested revision anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at the C5-C6 level. After reviewing the clinical records, imaging reports, and applicable California regulatory requirements, we are OVERTURNING the initial denial. The requested procedure is authorized.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 48-year-old male presenting with a recurrence of right-sided C6 radiculopathy symptoms following a prior ACDF in 2021. Clinical examination on September 20, 2024, demonstrated objective neurological deficits, including 4/5 bicep weakness, diminished bicep reflex, and C6 dermatomal numbness. You have completed an extensive course of conservative management, including eight weeks of supervised physical therapy (exceeding the plan's six-week requirement), four months of Naproxen, three months of Gabapentin, and a cervical epidural steroid injection on October 10, 2024, all of which failed to provide sustained relief.

PLAN CRITERIA COMPLIANCE
The initial denial (October 30, 2024) stated that documentation did not confirm "hardware failure." However, the plan's own internal criteria (CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2) and the denial notice itself identify "pseudarthrosis" (failure of the bones to fuse) as a valid clinical indication for revision surgery. 

1. Imaging Evidence: A CT scan dated September 15, 2024, explicitly confirmed "incomplete bridging bone formation" and "lucency around hardware suggesting pseudarthrosis." While the MRI from September 1, 2024, was inconclusive due to hardware artifact, the CT scan—the gold standard for assessing fusion—confirmed the pathology.
2. Conservative Treatment: You have satisfied all requirements under Section 2.2 of the Clinical Coverage Criteria, including physical therapy, pharmacotherapy, and interventional trials.

REGULATORY RESOLUTION AND HIERARCHY
Under the California regulatory hierarchy, this determination is reached based on the following:

1. Defective Notice (CHSC § 1374.31(b)): The initial denial notice was procedurally and factually defective. It cited pseudarthrosis as a requirement for coverage but then ignored the submitted CT scan that confirmed that exact condition, focusing exclusively on the absence of hardware fracture. Under California law, a notice that fails to address the specific clinical evidence provided is considered defective.
2. Clinical Standard Override (CHSC § 1374.32(b)): California law requires that medical necessity determinations be made in accordance with generally accepted standards of medical practice, regardless of whether a plan's internal criteria are more restrictive. Generally accepted standards (AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines 2023; NASS 2020) establish that symptomatic pseudarthrosis with objective neurological deficit is a primary indication for revision surgery.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICT
The conflict between the plan's claim (no hardware failure) and the clinical evidence (confirmed pseudarthrosis) is resolved in favor of the clinical evidence. Because pseudarthrosis is both a plan-recognized criterion and a clinically accepted indication for surgery, the plan's failure to acknowledge the CT findings constitutes a reversible error. 

WARRANT
The prior authorization is overturned because the member has documented symptomatic pseudarthrosis with objective neurological deficits and has exhausted all required conservative treatments, satisfying both HealthFirst CC-SPINE-2024 and the medical necessity standards mandated by CHSC § 1374.32.
