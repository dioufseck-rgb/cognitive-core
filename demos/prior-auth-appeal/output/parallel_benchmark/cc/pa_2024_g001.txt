HealthFirst has completed the appeal review for the requested anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at the C6-C7 level. After reviewing the clinical records, plan criteria, and California regulatory requirements, we have determined that the initial denial was issued in error. The request for surgery is now APPROVED.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
Mr. Reyes is a 49-year-old male with persistent right-sided C6-C7 cervical radiculopathy. Clinical documentation includes:
1. Imaging: An MRI dated August 20, 2024, demonstrating a large C6-C7 disc herniation with moderate right C7 nerve root compression and right foraminal stenosis.
2. Neurological Exam: An exam on August 25, 2024, documented objective neurological deficits, including 4/5 right-sided grip strength and a diminished right tricep reflex.
3. Conservative Treatment: The member completed 9 weeks of supervised physical therapy (PT) across two providers: Valley Physical Therapy (3 weeks, June 2024) and Capital Physical Therapy (6 weeks, July–August 2024). The Capital PT discharge summary explicitly documents a functional plateau, stating "Maximum therapeutic benefit achieved. No further improvement expected."
4. Additional Treatment: The member completed a 4-month trial of Gabapentin, a 3-month trial of Naproxen, and a right C7 epidural steroid injection on September 15, 2024, with only temporary relief.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2, authorization for ACDF requires a 6-week minimum course of physical therapy, trials of two pharmacotherapy agents for 4 weeks each, and one interventional treatment. 

The initial denial (November 1, 2024) asserted that the member completed only 3 weeks of PT and failed to document a functional plateau. However, the clinical record confirms 9 total weeks of PT were completed and a functional plateau was explicitly documented. Consequently, the member satisfies all requirements of CC-SPINE-2024 Section 2.2(a), (b), and (c).

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) and (c) prohibits health plans from mechanically applying a minimum duration for physical therapy when a member has reached a documented functional plateau. Furthermore, under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b), a denial notice based on a factual error (miscounting PT duration) is considered procedurally defective. 

CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
According to the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Guidelines (2020), surgical intervention is indicated for cervical radiculopathy (Tier 2) when a patient presents with persistent symptoms and objective neurological deficits (e.g., 4/5 weakness) that have failed to respond to 6–12 weeks of conservative management. Mr. Reyes meets this clinical standard.

CONFLICT RESOLUTION AND WARRANT
There is a direct conflict between the plan's initial denial and the clinical evidence. Applying the hierarchy of authority (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria), the plan's mechanical application of a 6-week PT requirement is overridden by CIC § 10169.5 due to the documented functional plateau. Additionally, the plan's factual claim that only 3 weeks of PT were completed is directly contradicted by the submitted records from Capital Physical Therapy. 

The warrant for overturning this denial is that the member has objectively satisfied both the plan's internal criteria (9 weeks PT > 6 weeks required) and the prevailing clinical standards for surgical intervention in the setting of persistent radiculopathy with neurological deficit.
