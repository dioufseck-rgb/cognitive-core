HealthFirst has completed the clinical appeal review for the requested cervical spinal surgery. Our decision is a PARTIAL OVERTURN of the initial denial.

DISPOSITION
- C5-C6 Level (CPT 22551): OVERTURNED. The request for surgery at this level is APPROVED.
- C6-C7 Level (CPT 22552): UPHELD. The request for surgery at this level remains DENIED.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
Mr. Patel is a 60-year-old male with a diagnosis of two-level cervical disc disease. Clinical records demonstrate the following:
- Imaging: An MRI dated 09/15/2024 shows a large disc herniation at C5-C6 with moderate-to-severe right C6 nerve root compression and foraminal stenosis. At C6-C7, imaging shows only a small disc bulge and mild foraminal narrowing with no identifiable nerve root compression or cord contact.
- Physical Exam: A neurological exam on 09/20/2024 revealed objective deficits at the C6 level, including 3+/5 weakness in the right bicep and an absent bicep reflex. Findings at the C7 level were equivocal, with a slightly diminished tricep reflex but no definitive dermatomal symptoms.
- Conservative Treatment: The member completed 8 weeks (24 sessions) of physical therapy, reaching a functional plateau where C6 symptoms persisted. Pharmacotherapy included a 4-month trial of Pregabalin and a 3-month trial of Naproxen. A C6 epidural injection on 10/15/2024 provided 70% relief that was temporary.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
Per HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
- Section 2.1 (Diagnosis): The C5-C6 level meets criterion (c) for cervical disc herniation with objective neurological deficit. The C6-C7 level fails this criterion as there is no objective neurological deficit clearly documented for that level.
- Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment): The member satisfied all requirements, including 6 weeks of PT (2.2a), two medications (2.2b), and an interventional treatment (2.2c).
- Section 2.3 (Imaging): The C5-C6 level meets this requirement as the MRI demonstrates structural pathology consistent with symptoms. The C6-C7 level fails this requirement because the imaging (mild changes, no compression) does not correlate with the requested surgical intervention.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE (CALIFORNIA LAW)
- CHSC § 1374.32(a): California law requires that medical necessity be determined based on generally accepted standards of medical practice. The denial of the C5-C6 level, which meets all clinical standards, is inconsistent with this statute.
- CIC § 10169.5: The plan may not mechanically apply physical therapy requirements when a member has reached a functional plateau. Records confirm a functional plateau was reached for the C6 radiculopathy after 8 weeks of therapy.
- Conflict Resolution: The initial denial (11/01/2024) stated that a two-level fusion is not approved when one level meets criteria and the other does not. Under California regulatory standards, the plan must authorize those portions of a request that are medically necessary. Denying the entire procedure because one level was unsupported constitutes a violation of the duty to provide medically necessary care for the C5-C6 level.

CLINICAL STANDARD APPLICATION
- AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023): Surgical intervention for the C5-C6 level is classified as a Tier 2 indication (Radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and objective deficit). The C6-C7 level is classified as Tier 3 (Surgery not indicated) because imaging findings lack correlating clinical symptoms or objective compression.

WARRANT
The C5-C6 level is medically necessary because it meets plan criteria (CC-SPINE-2024 § 2.1-2.3), California regulatory standards for medical necessity (CHSC § 1374.32), and generally accepted clinical guidelines (AANS/CNS Tier 2). The C6-C7 level is not medically necessary at this time because imaging does not demonstrate nerve root compression (Plan § 2.3) and the clinical exam does not show a correlating C7 deficit (AANS Tier 3).

You may apply for an IMR if you believe the decision was not medically necessary. The IMR application is enclosed with this letter.
