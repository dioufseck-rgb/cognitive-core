Dear Mr. Obi,

HealthFirst has completed the appeal review of the prior authorization denial for the requested cervical spine surgery. After a comprehensive evaluation of your clinical records, the HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria (CC-SPINE-2024), and applicable California regulatory standards, we have determined that the initial denial was incorrect. The request for ACDF at C6-C7 is now APPROVED.

REASON FOR OVERTURN
The initial denial issued on November 8, 2024, was based on two grounds: (1) an alleged shortfall in physical therapy duration and (2) suboptimal diabetes control (HbA1c 8.2%). This appeal review finds both grounds to be invalid based on the following evidence:

1. Factual Correction of Physical Therapy Duration
The initial denial stated that only 5 weeks of physical therapy (PT) were completed, failing to meet the 6-week requirement. However, the clinical record contains documented evidence of continuous, supervised PT from 2024-07-01 through 2024-08-16. This constitutes a total of 7 weeks of treatment, which exceeds the 6-week minimum required under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(a). Furthermore, the PT discharge summary explicitly documents a "functional plateau," noting that no further improvement was expected. Under California Insurance Code § 10169.5(a)(2), a plan may not condition approval on additional therapy once a functional plateau has been documented by the treating provider.

2. Rejection of Diabetes Control as an Authorization Criterion
The initial denial cited your HbA1c level of 8.2% as a basis for non-authorization due to perioperative risk. Upon review of the governing plan document, CC-SPINE-2024, it is confirmed that there are no metabolic, glycemic, or HbA1c-specific criteria required for the authorization of ACDF. As noted by your treating surgeon, Dr. Amina Diallo, perioperative diabetes optimization is a standard surgical planning responsibility and a clinical management protocol; it is not a prerequisite for insurance coverage under your plan. Because this requirement does not exist in the plan’s medical necessity criteria, it is rejected as a valid basis for denial.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
All other requirements for this procedure have been previously verified and remain satisfied:
- Diagnosis: Confirmed cervical radiculopathy at C6-C7 with objective neurological deficits (4/5 tricep weakness and diminished reflexes) per Section 2.1.
- Imaging: MRI dated 2024-09-20 demonstrates a large disc herniation at C6-C7 with moderate nerve root compression, correlating with clinical symptoms per Section 2.3.
- Conservative Care: In addition to the 7 weeks of PT, you have completed trials of two neuropathic agents (Pregabalin and Duloxetine) and a C7 epidural steroid injection, satisfying Section 2.2(b) and (c).
