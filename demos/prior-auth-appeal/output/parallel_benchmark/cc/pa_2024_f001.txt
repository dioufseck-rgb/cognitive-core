DISPOSITION: OVERTURN

Dear Ms. James,

HealthFirst Health Plan has completed the appeal review for the requested anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at C6-C7. After reviewing the additional clinical records provided with your appeal, we have determined that the initial denial should be OVERTURNED. The requested procedure is now approved.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
Ms. James is a 51-year-old female with a diagnosis of bilateral C6-C7 cervical radiculopathy. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) dated September 1, 2024, demonstrates a large disc herniation at C6-C7 with bilateral C7 nerve root compression and foraminal stenosis. A neurological examination on September 5, 2024, confirmed objective deficits, including bilateral tricep weakness (4/5) and diminished tricep reflexes. Conservative treatment has included 8 weeks of supervised physical therapy (Valley PT and Mountain PT), pharmacotherapy with Pregabalin (3 months) and Naproxen (2 months), and a bilateral C7 epidural steroid injection on September 20, 2024.

PLAN CRITERIA COMPLIANCE
The request was initially denied under Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2(a), because the plan could only verify 4 weeks of physical therapy. Upon appeal, the submission of records from Mountain Physical Therapy confirms an additional 4 weeks of treatment (July 15 to August 9, 2024).

1. Diagnosis Requirement (Section 2.1): Met. Documentation confirms cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficits and correlating MRI findings.
2. Physical Therapy (Section 2.2a): Met. The member has completed 8 total weeks of supervised therapy, exceeding the 6-week minimum requirement.
3. Pharmacotherapy (Section 2.2b): Met. Trials of Pregabalin (neuropathic agent) and Naproxen (NSAID) both exceeded the 4-week requirement.
4. Interventional Treatment (Section 2.2c): Met. A cervical epidural steroid injection was performed on September 20, 2024.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
This determination accounts for California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5, which prohibits conditioning surgical approval on physical therapy when a member has reached a functional plateau. Records from Mountain Physical Therapy explicitly document a plateau in neurological symptoms at the time of discharge. Furthermore, under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32, the service meets the definition of medical necessity as it is clinically appropriate and consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice.

CLINICAL STANDARD OF CARE
According to the AANS/CNS Joint Section on Disorders of the Spine (2023 Update) and NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines (2020), surgical intervention is indicated for patients with radiculopathy who demonstrate objective neurological deficits and persistent symptoms after 6–12 weeks of conservative management. Ms. James meets these Tier 2 clinical standards, as her tricep weakness and reflex changes persisted despite 8 weeks of therapy and interventional treatment.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
The initial denial was based on an administrative failure to receive complete records. There is no conflict between the plan criteria and clinical standards; both support the medical necessity of the procedure once the full 8-week physical therapy history is considered.

WARRANT FOR OVERTURN
The denial is overturned because the member has now provided documentation satisfying all conservative treatment requirements under Section 2.2 of the plan criteria. Additionally, the presence of objective neurological deficits and a documented functional plateau necessitates approval under California regulatory standards (CIC § 10169.5).
