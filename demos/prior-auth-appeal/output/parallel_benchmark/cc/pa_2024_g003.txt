HealthFirst has completed the first-level internal appeal review for the requested cervical spinal surgery (ACDF). After reviewing the clinical record, plan criteria, and California regulatory requirements, we are REMANDING this case for an Independent Physician Review (GATE) to reconcile conflicting clinical evidence regarding the urgency of your condition.

CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 61-year-old male with a diagnosis of mild cervical myelopathy at C5-C6. Your neurological examination on October 5, 2024, demonstrated a modified Japanese Orthopaedic Association (mJOA) score of 15/18, which is classified as mild. Objective findings included bilateral Hoffman signs and mild hyperreflexia, but your gait remains intact and activities of daily living are fully independent. Your clinical record (functional_status) explicitly notes that your symptoms have been stable for 6 months. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) dated October 1, 2024, shows moderate central cord compression at C5-C6 but specifically notes the absence of T2 signal change or myelomalacia (spinal cord bruising).

PLAN CRITERIA REVIEW
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, authorization for spinal surgery requires the completion of conservative treatment. 
1. Physical Therapy (Section 2.2a): The initial denial cited a failure to complete 6 weeks of physical therapy. However, under the Myelopathy Pathway (Exception 2.2A), this requirement is reduced to 4 weeks. You have completed 3 weeks.
2. Pharmacotherapy (Section 2.2b): The criteria require a trial of at least two pharmacotherapy agents (e.g., NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, or neuropathic agents) for 4 weeks each. Your record indicates the use of only one agent (Baclofen). Exception 2.2(A) explicitly states that the pharmacotherapy requirement is NOT waived for myelopathy patients.

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL STANDARD RESOLUTION
1. Physical Therapy Waiver: California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(3) prohibits health plans from conditioning surgical approval on physical therapy when there is objective evidence of structural neurological compromise. Because your MRI confirms cord compression, the plan’s physical therapy requirement is legally overridden.
2. Urgency and Progression: Your treating neurologist, Dr. Osei, has declared "surgical urgency" and characterized your condition as "progressive." However, the longitudinal clinical record (functional_status) describes your symptoms as "stable" for 6 months. Furthermore, the AANS/CNS 2023 Guidelines classify a patient with an mJOA of 15 and no myelomalacia as Tier 2 (Elective Surgical Candidate), not Tier 1 (Urgent). 
3. Mandatory Waiver (DMHC APL 22-014): The regulatory requirement to waive all conservative treatment for urgent cases is not triggered here, as that mandate requires objective evidence of progression or myelomalacia, neither of which is present in your imaging or functional history.

REASON FOR REMAND
A conflict exists between the treating specialist’s declaration of urgency and the objective clinical findings (mJOA 15, stable 6-month course, no myelomalacia). While the physical therapy requirement is waived by law, the pharmacotherapy requirement (Section 2.2b) remains unsatisfied. Because surgery may be clinically appropriate as an elective (Tier 2) procedure despite the unmet pharmacotherapy, but is not supported as an "urgent" intervention that would bypass all criteria, an independent physician review is required to determine medical necessity.
