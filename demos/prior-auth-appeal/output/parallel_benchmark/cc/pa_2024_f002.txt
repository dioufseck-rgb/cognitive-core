Dear Mr. Clark,

HealthFirst has completed the first-level appeal review of the prior authorization request for an anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) at the C5-C6 level. After reviewing the clinical record, the HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria, California regulatory requirements, and generally accepted standards of medical practice, we have determined that the initial denial should be OVERTURNED. Authorization for this procedure is now granted.

CLINICAL FINDINGS SUMMARY
You are a 46-year-old male with a diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at C5-C6. Clinical records demonstrate significant functional impairment and objective neurological deficits. A neurological examination dated August 20, 2024, documented 4/5 bicep weakness on the right, an absent right bicep reflex, and a positive Spurling sign. Imaging via MRI on August 15, 2024, confirmed a large disc herniation at C5-C6 with severe right C6 nerve root compression and foraminal stenosis. You have completed an extensive course of conservative treatment, including 12 weeks of supervised physical therapy (6 weeks at Riverside PT and 6 weeks at Coastal PT), five months of Gabapentin, three months of Duloxetine, and a C6 epidural steroid injection on August 28, 2024, which provided only temporary relief.

PLAN CRITERIA ANALYSIS
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2, the following requirements were met:
1. Diagnosis (Section 2.1): Confirmed cervical radiculopathy with objective neurological deficit and correlating MRI findings.
2. Physical Therapy (Section 2.2a): The plan requires a minimum of 6 weeks of supervised therapy. While the initial denial cited a discrepancy in Coastal PT records, the records from Riverside Physical Therapy (April 1 to May 10, 2024) independently document 6 weeks (18 sessions) with a functional plateau. This alone satisfies the requirement.
3. Pharmacotherapy (Section 2.2b): Documented trials of Gabapentin and Duloxetine (neuropathic agents) for more than 4 weeks each satisfy this criterion.
4. Interventional Treatment (Section 2.2c): The right C6 epidural steroid injection satisfies the requirement for one interventional treatment.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
The initial denial is inconsistent with California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5. Specifically, CIC § 10169.5(a)(2) and (b) prohibit conditioning surgical approval on further physical therapy when a "functional plateau" has been documented by the treating therapist. Furthermore, CIC § 10169.5(c) prohibits the mechanical application of a minimum-week requirement without reference to the member's neurological status. Given the objective evidence of structural neurological compromise (4/5 weakness and absent reflex), the plan's delay based on documentation "uncertainty" was legally impermissible.

CLINICAL STANDARD ANALYSIS
According to the AANS/CNS Joint Section on Disorders of the Spine Guidelines (2023), your presentation meets the "Tier 2 — Reasonable Indication" for surgical intervention. The guidelines state that surgery is indicated for radiculopathy with persistent symptoms and objective deficits after 6–12 weeks of conservative treatment. Clinical evidence (Cochrane Review 2021) further indicates that for patients with objective neurological deficits who have reached a functional plateau, extended conservative management is not supported and may lead to poorer outcomes.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS
The initial denial was based on a documentation discrepancy regarding the duration of therapy at Coastal PT. However, the hierarchy of review (State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria) dictates that the denial cannot stand. First, the Riverside PT records were already verified and sufficient to meet the 6-week threshold. Second, the complete records for Coastal PT (6 weeks) have now been provided, resolving the discrepancy. Third, California law (CIC § 10169.5) protects members from surgical delays due to PT requirements when neurological deficits are present.

WARRANT
The prior authorization is overturned because the member has satisfied all internal medical necessity criteria, the clinical presentation meets the national standard of care for surgical intervention, and the basis for the initial denial violated California regulatory protections regarding conservative treatment prerequisites.
