HealthFirst has completed the formal appeal review of the prior authorization denial for the requested cervical spine surgery. After a comprehensive review of the clinical records, treating surgeon statements, plan coverage criteria, and California regulatory standards, the original decision to deny coverage is UPHELD.

CLINICAL FINDINGS
The clinical record identifies a diagnosis of C5-C6 cervical radiculopathy. An MRI dated September 10, 2024, demonstrated a mild-to-moderate disc herniation at C5-C6 with nerve root contact but specifically noted no measurable compression, no spinal cord contact, and no myelomalacia (cord signal change). A neurological examination performed on September 15, 2024, documented 5/5 grip strength, no weakness, and no upper motor neuron signs. You are currently working full-time with no documented activity limitations. Your conservative treatment history includes 4 weeks of physical therapy (12 sessions) and an 8-week trial of Naproxen.

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2 (Conservative Treatment Requirement), authorization for ACDF requires the completion of a documented conservative treatment pathway including:
1. Section 2.2(a): Minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy.
2. Section 2.2(b): Trial of at least two pharmacotherapy agents for 4 weeks each.
3. Section 2.2(c): At least one interventional treatment (e.g., epidural steroid injection).

Your clinical record confirms only 4 weeks of physical therapy, the use of only one medication (Naproxen), and no interventional treatments. Consequently, the request fails to meet the plan's medical necessity criteria.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND CLINICAL STANDARDS
We reviewed this case under California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.32 and California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5. While CIC § 10169.5(a) provides exceptions to physical therapy requirements, those exceptions do not apply here because there is no objective evidence of "structural neurological compromise" (as the MRI shows no cord compression) and no documentation of a "functional plateau."

Furthermore, generally accepted clinical standards, including the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) and NASS Evidence-Based Guidelines (2020), classify your presentation as Tier 2 (Reasonable Indication) or Tier 3 (Surgery Not Indicated) at this time. These guidelines recommend surgical intervention for radiculopathy only after a failed 6-to-12-week trial of conservative care, unless a progressive neurological deficit is present. Your examination on September 15, 2024, confirmed no such deficit.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTING OPINIONS
We have carefully considered the appeal letter from Dr. Walsh, who cites 25 years of experience and asserts that surgery is necessary to prevent permanent disability. However, under the hierarchy of clinical review, objective imaging and physical examination findings take precedence over subjective declarations of urgency. The MRI (2024-09-10) and the neurological exam (2024-09-15) both demonstrate a stable, non-urgent condition without cord involvement. Therefore, the clinical standard requiring completion of conservative therapy remains the governing factor.
