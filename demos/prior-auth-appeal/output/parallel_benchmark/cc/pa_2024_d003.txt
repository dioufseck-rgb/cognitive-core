APPEAL DETERMINATION

DATE: October 30, 2024
CASE ID: PA-2024-D003
APPELLANT: Jessica Turner
PROCEDURE: Anterior Cervical Discectomy and Fusion (ACDF) C6-C7 (CPT 22551)

DISPOSITION: REMAND

I. SUMMARY OF CLINICAL FINDINGS
Ms. Jessica Turner is a 39-year-old female with a confirmed diagnosis of cervical radiculopathy at the C6-C7 level. Clinical documentation includes an MRI (2024-09-01) demonstrating a C6-C7 disc herniation with right C7 nerve root compression and moderate foraminal stenosis. A neurological examination (2024-09-05) revealed objective deficits, including diminished tricep reflex and 4+/5 grip strength. The member has completed five weeks of physical therapy (15 sessions) and a three-month trial of Meloxicam with partial relief. The treating physician, Dr. Robert Chen, has declared a functional plateau in the appeal documentation.

II. APPLICATION OF PLAN CRITERIA
Under HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024, Section 2.2, authorization for CPT 22551 requires:
(a) Minimum 6 weeks of supervised physical therapy;
(b) Trial of two medications for 4 weeks each; and
(c) One interventional treatment (e.g., epidural steroid injection).

The clinical record shows a shortfall in these requirements: the member completed 5 of the required 6 weeks of physical therapy, trialed Prednisone for only 2 of the required 4 weeks, and has not undergone an interventional treatment. Consequently, the request does not satisfy the plan's internal medical necessity criteria as written.

III. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE AND PROCEDURAL DEFECTS
Upon review of the initial denial issued on October 15, 2024, the following regulatory violations were identified:

1. DEFECTIVE NOTICE: California Health & Safety Code (CHSC) § 1374.31(b) states that a denial notice failing to specify the clinical criteria applied is defective and shall be treated as if no denial was issued for purposes of appeal timelines. The plan's denial was based on a verbal peer-to-peer call, and the written notice stated "Verbal only — peer-to-peer call" regarding the basis of the denial. This fails the statutory requirement for written criteria.
2. MISSING IMR NOTICE: CHSC § 1374.31(a)(2) requires the plan to include notice of Independent Medical Review (IMR) rights in every medical necessity denial. The plan failed to include this notice.
3. PHYSICAL THERAPY OVERRIDE: California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5(a)(2) prohibits a plan from mechanically conditioning surgery on a minimum number of PT weeks if the treating physician documents that the member has reached a functional plateau. Dr. Chen's declaration of a plateau overrides the plan's 6-week requirement.

IV. CLINICAL STANDARD OF REVIEW
Generally accepted standards of medical practice, specifically the AANS/CNS Joint Guidelines (2023) Tier 2, support surgical intervention for cervical radiculopathy when objective neurological deficits (e.g., reflex changes and weakness) persist despite conservative management. The member’s presentation of diminished tricep reflex and 4+/5 grip strength meets the clinical standard for surgical appropriateness, regardless of the plan's more restrictive internal duration requirements.

V. RESOLUTION OF CONFLICTS AND WARRANT
In resolving the conflict between the plan's internal criteria (not met) and the regulatory/clinical standards (met), the hierarchy of authority dictates that California State Law and generally accepted clinical standards take precedence. 

The denial is procedurally defective under CHSC § 1374.31(b) because the plan failed to provide written criteria and IMR notice. Furthermore, the plan's mechanical application of a 6-week PT requirement is superseded by CIC § 10169.5. Because the initial denial is legally "defective," it cannot be upheld.

VI. NEXT STEPS
This case is REMANDED to the plan. HealthFirst is directed to:
1. Rescind the defective denial dated October 15, 2024.
2. Re-evaluate the request in light of CIC § 10169.5 and the objective neurological deficits documented in the clinical record.
3. Issue a compliant written determination, including specific clinical criteria and IMR rights, within 30 days of this notice.

If the plan issues a new denial, the member maintains the right to request an Independent Medical Review (IMR) through the California Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC).
