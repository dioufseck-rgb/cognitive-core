CLINICAL SUMMARY
You are a 61-year-old male with a diagnosis of mild cervical myelopathy at the C5-C6 level. Your clinical examination on October 5, 2024, showed a modified Japanese Orthopaedic Association (mJOA) score of 15/18, indicating mild impairment. Your neurological exam noted bilateral Hoffman signs and mild hyperreflexia, but your tandem gait remains intact, hand grip strength is 4+/5, and your activities of daily living (ADLs) are documented as fully intact. Your symptoms have remained stable over a six-month duration.

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) dated October 1, 2024, demonstrates moderate central cord compression at C5-C6 with a canal diameter of 8mm. Critically, the imaging shows no T2 signal change and no evidence of myelomalacia (spinal cord scarring).

PLAN CRITERIA APPLICATION
Per HealthFirst Clinical Coverage Criteria CC-SPINE-2024:
1. Section 2.2(b) (Pharmacotherapy): Requires a trial of at least two different agents (e.g., NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, or neuropathic agents) for a minimum of 4 weeks each.
2. Section 2.2A (Myelopathy Pathway): While this pathway waives interventional treatments and reduces physical therapy requirements, it explicitly states that the "Pharmacotherapy requirement (2.2b) remains in full."

Your records indicate a trial of only one agent (Baclofen) for 6 weeks. Because you have not trialed a second required agent, the criteria for surgical authorization are not met.

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL STANDARD ALIGNMENT
We have evaluated this case under California Insurance Code (CIC) § 10169.5. This statute prohibits conditioning surgical approval on physical therapy (PT) when there is objective evidence of structural neurological compromise. As your diagnosis involves spinal cord compression (myelopathy), the plan's PT requirement is waived and was not a factor in this appeal decision.

However, the pharmacotherapy requirement remains enforceable. We reviewed the treating neurologist's declaration of "surgical urgency" and "severe" compression. We find these subjective characterizations are not supported by the objective clinical record. According to the AANS/CNS 2023 Joint Guidelines, your presentation (mJOA 15, no myelomalacia, stable 6-month course, intact ADLs) is classified as Tier 2 (Elective Surgical Candidate). Tier 1 (Urgent) classification requires acute progressive deficits or myelomalacia, neither of which is present in your case. Because the condition is clinically stable and elective, the provider's urgency framing does not supersede the requirement to complete conservative medication trials.

RESOLUTION OF CONFLICT
In resolving the conflict between the provider's urgency claim and the objective record, the hierarchy of State Law > Clinical Standard > Plan Criteria was applied. While State Law (CIC § 10169.5) waives the PT requirement, the Clinical Standard (AANS/CNS Tier 2) confirms the case is elective rather than urgent. Consequently, the Plan Criteria (Section 2.2b) requiring a second medication trial remains the governing requirement and has not been satisfied.

IMR RIGHTS AND NEXT STEPS
You have the right to an Independent Medical Review (IMR) through the California Department of Managed Health Care (DMHC). An IMR is a process where expert independent doctors who do not work for HealthFirst review your case. You may request an IMR if you disagree with this decision. Application forms are available at www.dmhc.ca.gov.

To move forward with this request through HealthFirst, your provider should document a trial of a second pharmacotherapy agent as required by Section 2.2(b) or provide objective evidence of new neurological progression.
