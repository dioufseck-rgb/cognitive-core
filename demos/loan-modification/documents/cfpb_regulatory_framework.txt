PART I — LOSS MITIGATION APPLICATION OBLIGATIONS
12 CFR § 1024.41 | Effective: January 10, 2014; amended 2021

§ 1024.41(b) — RECEIPT OF LOSS MITIGATION APPLICATION
(1) ACKNOWLEDGMENT REQUIREMENT: Upon receipt of a loss mitigation application,
    a servicer shall, within 5 business days, provide the borrower a written
    notice acknowledging receipt of the application.

(2) COMPLETE APPLICATION NOTICE: The notice must state:
    (a) Whether the application is complete or incomplete
    (b) If incomplete: the specific documents and information required to
        complete the application
    (c) The date by which the borrower must submit the missing documents
    (d) That the servicer may deny the application if the borrower fails
        to submit the missing documents

(3) INCOMPLETE APPLICATION — SERVICER OBLIGATION: If a loss mitigation
    application is incomplete, a servicer shall exercise reasonable diligence
    in obtaining documents and information to complete the application.
    The servicer SHALL NOT deny a loss mitigation application solely because
    it is incomplete without first providing the borrower a reasonable
    opportunity to complete the application.

    CRITICAL: A servicer that denies an application without issuing a
    complete application notice and allowing time to cure is in violation
    of § 1024.41(b)(2). Such a denial is procedurally defective.

§ 1024.41(c) — EVALUATION OF LOSS MITIGATION APPLICATION
(1) COMPLETE APPLICATION REQUIREMENT: If a servicer receives a complete
    loss mitigation application more than 37 days before a foreclosure sale,
    the servicer shall:
    (a) Evaluate the borrower for all loss mitigation options available to
        the borrower; and
    (b) Provide the borrower with a notice in writing stating the
        servicer's determination of which loss mitigation options, if any,
        it will offer to the borrower on behalf of the owner or assignee.

(2) ALL OPTIONS MUST BE EVALUATED: The servicer must evaluate the borrower
    for ALL available loss mitigation options, not merely the option the
    borrower requested. Failure to evaluate all available options is a
    violation of § 1024.41(c)(1)(i).

    Available options typically include (as applicable per investor guidelines):
    - Repayment plan
    - Forbearance agreement
    - Loan modification (rate reduction, term extension, principal forbearance)
    - Short sale
    - Deed-in-lieu of foreclosure

§ 1024.41(d) — DENIAL OF LOAN MODIFICATION
(1) SPECIFIC REASONS REQUIRED: If a servicer receives a complete loss
    mitigation application and the servicer determines that the borrower
    is not eligible for any available loan modification option, the servicer
    shall provide the borrower a notice that states:
    (a) The specific reason or reasons for the servicer's determination; and
    (b) If applicable, that the borrower was not evaluated for a specific
        loan modification option because the borrower did not meet the
        minimum eligibility criteria for that option, with the specific
        criteria identified.

(2) A denial notice that states only that the borrower "does not qualify"
    without specifying the criteria applied and the specific reason for
    non-qualification is PROCEDURALLY DEFECTIVE under § 1024.41(d).

§ 1024.41(h) — APPEAL RIGHTS
(1) RIGHT TO APPEAL: If a servicer denies a borrower for any loan
    modification option available to the borrower, the servicer shall
    permit a borrower to appeal the denial of any loan modification option.

(2) APPEAL TIMEFRAME: The borrower has 14 days from the date of the
    denial notice to submit an appeal.

(3) DIFFERENT REVIEWER: The appeal must be reviewed by a different
    individual than the one who conducted the initial review.

PART II — DUAL TRACKING PROHIBITION
12 CFR § 1024.41(f) | Effective: January 10, 2014

§ 1024.41(f)(2) — PROHIBITION ON FORECLOSURE REFERRAL DURING REVIEW
(1) If a borrower submits a complete loss mitigation application after a
    servicer has made the first notice or filing required by applicable law
    for any judicial or non-judicial foreclosure process, but more than 37
    days before a foreclosure sale, the servicer shall NOT move for
    foreclosure judgment or order of sale, or conduct a foreclosure sale,
    unless:
    (a) The servicer has sent the borrower a notice pursuant to § 1024.41(c)(1)(ii)
        stating that the borrower is not eligible for any loss mitigation option;
    (b) The borrower has rejected all loss mitigation offers; or
    (c) The borrower has failed to perform under a loss mitigation agreement.

CRITICAL INTERPRETATION: Dual-tracking occurs when a servicer simultaneously
pursues foreclosure and loss mitigation review. A denial issued while
foreclosure proceedings are active — without proper loss mitigation review
completion — may constitute a dual-tracking violation.

PART III — GOOD FAITH AND REASONABLE DILIGENCE
12 CFR § 1024.41(b)(1); CFPB Official Interpretations

REASONABLE DILIGENCE STANDARD: A servicer must exercise reasonable diligence
in obtaining all documents and information necessary to complete a loss
mitigation application. This includes:
(a) Promptly requesting missing documents upon application receipt
(b) Not denying based on missing documents the servicer failed to request
(c) Not denying based on information the servicer could verify independently
    (e.g., property value, public records)

GOOD FAITH EVALUATION: A servicer must evaluate all loss mitigation options
in good faith. This means:
(a) Applying the correct eligibility criteria for each option
(b) Correctly calculating income, expenses, and payment ratios
(c) Not applying criteria not specified in the applicable guidelines
(d) Not imposing additional requirements beyond those in the governing
    investor/GSE guidelines

PART IV — SUCCESSOR IN INTEREST AND CO-BORROWER PROVISIONS
12 CFR § 1024.38; CFPB Final Rule 2018

§ 1024.38(b)(1)(vi) — SUCCESSORS IN INTEREST
A servicer shall maintain policies and procedures reasonably designed to
ensure that upon receiving notice of the death of a borrower or a transfer
of the property securing the mortgage loan, the servicer can facilitate
the ability of potential successors in interest to:
(a) Communicate with the servicer about loss mitigation options; and
(b) Be evaluated for loss mitigation if they assume the loan or otherwise
    establish their interest in the property.

NOTE: A servicer may not deny loss mitigation solely on the basis that the
applicant is not the original borrower, if that applicant is a successor
in interest who has assumed or seeks to assume the loan obligation.

PART V — DOCUMENTATION AND RECORD-KEEPING
12 CFR § 1024.38(b)(2)

EVALUATION DOCUMENTATION: Servicers must maintain documentation of:
(a) The complete application as submitted by the borrower
(b) All documents and information considered in the evaluation
(c) The specific criteria applied for each loss mitigation option
(d) The calculations performed (income, payment, NPV test where applicable)
(e) The determination and specific reasons for denial of each option

AUDIT OBLIGATION: Servicers must be able to demonstrate, in any regulatory
examination or legal proceeding, that they:
(a) Evaluated the borrower for all available options
(b) Applied the correct criteria and calculations
(c) Issued compliant denial notices with specific reasons

PART VI — ERROR RESOLUTION
12 CFR § 1024.35

§ 1024.35(b)(7) — ERRORS SUBJECT TO RESOLUTION
A servicer must respond to a notice of error asserting that the servicer:
"Failed to properly evaluate the borrower for loss mitigation options prior
to initiating or resuming the foreclosure process."

ERROR RESOLUTION TIMELINE:
- Acknowledge error notice: within 5 business days
- Correct error or respond: within 30 business days (or 45 in complex cases)

A servicer that fails to properly evaluate all loss mitigation options,
applies incorrect criteria, or issues a procedurally defective denial
is subject to error resolution obligations and potential CFPB enforcement.
