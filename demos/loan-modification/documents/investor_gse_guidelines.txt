PART I — FANNIE MAE FLEX MODIFICATION (Conventional/Agency)

§ F1.1 PROGRAM OVERVIEW
The Fannie Mae Flex Modification replaces HAMP for Fannie Mae-owned or
guaranteed loans. It is the standard modification program for conventional
loans in the Meridian servicing portfolio where Fannie Mae is the investor.

§ F1.2 ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
(a) Loan must be a conventional first-lien mortgage owned or guaranteed
    by Fannie Mae
(b) Property must be the borrower's primary residence (owner-occupied)
(c) Loan must be at least 12 months old from origination date
(d) Borrower must have a documented financial hardship
(e) Current or no more than 12 months delinquent
(f) Borrower must not have received a Fannie Mae Flex Modification in
    the prior 24 months

§ F1.3 MODIFICATION WATERFALL (Fannie Mae Flex)
Waterfall steps applied in sequence until target ratio achieved:
1. Capitalize arrears (add delinquent amounts to UPB)
2. Rate reduction: reduce to Fannie Mae published cap rate
   (Current cap: lesser of 3.50% or existing note rate)
3. Term extension: extend to 480 months from modification effective date
4. Principal forbearance: defer a portion of UPB interest-free
   - Available only if Steps 1-3 do not achieve target
   - Forborne amount due as balloon at maturity or payoff
   - Maximum forbearance: 30% of UPB

§ F1.4 TARGET PAYMENT RATIO — FANNIE MAE FLEX
Target: Modified monthly payment = 31% (±2%) of verified gross monthly income
- Acceptable range: 29% to 33% of GMI
- If target not achievable by any waterfall step: modification NOT approved

§ F1.5 PRINCIPAL REDUCTION — FANNIE MAE/FREDDIE MAC
PROHIBITION: Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac DO NOT permit principal reduction
on conventional loans they own or guarantee.

Authority: FHFA Guidance 2012-03 (original); FHFA Statement 2018
(reaffirmed prohibition).

"Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac will not implement principal reduction
modifications. The Enterprises are not authorized to forgive principal
on loans they own or guarantee."
— FHFA Director Statement, April 2012, reaffirmed March 2018

SCOPE OF PROHIBITION:
- The prohibition applies to ALL conventional Fannie/Freddie loans
- No exception exists for:
  * Death of co-borrower
  * Medical hardship
  * Long-term or permanent income reduction
  * Any other compelling hardship circumstance
  * Cases where modification is otherwise infeasible without principal reduction
- A servicer CANNOT offer principal reduction on a Fannie/Freddie loan
  regardless of any internal policy or discretion

CONSEQUENCE FOR EVALUATION: If a borrower cannot achieve 31% DTI through
Steps 1-3 of the Flex Modification waterfall (rate reduction, term extension,
principal forbearance), the modification is NOT feasible. The servicer must
document that all waterfall steps were applied, state the resulting DTI
at each step, and deny with specific DTI values.

§ F1.6 TRIAL PERIOD PLAN
Before a permanent modification is executed, the borrower must complete
a Trial Period Plan (TPP):
- Duration: 3 months
- Payments: equal to the proposed modified payment
- Borrower must make all 3 trial payments on time
- Servicer evaluates income documentation during trial period
- If all 3 payments made: permanent modification is offered
- If trial payments missed: modification is denied

PART III — INCOME VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS (GSE)

§ G3.1 FANNIE MAE INCOME DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
For self-employed borrowers:
- 2 years signed personal federal tax returns (IRS 4506-C required)
- 2 years signed business tax returns if applicable
- Most recent year-to-date profit and loss statement
- 2 months business bank statements

CRITICAL — SELF-EMPLOYMENT INCOME CALCULATION:
Fannie Mae income = (Schedule C net profit + depreciation + depletion +
amortization) / 24 months. Gross revenue is NOT income.
Only documented, verifiable net income counts.

For wage earners:
- Last 2 pay stubs
- Most recent W-2
- IRS 4506-C signed

For retirement/Social Security:
- Award letter (Social Security) or pension statement
- 2 months bank statements showing receipt

§ G3.2 INCOME GAPS AND REDUCTIONS
If income has decreased since tax return filing, current income governs.
If income has increased since tax return filing, the lower of current
income and 24-month average governs, unless increase is documented as
permanent (new employment with offer letter).

PART IV — HARDSHIP ASSESSMENT — GSE STANDARDS

§ G4.1 WHAT DOES NOT CONSTITUTE A HARDSHIP EXCEPTION
The following do NOT create exceptions to eligibility criteria or
waterfall limitations:
- Severity of hardship (a more severe hardship does not unlock options
  unavailable to less severe hardships)
- Duration of homeownership or payment history prior to hardship
- Emotional or personal circumstances, however compelling
- Letters from housing counselors, attorneys, or elected officials
  asserting that an exception is warranted

§ G4.2 WHAT CONSTITUTES DOCUMENTED HARDSHIP
Hardship is relevant to eligibility (§ F1.2(d)) but does NOT:
- Override the DTI target requirement
- Make a modification feasible when the waterfall cannot achieve 31% DTI
- Authorize principal reduction on Fannie/Freddie loans
- Override investor-specific restrictions on any option

Hardship is a threshold eligibility criterion, not a merits override.
Documenting a compelling hardship satisfies § F1.2(d) but does not
independently support approval if other criteria are not met.

PART V — HOUSING COUNSELOR INTERACTION

§ G5.1 ROLE OF HUD-APPROVED HOUSING COUNSELORS
HUD-approved housing counselors may assist borrowers in preparing and
submitting loss mitigation applications. Their role is:
- Helping borrowers gather documentation
- Explaining options and eligibility criteria
- Advocating for accurate and complete review

§ G5.2 LIMITS OF HOUSING COUNSELOR AUTHORITY
Housing counselors do NOT have authority to:
- Determine servicer eligibility outcomes
- Override investor guidelines or GSE restrictions
- Create exceptions to principal reduction prohibitions
- Require a servicer to approve an option the borrower does not qualify for

A housing counselor letter asserting that "the borrower deserves a principal
reduction" or that "an exception should be made" does not constitute
authority that the servicer must follow or that changes the applicable
investor guidelines.

§ G5.3 ESCALATION TO INVESTOR
If a housing counselor or borrower asserts that an exception to investor
restrictions is warranted, the servicer may escalate to the investor
(Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac) for guidance. However:
- The investor's published guidelines govern
- An escalation does not stay the evaluation timeline
- Escalation outcome does not alter published principal reduction prohibition

PART VI — NON-RETENTION OPTIONS

§ G6.1 EVALUATION SEQUENCE
Non-retention options (short sale, deed-in-lieu) are evaluated only after
all retention options have been evaluated and found infeasible. The servicer
must document:
(a) Which retention options were evaluated
(b) Why each retention option is not feasible
(c) Borrower's consent to explore non-retention

§ G6.2 SHORT SALE — FANNIE MAE
- Servicer must obtain Fannie Mae approval for any short sale
- Approved list price must reflect current BPO or appraisal value
- Deficiency waiver: standard for primary residence short sales
- Timeline: typically 120-day listing period

§ G6.3 DEED-IN-LIEU — FANNIE MAE
- Property must be free of subordinate liens
- Borrower must have marketed property for 90 days (unless waived)
- Servicer must obtain Fannie Mae approval
- Relocation assistance: $3,000 standard per current Fannie Mae guidelines
