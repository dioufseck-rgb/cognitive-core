PART I — HARDSHIP ELIGIBILITY

§ 1.1 QUALIFYING FINANCIAL HARDSHIP
A borrower must document a qualifying financial hardship to be eligible
for any loss mitigation option. Qualifying hardships include:

(a) Income reduction — documented loss of employment, reduction in hours,
    furlough, or reduction in self-employment income
(b) Income elimination — death or incapacitation of a co-borrower whose
    income was material to the original qualification
(c) Medical hardship — documented medical expenses or income loss due to
    illness or disability exceeding 10% of gross monthly income
(d) Divorce or legal separation — documented change in household income
    following legal separation
(e) Disaster — federally declared disaster affecting the property or
    the borrower's primary employer
(f) Business failure — documented closure of borrower's primary business

§ 1.2 HARDSHIP DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
The borrower must provide documentation supporting the stated hardship:

Income reduction/elimination:
- Last 2 pay stubs (current and pre-hardship where available)
- Last 2 years federal tax returns (IRS Form 4506-C required)
- Most recent bank statements (2 months)
- Employer letter or termination notice if applicable

Death of co-borrower:
- Death certificate
- Documentation of co-borrower's income contribution
- Most recent tax returns showing combined household income
- Current income documentation for surviving borrower

Medical hardship:
- Physician letter documenting diagnosis and work limitations
- Documentation of unreimbursed medical expenses
- Disability determination if applicable

§ 1.3 HARDSHIP LETTER REQUIREMENT
All applications must include a signed borrower hardship letter describing:
(a) The nature of the hardship
(b) When the hardship began
(c) Whether the hardship is temporary or permanent
(d) The borrower's ability to sustain any proposed modified payment

PART II — INCOME CALCULATION

§ 2.1 GROSS MONTHLY INCOME DEFINITION
Gross monthly income (GMI) includes all verifiable income sources:
- Base salary or wages (annualized, divided by 12)
- Overtime and bonus income (2-year average, if consistent)
- Self-employment income (net per Schedule C, 2-year average)
- Rental income (75% of gross rental income, per Schedule E)
- Social Security, pension, disability income
- Alimony and child support (if court-ordered and documented)

§ 2.2 EXCLUDED INCOME
The following may NOT be included in GMI:
- Unemployment insurance (temporary only; may be considered for short-term options)
- Cash income without documented source
- Income from household members not on the loan

§ 2.3 INCOME VERIFICATION
GMI must be verified through at least two independent sources. Self-reported
income without documentation does not satisfy the verification requirement.

PART III — LOSS MITIGATION OPTIONS AND ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

§ 3.1 REPAYMENT PLAN
Purpose: Cure arrears while maintaining current payment obligations.
Eligible when:
- Borrower has documented ability to resume full contractual payment
- Arrears do not exceed 12 months of contractual payments
- Hardship was temporary and has resolved or is resolving
- Proposed repayment period: 3 to 12 months

Ineligible when:
- Current payment-to-income ratio (PTI) exceeds 45% of GMI after
  adding repayment installment
- Arrears exceed 12 months of contractual payments
- Hardship is permanent and income has not recovered

§ 3.2 FORBEARANCE AGREEMENT
Purpose: Temporary suspension or reduction of payments for 3–12 months.
Eligible when:
- Hardship is temporary with documented path to recovery
- Borrower demonstrates ability to resume full payment after forbearance
  period (verified income sufficient to sustain current contractual payment
  at forbearance end)
- Property is owner-occupied primary residence

Resume-ability test: GMI at end of forbearance period (projected or current)
must support a PTI ≤ 31% for the original contractual payment.

NOTE: Forbearance does not reduce the outstanding loan balance or payment.
Forborne amounts are deferred and become due at end of forbearance period
or through a subsequent workout arrangement. If the borrower cannot sustain
the original payment after forbearance, forbearance alone is not a complete
solution — the borrower must also qualify for a subsequent modification or
other resolution.

CRITICAL EVALUATION OBLIGATION: If a forbearance is approved, the servicer
must simultaneously evaluate whether the borrower will need a subsequent
modification or other option at forbearance end. A forbearance that defers
an unaffordable payment without addressing the underlying payment problem
is not a good faith evaluation of all available options.

§ 3.3 STANDARD LOAN MODIFICATION
Purpose: Permanent change to loan terms to create an affordable payment.

§ 3.3.1 ELIGIBILITY — ALL MODIFICATIONS
- Documented qualifying hardship (§ 1.1)
- Property is owner-occupied primary residence
- Loan is a first-lien mortgage
- Current or no more than 12 months delinquent at time of application
- Borrower has not received a permanent loan modification in the prior
  24 months (unless hardship is new and documented separately)

§ 3.3.2 WATERFALL — MODIFICATION STEPS
Modifications are evaluated in waterfall sequence. Each step is applied
only to the extent needed to reach the target payment ratio:

STEP 1 — RATE REDUCTION: Rate reduced to market rate floor (currently
  3.50% per GSE/investor guidelines), not below floor.

STEP 2 — TERM EXTENSION: Loan term extended up to 480 months (40 years)
  from modification date.

STEP 3 — PRINCIPAL FORBEARANCE: A portion of principal is deferred
  interest-free to the end of the loan term, reducing the interest-bearing
  UPB. Available only on conventional loans — see § 3.3.4.

STEP 4 — PRINCIPAL REDUCTION: Reduction of outstanding principal balance.
  Availability is investor-specific — see § 3.3.4.

§ 3.3.3 TARGET PAYMENT RATIO
The modification must achieve a target front-end DTI of 31% (±2%) of GMI.
- Target range: 29% to 33% of GMI
- If target cannot be achieved with Steps 1-2: proceed to Step 3 (if available)
- If target still cannot be achieved with Steps 1-3: evaluate Step 4 (if available)
- If target cannot be achieved by any available waterfall step: modification
  is NOT feasible and must be DENIED with documentation of each step attempted
  and the resulting payment ratio

CRITICAL: The target is 31% DTI. A modification that achieves 31% DTI is
feasible. A modification that achieves only 38% DTI (above the 33% ceiling)
is NOT feasible regardless of whether the payment represents an improvement
over the current payment.

§ 3.3.4 INVESTOR RESTRICTIONS ON WATERFALL STEPS
Principal forbearance (Step 3) and principal reduction (Step 4) are
subject to investor authorization:

Conventional/Agency (Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac):
- Principal forbearance: AVAILABLE (Fannie/Freddie Flex Modification)
- Principal reduction: NOT AVAILABLE — prohibited by GSE policy
  (See FHFA Guidance 2012-03, affirmed 2018)
  NOTE: Principal reduction prohibition applies regardless of hardship
  severity. No exception exists for death of co-borrower, medical hardship,
  or other compelling circumstances.

FHA loans:
- Principal forbearance: AVAILABLE (FHA-HAMP partial claim)
- Principal reduction: AVAILABLE in limited circumstances (FHA MCA)
  Requires HUD approval; not available through servicer alone

VA loans:
- Principal forbearance: AVAILABLE (VA Compromise Sale, VAPLS)
- Principal reduction: AVAILABLE under VA NADL and certain refund programs

Portfolio loans (held by servicer):
- Principal forbearance: AVAILABLE at servicer discretion
- Principal reduction: AVAILABLE at servicer discretion
  Requires VP-level approval; NPV test governs

§ 3.3.5 NPV TEST
For all loan modifications on portfolio loans, the servicer must conduct
an NPV (Net Present Value) analysis comparing:
- NPV of modification scenario (projected cash flows under modified terms)
- NPV of foreclosure scenario (projected recovery through foreclosure)

If NPV of modification > NPV of foreclosure: modification is NPV-positive
  → Servicer SHOULD approve if eligibility criteria are met
If NPV of modification < NPV of foreclosure: modification is NPV-negative
  → Servicer MAY deny on NPV grounds; must document NPV calculation

NOTE: On agency (Fannie/Freddie) loans, the GSE conducts the NPV test
through the applicable modification program. The servicer does not conduct
an independent NPV test for agency loans.

§ 3.4 SHORT SALE
Purpose: Sale of property for less than the outstanding mortgage balance,
with servicer agreement to accept proceeds as full or partial satisfaction.
Eligible when:
- Borrower cannot sustain any affordable payment (no modification feasible)
- Property cannot be retained due to hardship (relocation, financial inability)
- Property value is less than outstanding loan balance
- Borrower obtains purchase offer within listed price range

§ 3.5 DEED-IN-LIEU OF FORECLOSURE
Purpose: Borrower voluntarily transfers property title to servicer.
Eligible when:
- All other retention options (modification, repayment, forbearance) exhausted
- Property is free of junior liens that would survive transfer
- Short sale was attempted or not feasible
- Borrower vacates and surrenders property in agreed condition

PART IV — EVALUATION SEQUENCE AND DOCUMENTATION

§ 4.1 REQUIRED EVALUATION SEQUENCE
The loss mitigation analyst must evaluate options in this order:
1. Confirm complete application receipt and document completeness
2. Verify qualifying hardship and income (GMI calculation)
3. Evaluate retention options first (repayment plan, forbearance, modification)
4. If no retention option is feasible: evaluate non-retention options
   (short sale, deed-in-lieu)
5. Document each option evaluated with specific criteria and calculation

§ 4.2 EVALUATION DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
For each option evaluated, the analyst must record:
- Option name
- Eligibility criteria checked (pass/fail for each)
- For modification: waterfall steps applied, resulting DTI at each step
- Reason for approval or denial
- If denied: specific criterion not met

§ 4.3 COMPLETENESS GATE
An application is COMPLETE when all of the following are received and verified:
☐ Signed borrower hardship letter
☐ Last 2 pay stubs (or equivalent income documentation)
☐ Last 2 years signed federal tax returns (IRS 4506-C signed)
☐ 2 months bank statements
☐ Mortgage statement (most recent)
☐ HOA statement (if applicable)
☐ Hardship documentation (per § 1.2)

An application missing any of these items is INCOMPLETE. The analyst must
issue an Incomplete Application Notice specifying the missing items before
any eligibility determination is made.

PART V — DENIAL NOTICE REQUIREMENTS

§ 5.1 CONTENT OF DENIAL NOTICE
A denial notice must state for each option denied:
(a) The name of the loss mitigation option
(b) The specific reason for denial (not just "does not qualify")
(c) The specific criterion not met, with the value required and the
    value the borrower presented
    Example: "Denied — Loan Modification: Monthly income insufficient
    to achieve target DTI. Required: ≤33% DTI. Calculated: 42% DTI
    after full waterfall application."
(d) Whether principal reduction would be needed to achieve target DTI
    and whether principal reduction is available for this loan type
(e) Appeal rights under § 1024.41(h) (14 days to appeal)

§ 5.2 DEFECTIVE DENIAL NOTICE
A denial notice is DEFECTIVE if it:
- States only "does not qualify" without specifying criteria
- Fails to identify which options were evaluated
- Fails to state the specific disqualifying value for each denied option
- Fails to include appeal rights notice

A defective denial notice does not start the borrower's appeal clock
and does not constitute proper completion of the loss mitigation evaluation.
